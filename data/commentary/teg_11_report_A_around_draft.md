# The Baker Boys Take Catalonia

## Stadium of Light

The Trophy was settled, as so much of this week was, on a green at the PGA Catalunya Stadium course, with Jon Baker doing very little out of the ordinary and his brother Alex doing rather too much.

Round four, the par-4 6th. Jon had just signed for a double bogey — not the sort of swing you'd usually mark as a turning point — and yet there it was. Alex, two clear at the start of the day, was suddenly level. He would not be level for long. By the time the brothers were on the homeward stretch, the Trophy had quietly migrated across the family, and Jon Baker had never relinquished it.

That is the ending. To understand how Catalonia produced it, rewind.

## Round 1 — Stadium of Pain

TEG returned to the Stadium course for the first time since its debut visit, and the championship layout immediately reminded everyone what it was for. Everyone, that is, except one Baker.

Jon opened with 35 Stableford points and +18 gross — nothing showy, just the cleanest card of the day on a course determined to mug the field. From the third hole onward he had a share or sole possession of the Gross lead, a position he would not surrender all week. The Trophy, too, was his by close of play.

Around him, the demolition started early. Gregg Williams opened triple-triple-double across the first three holes — eight shots gone before he'd properly settled into his shoes — and then added a quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 5th for good measure. John Patterson, meanwhile, was constructing his own greatest-hits album: a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 4th, an 8 at the 10th, another quintuple 9 at the 13th, a triple at the 14th, and an 8 at the 17th. Somewhere in there — at the 14th, to be precise — he slid to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon standings, where he would set up camp for the next day and a half.

## Round 2 — Tour de Farce

On to the Tour course, billed as the Stadium's slightly more accommodating sibling. The 15th had not read the press release.

Jon Baker shot 41 points — his best round of the week — and continued to lead both competitions without obvious sweat. The par-5 15th, meanwhile, was where the rest of the field went to be humbled. Patterson made an 11 there: a sextuple bogey, six over par on a single hole, the kind of number you don't write down so much as confess to. Williams went round in a 10 of his own a few groups along. And Alex Baker, the man who would shortly emerge as Jon's only credible threat, chipped in with a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 12th.

At halfway, the Bakers were the story; the rest of the field was bookkeeping.

## Round 3 — Brotherly Shove at El Prat

TEG's first visit to El Prat's Azul course — Greg Norman's Catalan routing — was supposed to be the change of scenery. It turned into the only round Jon Baker didn't win and the only day he didn't lead the Trophy at sundown.

Alex drew level at the 1st with a bogey. The brothers then exchanged blows in the polite, fraternal manner of two men who have shared a back seat since 1985, until Jon found a genuinely cold patch on the back nine: double bogey at the par-4 11th, double at the par-3 12th, triple at the par-4 13th. Seven shots gone in three holes. Alex parred the 13th and took outright Trophy lead for the first time all week. He would end the day two points clear — the first time in the tournament that Jon's name was not at the top of the Stableford.

The Jacket flirted with chaos too. David Mullin, quietly assembling the week's other consistent Gross card, drew level with Jon at the par-4 3rd with a par. But Jon answered at the par-3 6th with a bogey that was good enough to retake outright lead, and the Jacket arithmetic settled there: Jon by three, and holding.

Mullin's own day was unravelling elsewhere. A quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 6th was the first crack; by the par-3 12th he had slid past Patterson into the Spoon basement, where Patterson had been comfortably resident since the previous afternoon. The handover was decisive: Mullin would not climb out.

So to Sunday: Alex led the Trophy by two, Jon led the Jacket by three, Mullin had the Spoon by the scruff. Three competitions, three margins, one course left.

## Round 4 — Jon's Catalan Coronation

Back to the Stadium, which proceeded to bare its teeth at the whole field with the lone exception, once again, of Jon Baker.

The Gross moved first. Mullin drew level with Jon at the par-5 3rd — pars all round, a familiar dance. And then the Trophy moved at the par-4 6th, where Jon's double bogey was enough to pull him alongside Alex. From that moment, the elder Baker did not yield outright lead in either competition.

He didn't have to play brilliantly. He had only to play. Alex did the rest himself, in three distinct bleed stretches that told the story of the round better than any leaderboard. Holes 4-6: double, double, triple, seven shots dropped. Holes 12-14: triple, triple, double, eight more. Holes 16-18: double, quadruple, double, eight to close. Twenty-three shots over par across nine holes. The card came in at 27 Stableford points — Alex's worst round to date.

Jon himself was not immaculate. A quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 13th was a reminder that the Stadium doesn't discriminate, but by then Alex's wheels were already at the side of the road and Jon's lead in both competitions was a matter of admin rather than nerve.

Mullin, in the meantime, was sealing his own quiet drama. A 7-shot bleed across holes 15-17 — triple at the par-5 15th, double at the par-3 16th, double at the par-4 17th — completed a 31-point round and confirmed the Spoon. Williams added a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 10th to his collection, which by now ran to a quintuple in each of the four rounds. Even his consistency was a kind of damage.

## The Three Competitions, Sealed

**The Trophy** went to Jon Baker on 143 points, by six from Alex. He led from R1, was overtaken only briefly at El Prat, drew level at the Stadium's 6th on Sunday, and was never headed again.

**The Green Jacket** went to Jon Baker at +70 gross, by three from Mullin. He led from the third hole of the first round and finished the tournament in front of every leaderboard check. Mullin drew level twice — R3 hole 3 and R4 hole 3, both on the Stadium's and Azul's par-fours — but never moved ahead.

**The Wooden Spoon** went to David Mullin on 125 points. Patterson had held the basement from R1's 14th, but Mullin's quadruple at El Prat's par-3 6th and his slide at the 12th took the keys off him in R3, and the 15-17 bleed at the Stadium on Sunday closed the door.

A Baker double, a Mullin Jacket-and-Spoon parlay, and a course — the Stadium — that asked the field a serious question and got back one serious answer.

## The men, in brief

- **Jon Baker** — Wire-to-wire on the Gross from R1's third hole; six points clear on Stableford come Sunday night. An El Prat wobble (32 pts; seven shots dropped across 11-13) aside, this was an exhibition of doing the ordinary things without interruption. Trophy and Jacket, both his.
- **Alex Baker** — The only man to lay a glove on his brother all week, and he laid it cleanly at El Prat's 13th to take outright Trophy lead. Then Sunday: three separate bleed stretches at the Stadium, 27 points, his worst round to date, and the lot handed back. Trophy runner-up, with footnotes.
- **David Mullin** — Drew level with Jon on Gross twice without ever passing him; finished Jacket runner-up at +73. Simultaneously bottom of the Trophy on 125, undone by the quadruple at El Prat's par-3 6th and the 15-17 surrender on Sunday. A week of two scoreboards pointing in opposite directions.
- **John Patterson** — The week's most committed demolition contractor: quintuple-bogey 9s at R1's 4th and 13th, the sextuple 11 at R2's 15th, and another quintuple at R3's 16th. Held the Spoon basement for the better part of two and a half rounds, and somehow walked away from the week not last.
- **Gregg Williams** — Triple-triple-double opening on Thursday, then a quintuple in every round that followed, capped by the 10 at the Tour course's 15th. Spared the Spoon only because Mullin and Patterson were even more generous.
