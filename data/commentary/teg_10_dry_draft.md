# Third Time, First Trophy — Draft

## OVERVIEW

Alex Baker won the Trophy on 184 Stableford points, 11 clear of runner-up Gregg Williams. David Mullin won the Green Jacket at +51 gross, 12 ahead of Jon Baker. John Patterson took the Wooden Spoon on 112 points.

## Round 1 — Mullin Lights Up Boavista

Saturday at Boavista, the 11th TEG round at the venue. David Mullin shot 75 gross (+4), a new Boavista course record by seven shots — the prior mark was 82 across 50 prior visits — and the best gross round in TEG history to date (b03, cr01). The 49 Stableford points he posted alongside it stood, at the time, as the second-best round in TEG history (b05). It opened a 14-shot Jacket lead and an 8-point Trophy lead before the day was out.

Behind him, Stuart Neumann ran up a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 18th (b17).

The bottom of the Spoon table changed hands on R1 H4: John Patterson dropped to last place and stayed there for every remaining hole of the tournament. Patterson's round also included a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 8th, one of the hardest holes (SI 2) (b26).

## Round 2 — Three Personal Bests and a Ten at the First

Sunday at Palmares (Praia / Alvor), a new course for TEG. Three personal-best Stableford rounds were posted: Alex Baker 45 (b08), Gregg Williams 48 (b13), and the gross equivalents for Baker at +23 (b09) and Williams at +14 (b14). Jon Baker shot +10 gross — the third-best gross round in TEG history to date (b07).

John Patterson opened with a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 1st (b15).

Stuart Neumann had a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 18th — a second consecutive 10 at an 18th hole (b18) — and earlier ran up a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 13th (b32). Across holes 13–15 he dropped 13 shots: 10, 9, 7 against pars of 5, 4, 4 (b77).

Mullin's Trophy lead came in to 5 points.

## Round 3 — Baker Steps Through

Monday at Palmares (Alvor / Lagos), again new ground. Alex Baker posted a personal-best 47 points (b10) and a personal-best +22 gross (b11). The round included a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 13th (b24).

The Trophy lead changed hands. On H6, Alex Baker took outright lead of the Trophy for the first time in the tournament (b31). On H13, Gregg Williams took outright lead with a bogey 6 at the par-5 (b36). Baker re-took the lead on H15 and finished the round 11 points clear.

John Patterson's round broke across holes 11–15: triple bogey 7, quintuple bogey 8, triple bogey 8, quadruple bogey 7, quadruple bogey 9 — 19 shots dropped in five holes (b38). Inside that, the quintuple at the par-3 12th (SI 18, the easiest hole) and the quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 8th earlier in the round (b27) were the biggest single holes.

## Round 4 — Fifty-One and a Trophy

Tuesday, back at Boavista. Alex Baker shot 51 Stableford points — the best round in TEG history to date (b04) — and a personal-best +17 gross (b12). Across holes 5–10 he gained 21 points: bogey 5 at the par-4 5th, par 3 at the par-3 6th, par 4 at the par-4 7th, bogey 6 at the par-5 8th (SI 2), par 4 at the par-4 9th (SI 18, the easiest hole), par 4 at the par-4 10th — three bogeys and three pars, +2 over the stretch (b06).

John Patterson signed off with 21 Stableford points, his worst round to date (b34), including a sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 8th (SI 2) (b16).

Personal-best gross totals were set by Alex Baker at +90 (b21), Gregg Williams at +76 (b22) and Jon Baker at +63 (b23).

## HOW THE COMPETITIONS WERE DECIDED

**Trophy (Stableford).** David Mullin led after R1 by 8 and after R2 by 5. Alex Baker took outright lead on R3 H6 (b31) — the decisive takeover. Williams drew level and took outright lead on R3 H13 (b36); Baker re-took it on H15 and closed R3 11 points clear. In R4 Baker shot 51, matching the margin exactly: final winning margin 11 points over Williams (b01).

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Mullin led wire-to-wire. The R1 75 at Boavista (b03, cr01) opened a 14-shot lead by the end of day one. The leads by round were 14, 13, 10, 12. Final margin 12 shots over Jon Baker, finishing at +51 (b02).

**Wooden Spoon.** Patterson dropped to last on R1 H4 and was bottom at the end of every subsequent round (positions 6, 6, 6, 6). Round scores of 29, 35, 27, 21. Damage came from sextuple/quintuple bogeys at R1 H8 (b26), R2 H1 (b15), R3 H8 (b27) and R4 H8 (b16), the 19-shot R3 H11–15 stretch (b38), and the closing 21-point round (b34). Final total 112 (b30).
