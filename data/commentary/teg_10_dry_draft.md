# TEG 10 — Dry Storyline Draft

## 1. Overview

Alex Baker won the Trophy on 184 Stableford points, 11 clear of runner-up Gregg Williams — the highest Trophy total in TEG history. David Mullin won the Green Jacket at +51 gross, 12 shots clear of runner-up Jon Baker. John Patterson collected the Wooden Spoon on 112 Stableford points.

## 2. Round by Round

### Round 1 — Mullin Goes Bang: 49 Points and an Eight-Shot Lead at Boavista

Boavista, 30/09/2017, the 11th TEG round at the venue.

David Mullin posted 49 points (+4 gross), the second-best round in TEG history to that point, to open an eight-point Trophy lead and a 14-shot Gross lead. John Patterson dropped to the bottom of the Stableford table at H4 and stayed there, with a quintuple bogey 10 at the par-5 8th. Stuart Neumann closed with a sextuple bogey 10 at the par-4 18th.

### Round 2 — Palmares Debuts and the PBs Pile Up

Palmares - Praia / Alvor, 01/10/2017, a new course for TEG.

Three personal-best rounds in the field on TEG's first visit: Gregg Williams 48, Alex Baker 45, with Mullin still leading the Trophy by five. John Patterson opened with a sextuple bogey 10 at the par-4 1st. Stuart Neumann ran up a quintuple bogey 10 at the par-5 13th, then bled 13 shots across holes 13-15 (10 at the par-5 13th, 9 at the par-4 14th, 7 at the par-4 15th), and finished with a second successive sextuple bogey 10 at the par-4 18th.

### Round 3 — Baker Takes the Wheel at Palmares

Palmares - Alvor / Lagos, 02/10/2017, a new course for TEG.

Alex Baker posted a third successive personal best, 47 points, and took outright Trophy lead at H6 with a par at the par-4 6th. He ran up a quintuple bogey 10 at the par-5 13th himself. Gregg Williams then took outright Trophy lead at H13 with a bogey at the par-5 13th. Baker drew level again from H15 to share the top by round's end. John Patterson recorded another quintuple bogey 10 at the par-5 8th and bled 19 shots across holes 11-15: triple bogey 7 at the par-4 11th, quintuple bogey 8 at the par-3 12th, triple bogey 8 at the par-5 13th, quadruple bogey 7 at the par-3 14th, quadruple bogey 9 at the par-5 15th.

### Round 4 — Fifty-One. Read That Again.

Boavista, 03/10/2017, the 12th TEG round at the venue.

Gregg Williams drew level for the Trophy lead at H5 with a birdie at the par-4 5th. Alex Baker then piled up 21 points across holes 5-10 — bogey 5 at the par-4 5th, par 3 at the par-3 6th, par 4 at the par-4 7th, bogey 6 at the par-5 8th, par 4 at the par-4 9th, par 4 at the par-4 10th — en route to 51 points, the best round in TEG history. That settled the Trophy by 11. David Mullin's closing 13-over confirmed the Green Jacket at +51. John Patterson posted his worst-ever round, 21 points, including a sextuple bogey 11 at the par-5 8th, sealing the Spoon on 112.

## 3. How the Competitions Were Decided

**The Trophy (Stableford) — Alex Baker, 184, by 11.** Mullin led by eight after R1 (49 pts) and by five after R2. Baker took outright lead at R3 H6 (the decisive takeover) with his 47-point round and was tied at the top entering R4. Williams drew level at R4 H5. Baker's R4 51 — including 21 points in six holes (H5-10) — put the Trophy out of reach.

**The Green Jacket (Gross) — David Mullin, +51, by 12.** Mullin led from R1 H6 onwards and was never headed (only Jon Baker briefly took the lead at R1 H9 before Mullin reclaimed it). Round scores: +4, +11, +23 (including a quadruple bogey 8 at the R3 18th), +13. Margin over Jon Baker: 12.

**The Wooden Spoon — John Patterson, 112.** Bottom of the Stableford table from R1 H4 onwards. Round scores 29, 35, 27, 21.

## 4. Players

- **Alex Baker** — 2nd, 2nd, 1st, 1st. Round scores 41, 45, 47, 51. Three successive personal bests in R2-R4; R4's 51 is the best round in TEG history; total of 184 is the highest Trophy total in TEG history.
- **David Mullin** — Trophy leader after R1 and R2; Green Jacket winner at +51, by 12. R1 49 points was second-best round in TEG history at the time.
- **Gregg Williams** — Trophy runner-up. PB 48 at Palmares Praia/Alvor in R2; took outright Trophy lead at R3 H13; drew level at R4 H5.
- **Jon Baker** — Briefly led both competitions at R1 H9; Green Jacket runner-up.
- **John Patterson** — Wooden Spoon, 112 points. Bottom from R1 H4. Blow-ups included quintuple 10 at R1 H8, sextuple 10 at R2 H1, quintuple 10 at R3 H8, 19 shots dropped across R3 H11-15, sextuple 11 at R4 H8; personal-worst 21 in R4.
- **Stuart Neumann** — Sextuple bogey 10s at the 18th in R1 (Boavista) and R2 (Palmares Praia/Alvor); quintuple 10 at R2's par-5 13th; 13 shots dropped across R2 H13-15.
