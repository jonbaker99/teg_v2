# OVERVIEW

Alex Baker won the Trophy on 184 Stableford points, 11 clear of Gregg Williams, with David Mullin third. Mullin won the Green Jacket at gross +51, 12 clear of Jon Baker at +63. John Patterson took the Wooden Spoon on 112 points.

# ROUND 1 — Mullin's +4 Rewrites the Boavista Tablets

At Boavista (the 11th TEG round at the venue), David Mullin opened with a gross +4 — the best Gross round in TEG history to date — worth 49 Stableford points, the second-best round in TEG history at the time. That gave him an eight-shot Trophy lead and a 14-shot Jacket lead by the close of play. Alex Baker shadowed him on 41 points.

John Patterson dropped to last after four holes and stayed there for the rest of the tournament. His round included a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 8th. Stuart Neumann finished with a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 18th.

# ROUND 2 — Palmares Debuts, Personal Bests Follow

TEG's first visit to Palmares (Praia / Alvor) produced three personal bests. Alex Baker posted a PB 45 points / gross +23. Gregg Williams posted a PB 48 points / gross +14. Jon Baker shot gross +10 — the third-best Gross round in TEG history to date.

Mullin's lead in the Trophy shrank to five; his Jacket lead narrowed to 13.

John Patterson opened with a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 1st. Stuart Neumann bled 13 shots across three holes from the 13th — a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 13th, a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 14th and a triple-bogey 7 at the par-4 15th — and added a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 18th.

# ROUND 3 — The Day Mullin's Cushion Vanished

At Palmares (Alvor / Lagos), Alex Baker posted a PB 47 points / gross +22. He took the outright Trophy lead at the par-4 6th. Mullin drew level again at the 7th. Alex then made a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 13th, and Gregg Williams took the outright lead at the same hole with a bogey 6. Alex retook the lead (not yet outright) at the 15th and slept on an 11-point overnight cushion.

Mullin's eight-shot opening Trophy lead was gone; his Jacket lead stood at 10.

John Patterson had a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 8th.

# ROUND 4 — 51 — A Number for the Record Book

Back at Boavista, Alex Baker signed for 51 Stableford points — the best round in TEG history to date — at a PB gross +17. The scoring core was a six-hole run from the 5th worth 21 points: bogey 5 at the par-4 5th (3 pts), par 3 at the par-3 6th (4 pts), par 4 at the par-4 7th (4 pts), bogey 6 at the par-5 8th (3 pts), par 4 at the par-4 9th (3 pts), par 4 at the par-4 10th (4 pts).

Mullin shot gross +13 to seal the Green Jacket by 12. Alex's final Trophy margin was 11.

John Patterson posted his worst-ever round of 21 points, including a sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 8th.

# HOW THE COMPETITIONS WERE DECIDED

**Trophy.** Mullin led after R1 by 8 and after R2 by 5, with Alex Baker second on 41 then 45. The decisive takeover came in R3 at Palmares (Alvor / Lagos): Alex took the outright lead at the par-4 6th. After a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 13th he briefly ceded the outright lead to Gregg Williams, then retook it by the 15th and slept on an 11-shot lead. His record 51 at Boavista in R4 held the margin at 11. Final: Alex Baker 184, Gregg Williams second, Mullin third.

**Green Jacket.** Mullin led wire-to-wire after his opening gross +4. Round leads of 14, 13, 10 and 12. Jon Baker's gross +10 in R2 was the only round that closed any ground. Final margin: 12, Mullin +51 to Jon Baker +63.

**Wooden Spoon.** Patterson dropped to last at the 4th hole of R1 and stayed bottom at every checkpoint. Round-by-round scores: 29, 35, 27, 21. The route included a quintuple-bogey 10 at Boavista's 8th in R1, a sextuple-bogey 10 at Palmares's 1st in R2, a quintuple-bogey 10 at Palmares's 8th in R3, and a sextuple-bogey 11 at Boavista's 8th in R4. Final total: 112.
