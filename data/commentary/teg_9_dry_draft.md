# Patterson's Portuguese Coronation — Dry Storyline Draft

## 1. Overview

John Patterson won the Trophy on 168 Stableford points, six clear of Alex Baker — a new all-time TEG Stableford record. David Mullin took the Green Jacket at +67 gross, winning by ten from Jon Baker. Stuart Neumann finished last on 127 Stableford points to take the Wooden Spoon.

---

## 2. Round 1 — Seve's Last Design, and Six Players Lost in It

TEG 9 opened at Royal Óbidos, the second TEG round at Seve Ballesteros' final design, and the scoring was scattered with blow-ups. Gregg Williams started with a quintuple bogey 9 at the par-4 1st. Alex Baker followed with a quadruple bogey 9 at the par-5 2nd, which dropped him to the bottom of the Spoon table. The Jacket lead changed hands at the 3rd, when David Mullin took outright gross lead with a par.

Patterson's day included a sextuple bogey 10 at the par-4 4th, a quintuple at the 4th from Neumann (also a 9) and, later, a quadruple bogey 7 at the par-3 15th. Despite the ten he still moved into outright Stableford lead at the par-5 5th with a par for four points. The front of the field traded the Stableford lead through the opening holes — Jon Baker drawing level at the 2nd, Mullin at the 3rd, Williams at the 4th, then Jon Baker again at the 6th — routine early-round shuffling.

Alex Baker's round contained a septuple bogey 11 at the par-4 14th and the quintuple 9 at the par-4 12th. Williams added a sextuple bogey 9 at the par-3 15th and a quadruple bogey 9 at the par-5 18th. Jon Baker closed with a quadruple bogey 9 at the 18th.

Neumann's back nine was the decisive Spoon material. He dropped to the bottom at the par-5 11th after a triple bogey 8, briefly escaped, then sank again with a quintuple bogey 8 at the par-3 15th — the decisive Spoon drop. Across holes 14–18 he bled 16 shots (a triple 7, the quintuple 8, a triple 7, a double 6, a triple 8). His front nine had been 11 points stronger than his back.

Mullin's day was the quietest. He went par-par-par through 13–15 before a double bogey 6 at the 16th, and reclaimed the outright gross lead at the par-5 18th with a closing par for +15 — the Jacket's decisive takeover. Jon Baker led the Trophy by one at +1; Mullin led the Jacket by two.

---

## 3. Round 2 — Forty-Nine and Out of Sight

The field moved to Praia D'El Rey, its third TEG visit. Patterson took outright Trophy lead at the par-4 1st with an opening bogey for three points. He then bled eight shots over holes 4–6 (double, triple, triple) and Mullin drew level at the 2nd, before Patterson drew level again at the par-3 11th — but from the par-5 7th onwards Patterson's round became historic.

He stopped the bleeding with a birdie 4 at the 7th. Through holes 9–13 he piled up 17 points (bogey, par, par, birdie, par). He closed with 14 points across 15–18 (par, bogey, par, bogey at the coastal finish). The round totalled 49 Stableford points, the second-best round in TEG history — turning a one-shot deficit into a ten-shot Trophy lead.

Alex Baker posted a personal-best 44 points despite a quadruple bogey 9 at the par-5 10th and a quadruple 7 at the par-3 14th. Jon Baker also had a quadruple 7 at the 14th, ending a par-par-par run through 11–13, before a birdie 4 at the par-5 17th stopped the slide. Mullin steadied a four-hole bogey run from the 6th with a birdie 4 at the 10th, posting +14 to extend his Jacket lead from two to seven.

Williams ran up a sextuple bogey 10 at the par-4 9th. Neumann added quadruple bogey 8s at the par-4 5th and par-4 9th and was last after the round on 53 points total.

---

## 4. Round 3 — Patterson Wobbles, Lead Survives

Back to Royal Óbidos for its third TEG visit. Patterson's round was untidy: three quadruple bogeys on one card. He bled eight shots through holes 2–4 (double 7 at the par-5 2nd, triple 6 at the par-3 3rd, triple 7 at the 4th), then ran up a quadruple bogey 8 at the par-4 12th and a quadruple bogey 9 at the par-5 18th. He still posted 34 points.

Jon Baker opened with 16 points across holes 1–5 (par, birdie, par, bogey, par) but ended a par-par-par run through 10 with a quadruple bogey 9 at the par-5 11th, and bled eight shots over 10–12. His round totalled 34, his front nine ten points stronger than his back.

Mullin's only real wobble of the week came at the par-4 10th, a quintuple bogey 9. He still came in at +22 to hold the Jacket lead at seven. Alex Baker added a quintuple bogey 9 at the par-4 14th and a cold run of eight shots dropped through 10–12. Neumann bled another eight across 16–18 (quadruple 8, double 6, double 7).

Patterson led the Trophy by three; Mullin led the Jacket by seven; Neumann remained last.

---

## 5. Round 4 — 168: A Number for the Record Book

The final round at Praia D'El Rey. Mullin opened with a quadruple bogey 8 at the par-4 1st, but it barely dented his cushion. Patterson had a quintuple bogey 9 at the par-4 5th. Alex Baker, en route to a personal-best 44 points to match his R2 score, played triple-bogey-quadruple-bogey through 4–7 before stopping the bleeding with a birdie 2 at the par-3 8th.

At the par-3 14th, Alex Baker made a double bogey 5 for two points — and drew level with Patterson at the top of the Trophy table. From there Alex Baker bled 11 shots across 14–17 (double 5, quadruple 8, triple 7, double 7). Patterson's closing stretch took him to 47 points for the round and 168 for the tournament — the highest Stableford total in TEG history, six clear of Alex Baker.

Mullin posted +16 to finish at +67, extending his Jacket lead from seven to ten. Jon Baker added a quadruple bogey 9 at the par-5 17th. Williams ran up a sextuple bogey 11 at the par-5 7th.

Neumann's day mixed both poles: he bled nine shots through 2–5 (double, triple, double, double), then piled up 16 points across 11–15 (par, par, birdie, par, bogey), before bleeding another eight across 16–18 (quadruple 8, double 7, double 6). He finished on 127 points, last for the fourth round running.

---

## 6. How the Competitions Were Decided

**Trophy (Stableford).** Jon Baker led by one after R1 on 39 points. Patterson took outright lead at R2 H1 with an opening bogey for three points — the decisive takeover — and then posted the 49-point round that pushed the lead to ten. R3's 34 trimmed it to three. In R4, Alex Baker drew level at the par-3 14th, but Patterson's closing run to 47 points and 168 total carried him home by six.

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Mullin took outright gross lead at R1 H3, was briefly level when Jon Baker drew alongside at the 6th, drew level again at the 15th and reclaimed outright at the par-5 18th — the decisive takeover. He led by 2, then 7, then 7, then 10, posting +15, +14, +22 and +16 to finish at +67, ten clear of Jon Baker.

**Wooden Spoon.** Williams was last after H1; Alex Baker after H2; Neumann from R1 H11. He briefly escaped, then dropped for good at R1 H15 with the quintuple bogey 8 — the decisive drop — followed by the 16-shot bleed across 14–18. He sat last after every round and finished on 127 points.

---

## 7. Players

- **John Patterson.** Survived a R1 sextuple bogey 10 at the 4th to post 38; took outright Trophy lead at R2 H1 and produced 49 points — the second-best round in TEG history — to lead by ten; held on through a messy 34 in R3; closed with 47 for 168, an all-time TEG record.
- **David Mullin.** Took outright Jacket lead at R1 H18 and never gave it back. Rounds of +15, +14, +22 and +16 for +67, winning by ten. Only real damage: a R3 quintuple at the 10th and a R4 opening 8.
- **Alex Baker.** Two personal-best 44-point rounds (R2 and R4) and a share of the Trophy lead at R4 H14. Undone by blow-ups: the septuple bogey 11 at R1 H14, the quintuple 9 at R1 H12, the quintuple 9 at R3 H14, and an 11-shot bleed over R4 holes 14–17.
- **Jon Baker.** Led the Trophy after R1 by one. Gross runner-up at +77. Hot R3 start of 16 points through five was undermined by an R2 H14 quadruple 7 and an R4 H17 quadruple 9.
- **Gregg Williams.** The week's blow-up specialist: a quintuple 9 at R1 H1, a sextuple 9 at R1 H15, a quadruple 9 at R1 H18, a sextuple 10 at R2 H9 and a sextuple 11 at R4 H7.
- **Stuart Neumann.** Bottom from R1 H15 to the finish. Round scores of 23, 30, 37, 37 for 127. R1 back-nine bleed of 16 shots over 14–18 set the tone; an R4 burst of 16 points through 11–15 was the brightest patch.
