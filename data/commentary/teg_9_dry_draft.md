# Patterson's Portuguese Coronation

## Overview

John Patterson won the Trophy on 168 points, a new all-time TEG Stableford record, six clear of Alex Baker. David Mullin won the Green Jacket at +67, ten ahead of Jon Baker. Stuart Neumann took the Wooden Spoon on 127 points.

This was TEG's third visit to the Lisbon Coast, alternating Seve Ballesteros' final design at Royal Óbidos (Rounds 1 and 3) with Cabell Robinson's Praia D'El Rey (Rounds 2 and 4).

## Round 1 — Seve's Last Design, and Six Players Lost in It

Royal Óbidos, in its second TEG outing, produced a blow-up-strewn opening day. Gregg Williams started with a 9 at the 1st and added a sextuple-bogey 9 at the 15th and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the 18th. Alex Baker carded a quadruple-bogey 9 at the 2nd, a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 12th and a septuple-bogey 11 at the 14th. Patterson contributed a sextuple-bogey 10 at the 4th and a quadruple-bogey 7 at the 15th, yet still posted 38 points — one behind Jon Baker, who closed with a quadruple-bogey 9 at the 18th.

In the gross race, Mullin took outright lead at the 3rd, was overhauled briefly by Jon Baker around the 6th, drew level again at the 15th and was back in outright lead by the 18th. His +15 was the only sub-twenty card on the day, giving him a two-shot cushion.

Neumann's day fell apart on the back nine. After a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 4th, a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 12th and a quintuple-bogey 8 at the 15th, he bled 16 shots across holes 14–18. He had dropped to last at the 11th, escaped briefly, then sank for good at the 15th. His front-nine/back-nine split was 11 points; his round total 23.

## Round 2 — Forty-Nine and Out of Sight

Patterson seized the outright Trophy lead at the 1st at Praia D'El Rey, surrendered a share to Mullin briefly at the 2nd, and was back ahead from the 11th onwards. The opening stretch was rough — six bogeys-or-worse in a row, including triple bogeys at the 5th and 6th — before a birdie at the par-5 7th steadied him.

From the 9th he went on a run: 17 points across holes 9–13 (bogey, par, par, birdie, par on the cards), then a closing 14 points across 15–18. The round totalled 49 points, the second-best in TEG history, and turned a one-point overnight deficit into a ten-shot lead.

Alex Baker matched his personal best with 44 points, despite a quadruple-bogey 9 at the 10th and a quadruple-bogey 7 at the 14th. Jon Baker also took a quadruple 7 at the 14th before a birdie at the 17th halted his slide. Williams ran up a sextuple-bogey 10 at the 9th. Neumann added quadruple bogeys at the 5th and 9th and stayed bottom on 30.

Mullin's day was unremarkable — bogeys through 6–9, a birdie at the 10th — but a +14 gross extended his Jacket lead to seven.

## Round 3 — Patterson Wobbles, Lead Survives

Royal Óbidos bit back. Patterson's card carried three quadruple bogeys — an 8 at the 12th, a 9 at the 18th, plus another along the way — and totalled 34 points. The cushion came down to three.

Nobody in the chasing pack capitalised. Jon Baker opened with 16 points across the first five holes (par, birdie, par, bogey, par) but then ran up a quadruple-bogey 9 at the 11th, finishing on 34. Alex Baker carded a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 14th. Mullin had the round's worst single hole, a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 10th, but his +22 still left him seven clear in the Jacket race.

## Round 4 — 168: A Number for the Record Book

Mullin opened with a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 1st. It barely mattered; he cruised home in +16 to win the Jacket by ten on +67.

In the Trophy, Alex Baker matched his personal best again with 44 points and drew level with Patterson at the 14th — a double-bogey 5 at the par-3 enough to square the lead on Stableford. Patterson had earlier dropped a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 5th. From the 14th he produced his closing kick: a 47-point round in total, taking him to 168 — the highest Stableford total in TEG history, six clear of Alex Baker.

Neumann's week ended as it had largely played out: bottom from R1 H15 to the final hole. He did, however, put together a 16-point burst across 11–15 (par, par, birdie, par, bogey) and added a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 16th, signing for 37 and a tournament total of 127. Williams ran up a sextuple-bogey 11 at the 7th. Jon Baker took a quadruple-bogey 9 at the 17th, sealing second in the gross.

## How the Competitions Were Decided

**Trophy (Stableford).** Jon Baker led by one after Round 1. Patterson took outright lead at the 1st in Round 2 — the decisive takeover — and his 49-point round opened a ten-shot gap. Round 3's wobbly 34 trimmed it to three. Alex Baker drew level at the 14th in Round 4 but Patterson's 47-point finish carried him to 168, winning by six.

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Mullin led wire-to-wire from Round 1. He took outright lead at the 3rd, was briefly overhauled by Jon Baker, regained level at the 15th and held outright from the 18th — the decisive takeover. Rounds of +15, +14, +22 and +16 stretched the margin from two to seven to seven to ten. Final score +67, by ten from Jon Baker.

**Wooden Spoon.** Neumann was bottom from the 15th in Round 1 onwards, the decisive drop coming with his quintuple-bogey 8 at that hole, part of a 16-shot bleed across 14–18. He never climbed off the bottom again across the remaining three rounds, finishing on 127.

## Players

- **John Patterson** — Survived a sextuple-bogey 10 in Round 1, then posted 49, 34 and 47 to set a new all-time TEG record of 168.
- **David Mullin** — Led the Jacket race from Round 1 H3 to the finish; won by ten on +67 without ever being headed outright after Round 1.
- **Alex Baker** — Two personal-best 44-point rounds and a brief share of the Trophy lead at R4 H14; the 11 at R1 H14 was the worst of several blow-ups that cost him.
- **Jon Baker** — Early Trophy leader and eventual gross runner-up; a hot R3 start (16 points in five holes) was undone by quadruple bogeys at R2 H14 and R4 H17.
- **Gregg Williams** — Blow-up of the week: sextuples at R1 H15, R2 H9 and R4 H7, plus a 9 on the opening tee.
- **Stuart Neumann** — Bottom from R1 H15 to the final hole; the 16-point burst across R4 11–15 was a rare bright patch in a 127-point week.
