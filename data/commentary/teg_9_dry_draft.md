# TEG 9 — Lisbon Coast, 2016 — Dry Storyline Draft

## OVERVIEW

John Patterson won the Trophy on 168 Stableford points, six clear of Alex Baker on 162. David Mullin won the Green Jacket at +67 gross, ten clear of Jon Baker. Stuart Neumann took the Wooden Spoon on 127 points.

## Round 1 — Seve's Last Joke

Royal Óbidos, Seve Ballesteros's final design, hosted its 2nd TEG round. Jon Baker emerged with a one-point lead on the Trophy, despite signing for a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 18th.

The carnage was distributed across the field. John Patterson ran up a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 4th. Alex Baker posted a septuple-bogey 11 at the par-4 14th. Stuart Neumann was the headline collapse: from 23 points and far stronger on his front nine (an 11-point split), he bled 16 shots over the closing five holes — a triple-bogey 7 at the 14th, a quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 15th, a triple-bogey 7 at the 16th, a double-bogey 6 at the 17th and a triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 18th — to finish on 23 points.

On the Trophy ladder, the early holes were busy and bunched: Jon Baker, Mullin, Gregg Williams and Patterson each held a share or outright lead between holes 2 and 5 before Jon Baker moved back in front at the 6th. The Green Jacket: Mullin took the outright Gross lead at the 3rd, Jon Baker drew level at the 6th, Mullin moved back to a share at the 15th and reclaimed the outright lead for good at the 18th. Bottom of the Trophy table changed hands at H1 (Williams), H2 (Alex Baker) and H11 (Neumann), with Neumann's decisive drop to last coming at the 15th.

## Round 2 — Patterson's 49 Lands a Haymaker

Praia D'El Rey, in its 3rd TEG appearance, played the more generous of the two courses. Patterson took the outright Trophy lead at the 1st and held it the rest of the way.

Patterson's round started badly: bogey, bogey, bogey, double, triple, triple over the opening six holes, before a birdie at the par-5 7th stopped the bleeding. He then piled up 17 points across holes 9–13: bogey at the par-4 9th (3), par at the par-5 10th (4), par at the par-3 11th (3), birdie at the par-5 12th (4), par at the par-4 13th (3). He closed with 14 points over the last four — par at the 15th (4), bogey at the 16th (3), par at the par-5 17th (4), bogey at the 18th (3) — for 49 points, the 2nd-best round in TEG history to date and a gross round of +14.

Alex Baker posted a personal-best 44 points in the same conditions, despite a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th and a quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 14th. Gregg Williams ran up a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 9th.

On the Green Jacket, Mullin briefly ceded a share of the lead to Patterson at the 2nd before reasserting; his round of +14 stretched the Jacket lead to 7. The Trophy lead changed hands twice in the round: Patterson at the 1st, a brief share with Mullin at the 2nd, and Patterson back to a share at the 11th. Patterson finished R2 ten points clear on the Trophy.

## Round 3 — Back to Óbidos, Back to Earth

Back at Royal Óbidos for its 3rd TEG round. Patterson posted 34 points, including a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 12th and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 18th. His Trophy lead shrank from 10 to 3.

Jon Baker was far stronger on the front nine (a 10-point split) and finished on 34 points overall; the back nine included a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 11th. Mullin posted +22 gross, his worst round of the week, including a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 10th — but the Jacket lead held at 7.

## Round 4 — Alex Baker Draws Level — Briefly

Back to Praia D'El Rey for its 4th TEG round. Alex Baker posted a second personal-best 44 points and drew level with Patterson on the Trophy at the par-3 14th with a double-bogey 5. Patterson responded over the closing holes to finish on 47 points and a final-margin gap of 6.

Alex Baker's round included quadruple-bogey 8s at the par-4 6th and the par-4 15th. Gregg Williams ran up a sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 7th — his third sextuple of the week (9, 10, 11). Jon Baker posted a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 17th. Mullin had a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 1st but closed at +16 to extend the Jacket margin from 7 to 10. Neumann's best stretch of the week came here: 16 points across holes 11–15 — par at the par-3 11th, par at the par-5 12th, birdie at the par-4 13th, par at the par-3 14th, bogey at the par-4 15th — but he still finished bottom on 37 points for the round and 127 overall.

## HOW THE COMPETITIONS WERE DECIDED

**The Trophy.** Jon Baker led by 1 after R1 on 39. Patterson took the outright lead at the 1st of R2 at Praia D'El Rey and never gave it back. His 49 in R2 was the decisive blow, opening a 10-point cushion. A 34 at Óbidos in R3 cut the gap to 3. Alex Baker drew level (not outright) at the 14th of R4 with a double-bogey 5, but Patterson's 47 reopened the gap. Final margin: 168 to 162.

**The Green Jacket.** Mullin took the outright Gross lead at the 3rd of R1, lost it as a share at the 6th to Jon Baker, regained a share at the 15th and the outright lead at the 18th — and held it wire-to-wire from there. Round-by-round leads of 2, 7, 7, 10. Rounds of +15, +14, +22, +16. Final margin 10 over Jon Baker.

**The Wooden Spoon.** Neumann dropped to last at the 15th of R1 — during the five-hole haemorrhage of 16 shots to close Óbidos — and was bottom at every round-end thereafter. Round scores of 23, 30, 37, 37 for 127.

## PLAYERS

- **John Patterson** — Trophy winner on 168. Sextuple-bogey 10 at Óbidos's 4th in R1; 49 points at Praia in R2 (2nd-best round in TEG history); 34 at Óbidos in R3 with quadruples at the 12th and 18th; 47 in R4 to seal the win by 6.
- **David Mullin** — Green Jacket winner at +67. Outright Gross lead from R1 H18 to the finish. Rounds of +15, +14, +22, +16. Quintuple at Óbidos's 10th in R3; quadruple at Praia's 1st in R4.
- **Alex Baker** — Trophy runner-up on 162. Twin personal-best 44s in R2 and R4 at Praia. Drew level on the Trophy at R4 H14. Septuple-bogey 11 at Óbidos's 14th in R1; multiple further quads across the week.
- **Jon Baker** — Trophy leader after R1; Green Jacket runner-up. Strong front nine in R3 (16 points across holes 1–5). Quadruples at Óbidos's 18th (R1) and 11th (R3), Praia's 14th (R2) and 17th (R4).
- **Gregg Williams** — Sextuple bogeys in three rounds: a 9 at Óbidos's par-3 15th in R1, a 10 at Praia's 9th in R2, an 11 at Praia's 7th in R4.
- **Stuart Neumann** — Wooden Spoon on 127. Bottom from R1 H15 onwards. Round scores 23, 30, 37, 37. Closing five-hole bleed of 16 shots at Óbidos in R1; best stretch of the week was 16 points across holes 11–15 in R4 at Praia.
