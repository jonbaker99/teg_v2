# Round 4 Draft — Oitavos Dunes

## Overview

David Mullin posted the round's best Stableford return on the way to taking the Green Jacket from Jon Baker by six gross. Henry Meller had the worst of it, bleeding 22 shots across the opening six holes alone. Gregg Williams saw the Trophy home by twelve from Alex Baker; Stuart Neumann collected the Wooden Spoon by eleven.

## The Round

This was TEG's first visit to Oitavos Dunes, Arthur Hills's Atlantic links at Cascais and the tour's second outing on the Lisbon Coast.

**Gregg Williams** began the day five clear in the Trophy race and finished twelve clear, but the round was not clean. A quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 5th started a four-hole bleed of 11 shots through 5-8 (quad-double-double-triple). The Trophy was effectively put away with 12 Stableford points across holes 12-15: par at the par-3 12th, bogey at the par-5 13th, par at the par-3 14th, par at the par-3 15th.

**David Mullin** started the round two gross shots behind Jon Baker. He took the outright Green Jacket lead at the par-4 2nd with a bogey 5. He then ran into a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 11th. He answered immediately with a birdie 4 at the par-5 13th to break the bogey-or-worse run, then, after bogeys at the 14th and 15th, made a second recovery birdie at the par-5 16th. He finished six clear of Baker on Gross.

**Jon Baker** went into the round as overnight Green Jacket leader, lost the outright gross lead at the 2nd, and did not get it back. He finished second on Gross, six behind Mullin, and slipped to third on the Trophy behind Alex Baker.

**Alex Baker** climbed past Jon Baker into Trophy runner-up. He took a quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 15th and closed with a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 18th, neither enough to change his Trophy position.

**Henry Meller** dropped 22 shots across holes 1-6: double bogey, quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 2nd, triple bogey at the par-3 3rd, sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 4th, quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 5th, double bogey at the 6th. He shed a further 6 shots across 11-13 (three double bogeys) and closed with another 9-shot bleed at 16-18 (double, triple, quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 18th).

**Stuart Neumann** confirmed the Wooden Spoon with quadruple-bogey 8s at the par-4 5th and the par-4 17th. He began the round 22 clear at the bottom and finished 11 clear.

## Race Shifts

The Trophy gap stretched from 5 to 12 in Williams's favour, with Alex Baker overtaking Jon Baker for second. The Green Jacket changed hands: Mullin overturned a 2-shot deficit and won by 6, the lead-grab coming as early as the 2nd. The Wooden Spoon was collected by Neumann, the cushion shrinking from 22 to 11 only because Meller's round was worse.
