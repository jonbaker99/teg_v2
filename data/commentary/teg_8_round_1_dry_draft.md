# OVERVIEW

Gregg Williams posted the best round of the day on Stableford, 38 points, to lead the Trophy by four from Jon Baker. David Mullin took the Green Jacket lead on gross with 20 over par, one clear of Jon Baker and five clear of Williams. Stuart Neumann's 17 points — gross 43 over — left him 12 adrift at the foot of the Wooden Spoon race.

# THE ROUND

TEG's first visit to Quinta da Marinha, the Robert Trent Jones Sr. parkland on the Estoril Coast, produced a heavy collective scorecard.

Alex Baker started fastest. Bogeys at each of the first five holes — 6 at the par-5 1st, 4 at the par-3 2nd, 6 at the par-5 3rd, 5 at the par-4 4th, 4 at the par-3 5th — banked 15 Stableford points in five holes.

Henry Meller produced the day's first signature blow-up: a 10 at the par-5 3rd. That dropped him to the foot of the Wooden Spoon race on hole 3, before a birdie at the par-3 5th (a 2) stopped the immediate bleeding. Stuart Neumann then took the Spoon basement off him at the 4th with a triple-bogey 7, and never gave it back.

David Mullin went through a cold patch at 6-8, dropping six shots in three holes: double-bogey 7 at the par-5 6th, double-bogey 5 at the par-3 7th, double-bogey 6 at the par-4 8th. Jon Baker drew level for the gross lead with a bogey at the 6th during the same stretch.

Alex Baker's round turned at the 8th. He ran up a quadruple-bogey 8 there, a triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 9th and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th — 11 shots dropped in three holes.

Williams produced the round's decisive surge across holes 8-11: bogey at the 8th, par at the 9th, birdie 4 at the par-5 10th, bogey at the 11th — 14 points in four holes. The birdie at the 10th took him outright into the Trophy lead. He also drew level on gross at the 11th, where Mullin had earlier drawn level at the 10th.

Neumann's afternoon disintegrated across the same stretch and beyond. From the 7th to the 15th he dropped 29 shots in nine holes: double-bogey 5 at the par-3 7th, double-bogey 6 at the 8th, triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 9th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 11th, quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 12th, double-bogey 6 at the 13th, quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 14th, double-bogey 6 at the 15th.

Jon Baker had his own disaster at the 13th, a quintuple-bogey 9. Mullin parred the same hole to move outright into the Green Jacket lead. Meller dropped six shots over 13-15 (double bogeys at each).

Williams gave shots back through 12-15: double-bogey 6 at the 12th, double-bogey 6 at the 13th, double-bogey 5 at the par-3 14th, quadruple-bogey 8 at the 15th — 10 shots dropped over four holes.

The closing stretch finished off Alex Baker. He dropped 14 shots over 15-18: double-bogey 6 at the 15th, triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 16th, quintuple-bogey 9 at the 17th, quadruple-bogey 8 at the 18th. The back nine cost him 14 points more than his front, splitting his card 20-10.

Jon Baker steadied with a birdie 3 at the 17th to keep within range in both races. Williams held on for 38 points; Mullin's gross 20 over held the Jacket by one.

# RACE SHIFTS

Williams seized the Trophy lead outright with his birdie at the 10th and finished four clear of Jon Baker; the field is already spread from 38 down to Neumann's 17. The Green Jacket lead changed hands repeatedly through the back nine — Mullin drew level at the 10th, Williams drew level at the 11th — before Mullin moved outright clear with his par at the 13th and held on by a single shot from Jon Baker, with Williams five back. The Wooden Spoon passed from Meller to Neumann at the 4th and Neumann's nine-hole bleed from the 7th to the 15th opened a 12-point gap to the rest.
