# Round 1 — Quinta da Marinha — Dry Draft

## Overview
Gregg Williams posted the day's best round on 38 Stableford points, taking an outright four-shot Trophy lead. Stuart Neumann posted the worst, a personal-low 17 points at 43 over gross. David Mullin leads the Green Jacket on 20 over, one clear of Jon Baker; Neumann sits 12 adrift at the bottom of the Wooden Spoon.

## The Round

**Gregg Williams (38 pts).** Best card of the day. His round turned on a four-hole burst from the 8th: bogey at the par-4 8th (3 pts), par at the par-3 9th (3), birdie at the par-5 10th (5) and bogey at the par-5 11th (3) — 14 points in four holes. The birdie at the 10th took him to the outright Trophy lead. He then bled 10 shots from the 12th to the 15th: doubles at the 12th, 13th and par-3 14th, then a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 15th. That was enough to surrender nothing meaningful at the top.

**Jon Baker (34 pts).** Drew level for the gross lead with a bogey at the par-5 6th. His one significant blemish was a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 13th. He stopped the slide late: bogey at the 15th, double at the par-3 16th, then a birdie at the par-4 17th.

**David Mullin (30 pts, 20 over gross).** Steadiest card on the gross sheet. A three-hole wobble from the 6th cost six shots — double bogeys at the par-5 6th, par-3 7th and par-4 8th. He drew level for the Jacket lead with a bogey at the 10th, and took it outright with a par at the par-4 13th.

**Alex Baker (30 pts, 20/10 front/back split).** Opened with five straight bogeys at the 1st through 5th for 15 points. From the 8th the card unravelled: quadruple-bogey 8 at the 8th, triple at the par-3 9th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th — 11 shots dropped in three holes. He then closed with a four-hole stretch from the 15th that shed 14 shots: double at the 15th, triple at the par-3 16th, quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 17th and quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 18th.

**Henry Meller (29 pts).** A quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 3rd briefly dropped him to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon race. He answered with a birdie at the par-3 5th. From the 13th he dropped six shots in three holes: doubles at the 13th, par-3 14th and 15th.

**Stuart Neumann (17 pts, 43 over).** Inherited the Wooden Spoon at the par-4 4th with a triple bogey and never lost it. From the 7th to the 15th he shed 29 shots over nine holes: double at the par-3 7th, double at the 8th, triple at the par-3 9th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 11th, quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 12th, double at the 13th, quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 14th, double at the 15th. Three quintuple bogeys in the stretch. Worst TEG round to date.

## Race Shifts
Williams took the outright Trophy lead with his birdie at the 10th and finished four clear of Jon Baker; the next four players are bunched between 29 and 30 points, with Neumann a further 12 back. The Green Jacket changed hands repeatedly: Jon Baker drew level at the 6th, Mullin drew level at the 10th, Williams drew level at the 11th, before Mullin's par at the 13th moved him outright — he ends one clear of Jon Baker, with Williams six back. Neumann took the Wooden Spoon at the 4th (after Meller's brief tenure from the 3rd) and the nine-hole bleed from the 7th opened a 12-shot gap to the rest of the field.
