# Round 3 — Quinta da Marinha — Draft

## Overview
Jon Baker carded the round of the day with 35 Stableford points; Stuart Neumann the worst with 20. Williams kept the Trophy lead but watched his cushion fall from 11 to 5, Jon Baker took the Green Jacket from David Mullin and finished 2 clear, and Neumann's Wooden Spoon gap to the field ballooned from 8 to 22.

## The Round
TEG's second visit to Robert Trent Jones Sr.'s Quinta da Marinha produced widespread damage. Five players carded at least one quadruple-bogey-or-worse, and between them they ran up four quintuple-bogeys-or-worse: Neumann had four on his own.

**Stuart Neumann** set the tone on the front nine. He opened with a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 1st, added a double bogey at the par-3 2nd and another double at the par-3rd, then a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 4th and a triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 5th — 17 shots dropped across the opening five holes. He added a quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 9th and a sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 10th, bleeding 13 shots across 9–11. He finished with 20 points.

**Alex Baker** went through three triple bogeys in a row at the 3rd, 4th and 5th — 9 shots dropped. After a bogey at the 6th and a double at the 7th, he ran up a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 8th, the centrepiece of a 13-shot bleed across 7–10. A quadruple-bogey 8 followed at the par-4 12th. A birdie at the par-4 17th finally broke a long streak of bogey-or-worse holes. He posted 32 points.

**Gregg Williams** arrived 11 clear in the Trophy and gave most of it back on the back nine. He bled 14 shots across holes 8–11: triple-bogey 7 at the 8th, double-bogey 5 at the 9th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th and a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 11th. He added a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 13th. He returned 29 points and remained the Trophy leader.

**David Mullin** began the day with the Green Jacket lead, 6 clear on gross. He posted his worst round to date at 26 points. At the par-4 12th he made a quadruple-bogey 8 — the hole at which Jon Baker drew level on gross. Mullin did not recover the lead and ended the round 2 behind.

**Jon Baker** was the day's quiet winner. With no headline blow-up he carded the round's best total at 35 points. He drew level with Mullin on gross at the 12th and went 2 clear by the close.

**Henry Meller** added two quadruple-bogey 8s of his own, at the par-4 13th and the par-4 18th, but stayed clear of the Spoon battle. He scored 34 points.

## Race Shifts
The Trophy lead held but more than halved: Williams arrived 11 clear and leaves 5 ahead of Jon Baker with one round to play. The Green Jacket changed hands — Mullin's six-shot overnight lead became a two-shot deficit, with Jon Baker drawing level at the 12th and pulling clear on the closing stretch. The Wooden Spoon position is now effectively settled: Neumann's gap to the field grew from 8 to 22.
