# The Baker's Dozen Minus Five

## Overview

Alex Baker won the Trophy on 169 Stableford points, eight clear of runner-up John Patterson. Gregg Williams won the Green Jacket at +66 gross, 14 ahead of David Mullin. Jon Baker collected the Wooden Spoon on 126 points.

## Round 1 — Baker Opens the Oven at 46

TEG's 4th visit to Catalonia opened on the Tour Course at PGA Catalunya. Alex Baker posted 46 Stableford points to lead the Trophy from the close of day one. He took the outright lead at the par-5 5th with a bogey, briefly ceded it to David Mullin at the 4th, then reclaimed it at the 5th and never let go. The scoring came in two bursts: 17 points across holes 8-12 (bogeys at the 8th, 9th and 12th around pars at the 10th and 11th), and 13 points in three holes from 14-16, including a birdie at the par-5 15th.

Behind him, John Patterson bled 22 shots from holes 3-10, opening with a quadruple at the par-4 3rd and stringing together triples at the 4th, 7th, 8th and 9th. David Mullin took a quadruple at the par-4 6th and an 11 (sextuple) at the par-5 15th. Gregg Williams ran up a 9 at the par-5 7th. In the gross competition, Jon Baker took the outright lead at the 5th and held it at the close of the round. In the Spoon race, Jon Baker dropped to the bottom with a double bogey at the 18th.

## Round 2 — An Eleven on the Eighteenth

A second day on the Tour Course brought Alex Baker 41 points despite two large numbers: a quintuple bogey (8) at the par-3 16th and a sextuple (11) at the par-5 18th. He arrested the slide briefly with a birdie at the 17th between the two. The Trophy lead held.

Jon Baker took outright Jacket lead at the 9th with a par and finished the day still in front by two. Gregg Williams was closing in, drawing level three times during the round — at the 6th, the 10th and the 13th — and assembling 13 points in four holes from 13-16 with four straight pars. David Mullin took quadruples at the 4th and the 6th. Jon Baker's own 16 points across 14-18 (a birdie at the par-3 14th followed by four pars) lifted him out of the bottom of the Stableford table at the close of play, but he had passed through the Spoon position twice during the round, at the 3rd and the 7th, before Mullin took it on at the 18th.

## Round 3 — Stadium Rocks for Williams

The Stadium Course settled the supporting prizes. Gregg Williams took outright Jacket lead at the par-4 2nd with a par and opened with four straight pars from the 2nd through the 5th — 12 points in four holes — before a double bogey at the 6th broke the run. His round of 13 over par turned a two-shot deficit into an eight-shot lead.

At the same hole on the same morning, Jon Baker dropped into the Wooden Spoon position with a triple bogey at the 2nd and stayed there. He also took a quintuple 9 at the par-4 4th and bled eight more shots across 15-18, with double bogeys at every hole from the 15th to the 18th.

Alex Baker's day swung the other way and back again. He bled 12 shots from holes 6-10 (triple at the 6th, doubles at 7, 8 and 9, triple at the 10th) and then piled up 21 points in six holes from 11-16: pars at the 11th, 13th and 14th around bogeys at the 12th, 15th and 16th. The Trophy lead went out to 12.

## Round 4 — Procession on the Stadium

With the destinations decided, the final day produced one more Alex Baker blow-up — a sextuple 10 at the par-4 14th — alongside a quadruple 8 at the par-4 17th and a cold patch from the 9th to the 11th that cost seven shots. He still posted 38 and won the Trophy by eight.

Jon Baker bled 10 shots from the 7th to the 11th with five consecutive double bogeys, stopped the run with a birdie at the 13th, and finished on 126 points. David Mullin dropped nine shots from 14-17 to confirm his runner-up gross position. John Patterson picked up nine points from 4-6 and a birdie at the 17th to lock in second on Stableford. Gregg Williams added 12 over par for his round and took the Green Jacket by 14.

## How the Competitions Were Decided

**Trophy (Stableford).** Alex Baker led after every round — by 12, 11, 12 and 8. The decisive takeover came at R1 H5, with the outright Trophy lead, and the 46-point opening round opened a cushion no rival closed. Final margin: eight points over John Patterson.

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Jon Baker held the lead after R1 (by 2) and R2 (by 2). Gregg Williams drew level three times in R2 (at the 6th, 10th and 13th) before taking the outright lead at R3 H2 with a par. He led by eight after R3 and by 14 after R4. Runner-up: David Mullin.

**Wooden Spoon.** The bottom position changed hands ten times over the week. Jon Baker held it at the close of R1, David Mullin at the close of R2, and Jon Baker again from R3 onwards. The decisive drop was at R3 H2 with a triple bogey. He finished on 126.

## Players

- **Alex Baker** — Won the Trophy from the front despite three blow-ups of seven or worse (R2 18, R2 16, R4 14, R4 17), offset by hot stretches no one matched.
- **Gregg Williams** — Shipped quadruples at the Tour Course (R1 7, R1 17) and ended the week cantering home in the Green Jacket by 14, the Stadium Course bringing out a steadier game.
- **Jon Baker** — Led the Jacket for 36 holes, dropped into the Spoon at R3 H2 and finished on 126 — the rare double of leading and losing in the same week.
- **John Patterson** — Bled 22 shots through R1 holes 3-10 but recovered enough on the back nine and later rounds to finish runner-up in the Trophy.
- **David Mullin** — An 11 at R1's 15th set the tone; brief Spoon flirtations and a steady gross game left him runner-up for the Jacket.
