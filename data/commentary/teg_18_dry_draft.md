# TEG 18 — Catalonia: Dry Storyline Draft

## Overview
Alex Baker won the Trophy on 169 points, eight clear of John Patterson — the 7th-best Trophy total in TEG history. Gregg Williams took the Green Jacket at +66, 14 strokes ahead of David Mullin. Jon Baker finished last on 126 points to collect the Wooden Spoon.

## Round 1 — 46 and Out of Sight: Alex Baker's Statement on the Tour
*PGA Catalunya — Tour Course (the 5th TEG round at this venue).*

Alex Baker shot 46 points and finished the day 12 clear at the top of the Trophy. He went outright Trophy leader on hole 3, was passed for a single hole by David Mullin on the 4th, and retook the lead on the 5th — and stayed there for the rest of the week. He banked the cushion with a 17-point burst across holes 8–12 (three bogeys for 3 points apiece and pars at the par-3 11th and the par-5 10th) and added 13 more in three holes from the 14th, including a birdie at the par-5 15th. That came despite a quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 2nd and a closing quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 18th; a double-bogey 6 at the par-4 17th ended his steady stretch before the 9 at the last.

The Jacket was bunched. Jon Baker took outright Gross lead on hole 5 and held it at day's end, two clear. David Mullin sat in the mix despite a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 6th. Gregg Williams was third at +27 after two quadruples — a 9 at the par-5 7th and an 8 at the par-4 17th — with a birdie at the par-5 15th finally breaking a long run of bogey-or-worse.

John Patterson's morning was the worst of the day: 22 shots dropped across holes 3–10, beginning with a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 3rd and running through five triple bogeys in eight holes. He recovered on the back nine (34 Stableford to 30 gross v par split favouring the inward half).

Jon Baker ended the round at the bottom of the Spoon table.

## Round 2 — The 11 That Didn't Matter
*PGA Catalunya — Tour Course (the 6th TEG round at this venue).*

Alex Baker carded 41 points and went to the turn for the night still 11 clear, despite a quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 16th and a sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 18th. Between them he birdied the par-4 17th to stop the bleeding.

Jon Baker briefly held outright Green Jacket lead with a par at hole 9. He closed with 16 points over the last five holes, sparked by a birdie at the par-3 14th and followed by four straight pars. Gregg Williams kept pace with a 13-point par run across holes 13–16 (all four pars) and finished the round second in the Jacket, two behind Jon Baker. John Patterson posted 16 points of his own from holes 11–15.

The Spoon position changed hands four times during the round — Gregg Williams (H2), Jon Baker (H3), Jon Baker again (H7), and finally David Mullin with a double bogey at the 18th. Mullin's day included quadruple bogeys at the par-4 4th and par-4 6th, part of an 11-shot bleed across holes 4–6. Patterson dropped 11 across holes 6–10.

## Round 3 — The Hole That Decided Two Trophies
*PGA Catalunya — Stadium Course (the 6th TEG round at this venue).*

The second hole did the work on both Gross and Spoon. Gregg Williams parred the par-4 2nd to take outright Green Jacket lead for the first time, and a Jon Baker triple-bogey 7 at the same hole dropped him to the bottom of the Spoon race, where he stayed.

Williams added pars at 3, 4 and 5 for a 12-point opening run, before a double bogey at the par-4 6th broke the sequence. He finished the round in outright Jacket lead by eight.

Alex Baker bled 12 shots across holes 6–10 (a triple at the par-4 6th, doubles at 7, 8 and 9, a triple at the par-4 10th), then piled up 21 points over holes 11–16 — pars at the par-3 11th, par-4 13th, par-4 14th, and three more points each at the par-5 12th, par-5 15th and par-3 16th — to finish on 44 and stretch the Trophy lead back to 12.

Jon Baker also made a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 4th. David Mullin took 10 points in three holes from the 8th, including a birdie at the par-4 10th.

## Round 4 — The Coronations
*PGA Catalunya — Stadium Course (the 7th TEG round at this venue).*

Nothing left to decide. Alex Baker closed with 38 points, taking a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 14th and a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 17th but winning the Trophy by 8.

Gregg Williams added 12 points to bring in the Jacket by 14, with a nine-point three-hole run from the par-3 8th (par–bogey–par).

Jon Baker bled 10 shots across holes 7–11 — five straight double bogeys — and his round of 29 sealed the Spoon. He did break a long bogey-or-worse run with a birdie at the par-4 13th.

John Patterson took nine points across holes 4–6 and birdied the par-4 17th to end a triple-double-bogey stretch from the 14th.

## How the Competitions Were Decided

**Trophy (Stableford).** Alex Baker led from R1 hole 5 to the final putt. He led by 12 after R1, 11 after R2, 12 after R3 and won by 8. The decisive takeover was his retake of outright lead on R1 hole 5; no one led the Trophy after that point.

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Jon Baker led after R1 (+27, by 2) and R2 (+41, by 2). On R3 hole 2 at the Stadium, Gregg Williams parred to take outright Gross lead for the first time. He led by 8 after R3 and won by 14 — closing rounds of 13 and 12 on the Stadium course with no challenger in range.

**Wooden Spoon.** Jon Baker propped up the field after R1, climbed off the bottom in R2 with 39 points (Mullin ended R2 last), then a triple bogey at R3 hole 2 put him back at the bottom for good. R3 28 points, R4 29 points; 126 total.

## Players

- **Alex Baker** — Trophy winner on 169 (7th-best in TEG history). Led from R1 H5 to the finish, never less than 8 clear at any round-end, and still carded an 11 (R2 H18), a 10 (R4 H14), two 8s (R2 H16, R4 H17) and a 9 (R1 H18) along the way.
- **Gregg Williams** — Green Jacket winner at +66, by 14. Third after R1 with two quadruples (R1 H7, R1 H17), to second after R2, to outright lead with a par at R3 H2, and home by 14.
- **Jon Baker** — Wooden Spoon on 126. Held the Jacket lead through R1 and R2, briefly outright after R2 H9; a triple at R3 H2 dropped him to the bottom and he stayed there.
- **John Patterson** — Trophy runner-up, 8 back. Survived a 22-shot bleed over holes 3–10 in R1 and an 11-shot bleed over holes 6–10 in R2; consistent back-nine scoring across the week.
- **David Mullin** — Green Jacket runner-up. Briefly led the Trophy on R1 H4. Week included an 11 (R1 H15), two 8s (R1 H6, R2 H4, R2 H6) and a three-hole, 11-shot bleed in R2.
