# The Baker's Dozen Minus Five

## Overview

Alex Baker won the Trophy on 169 Stableford points, eight clear of John Patterson. Gregg Williams won the Green Jacket at +66 gross, beating runner-up David Mullin by 14. Jon Baker took the Wooden Spoon on 126 points.

TEG 18 was the 4th visit to Catalonia and a return to PGA Catalunya: two days on the Tour Course (the 5th and 6th TEG rounds at that course) followed by two days on the Stadium Course (the 6th and 7th).

## Round 1 — Baker Opens the Oven at 46

Tour Course, 11 October. Alex Baker shot 46 Stableford points and was effectively never caught. After a brief opening exchange — Alex went ahead at the 3rd, Mullin drew level/ahead at the 4th — Alex took outright lead of the Trophy at the 5th and held it for the rest of the week. The round was built on a 17-point run through holes 8–12 and a 13-point burst across 14–16 that included a birdie at the par-5 15th. By the close he led the Trophy by 12.

Behind him the field bled. John Patterson dropped 22 shots across holes 3–10, opening with an 8 at the 3rd and stringing together quadruple, triple, double, double, triple, triple, triple and double. David Mullin took an 8 at the 6th and an 11 at the par-5 15th. Gregg Williams ran up a 9 at the 7th.

Jon Baker led the Green Jacket after R1 by two. In the Spoon race, four players took turns at the bottom; Jon Baker held it at the close.

## Round 2 — An Eleven on the Eighteenth

Tour Course, 12 October. Alex Baker added 41 points and kept the Trophy lead at 11, despite an 8 (quintuple) at the par-3 16th and an 11 (sextuple) at the par-5 18th. He stopped the bleeding with a birdie at 17 between the two blow-ups.

Jon Baker took the outright Green Jacket lead at the 9th and finished the round still two ahead on gross — but Williams drew level repeatedly through the round and posted a four-hole run of pars at 13–16 worth 13 points. Williams's gross 14 was the round of the day. Jon Baker's own back nine was strong: 16 points across 14–18, opened by a birdie at the par-3 14th.

David Mullin ran up further 8s at the 4th and 6th and ended the day at the bottom of the Spoon race.

## Round 3 — Stadium Rocks for Williams

Stadium Course, 13 October. The supporting competitions were settled here.

Williams took the outright Green Jacket lead at the 2nd and opened the round with four straight pars (holes 2–5) for 12 points. A double bogey at the 6th ended the run, but his round of +13 was enough to turn a two-shot deficit into an eight-shot lead.

At the same 2nd hole, Jon Baker made a 7 and dropped to the bottom of the Spoon race for good. He then made a 9 (quintuple) at the 4th.

Alex Baker bled 12 shots across holes 6–10 — triple, double, double, double, triple — but answered with 21 points in six holes through 11–16 (par, bogey, par, par, bogey, bogey). The Trophy lead went back up to 12. Mullin took 10 points across 8–10, including a birdie at the 10th.

## Round 4 — Procession on the Stadium

Stadium Course, 14 October. Nothing material left to decide.

Alex Baker shot 38 and added one more big number — a 10 (sextuple) at the par-4 14th and a quadruple at the 17th — but won the Trophy by 8. Williams added a gross 12 to take the Jacket by 14, with a three-hole run of 9 points through 8–10.

Jon Baker dropped 10 shots across 7–11 (five straight double bogeys). He stopped the bleeding with a birdie at the 13th, but the Spoon was already his. Patterson made a triple at the 14th and a double at the 15th before a birdie at the 17th.

## How the competitions were decided

**Trophy (Stableford).** Alex Baker took the outright lead at R1 hole 5 and led at every round-end: by 12, 11, 12 and finally 8. The decisive blow was the 46-point opening round on the Tour Course, which the field never made meaningful inroads into. His R3 burst of 21 points in six holes restored the cushion after a 12-shot wobble through 6–10. Final margin: 8 points over John Patterson.

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Jon Baker led after R1 (by 2) and R2 (by 2). Williams drew level multiple times in R2 before taking the outright lead at R3 hole 2 on the Stadium Course. From there he stretched the lead to 8 after R3 and 14 after R4, with rounds of 13 and 12 over par on the Stadium. Runner-up: David Mullin.

**Wooden Spoon.** The bottom changed hands ten times. Jon Baker first took it at R1 hole 18, briefly handed it to David Mullin in R2, then dropped to the bottom for the last time at R3 hole 2 — the same hole at which Williams claimed the Jacket. R4's cold patch through 7–11 confirmed it. Final score: 126 points.

## Players

- **Alex Baker** — Won the Trophy from the front. Three blow-ups of 8 or worse (R2 16, R2 18, R4 14) were absorbed by hot stretches the field could not match.
- **Gregg Williams** — Shipped quadruples on the Tour Course in R1, then put together gross rounds of 14, 13 and 12 to win the Green Jacket by 14.
- **Jon Baker** — Led the Green Jacket after 36 holes, lost it at R3 hole 2 and took the Wooden Spoon at the same hole. Finished last on 126.
- **John Patterson** — Bled 22 shots through R1 holes 3–10, recovered on the back nine and finished Trophy runner-up, 8 points back.
- **David Mullin** — An 11 at the par-5 15th in R1 set the tone; a steady gross game left him runner-up for the Green Jacket.
