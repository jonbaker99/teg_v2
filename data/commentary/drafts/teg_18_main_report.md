## TOURNAMENT OVERVIEW {.tournament-overview}

Alex Baker claimed his second TEG Trophy with a performance that defied conventional logic, leading from start to finish despite suffering 27 worse-than-double bogeys across 72 holes. Spain's premier course returned to the schedule after a six-TEG absence. Baker's eight-point margin survived a catastrophic final round featuring six triple bogeys and a quintuple, while Gregg Williams seized the Green Jacket with back-to-back dominant displays, equalising David Mullin's new course record with a closing 84 at PGA Catalunya's Stadium layout.

The tournament marked the first confirmed wire-to-wire Trophy victory since tracking began. Jon Baker's collapse from Green Jacket leader through 36 holes to Wooden Spoon recipient provided the cruelest subplot, his third-round quintuple on the fourth hole triggering an irreversible slide. Williams erased his own opening disasters to claim his first Jacket alongside a third-place Trophy finish.

## Round 1: Tour Course Welcomes TEG with Front-Nine Chaos {.round1 .round}

The Tour Course at PGA Catalunya greeted competitors with benign conditions that masked early carnage. All five players started level. Spain's spring sunshine offered no protection from the disasters that would define the opening 18.

Baker opened with a quadruple bogey at the second, a par three that yielded no points and dropped him from first to fourth in the Trophy standings within minutes. The third hole produced triple bogeys for Williams and Jon Baker, whilst John Patterson recorded a quadruple of his own. Williams compounded his misery with another quadruple at the seventh, a par five averaging 2.6 over for the field. By the turn, Baker had recovered through a purple patch: four points at both the tenth and 11th holes sparked a 21-point surge across six holes that carried him to a commanding 46-point round total.

Jon Baker took the early Jacket lead at the fifth and never relinquished it over the opening 18. Patterson staged an epic back-nine comeback, rallying from 10 front-nine points to 24 on the inward half—a 14-point turnaround that hauled him from fifth to second in the Trophy race. Mullin opened with a triple at the first and registered five zero-point holes before limping home.

## Round 2: Williams Wins Both Formats as Alex Baker Extends Grip {.round2 .round}

The Tour Course returned for Saturday's second instalment with Baker holding a 12-point Trophy cushion and Jon Baker defending a slender two-stroke Jacket advantage. Conditions remained calm under Catalonian sun.

Williams seized the Jacket lead at the sixth as Jon Baker stumbled to a double bogey, only to hand it back three holes later with a triple at the ninth. Jon Baker reclaimed first place with a par at the same hole and reached the 36-hole mark atop the Gross leaderboard at 39 over. His back-nine brilliance—24 points including a birdie at the 14th—overcame front-nine struggles. Williams' 42-point round won both competitions on the day, propelling him to second in the Jacket race and fourth in the Trophy standings.

Baker endured another volatile afternoon. A triple at the third dropped him momentarily before a quintuple bogey at the 16th—an eight at the par three—threatened his entire campaign. He responded immediately with a birdie at the 17th, a five-point haul that steadied nerves and preserved his 11-point Trophy lead through 36 holes. Mullin and Patterson both posted quadruple bogeys at the fourth, whilst Mullin's three consecutive zero-point holes from the fourth to sixth represented the coldest spell of the round.

Patterson's steady 42 points tied Williams for round honours in the Trophy and kept him within touching distance in second place overall. Mullin's back-nine recovery—sparked by a birdie at the tenth, the only one of the round in gross terms—salvaged 20 points and limited the damage from his early implosion.

## Round 3: Stadium Course Delivers Record and Ruin {.round3 .round}

The Stadium Course—Spain's top-ranked layout and a World Top 100 test—arrived for the weekend's conclusion. Jon Baker carried a two-stroke Jacket advantage. Baker held a commanding 11-point Trophy lead. The course delivered its verdict swiftly.

Jon Baker lost the Jacket lead at the second hole with a triple bogey as Williams surged ahead. Two holes later, a quintuple at the fourth—part of a brutal stretch that saw him average close to three over per hole from the second to fourth—ended his Jacket hopes and sent him tumbling toward the Wooden Spoon. Williams held the Gross lead from that point to the tournament's end. Alex Baker's own struggles continued: triple bogeys at the second and third, another at the sixth, and a final one at the tenth marked his seventh worse-than-double disaster in 54 holes. Yet his 44 points, tied with Mullin for round honours, maintained a 12-point Trophy cushion.

Mullin produced the round's defining moment. His 84—twelve over par—established a new Stadium Course record. The only gross birdie of the day came at the tenth during a stretch where he averaged level par from the eighth to tenth and from the first to sixth. Strong front-nine scoring of 25 points offset back-nine struggles that included a triple at the 13th.

Patterson's 43 points kept him in second place overall, whilst Williams matched that total to tighten his grip on the Jacket. Baker led the Trophy for all 18 holes. Williams led the Gross for all 18. The stage was set for a final-round coronation in both competitions.

## Round 4: Williams Matches Record, Baker Survives Unsurvivable {.round4 .round}

The Stadium Course returned for Monday's finale with Baker defending a 12-point Trophy advantage and Williams holding an eight-stroke Jacket lead. The margins suggested comfort. The round delivered chaos.

Baker's horror show began early. Triple bogeys at the third and fourth—averaging 2.33 over par from the second to fourth—were merely appetisers. Another triple at the seventh and ninth meant four disasters before the turn. A temporary reprieve at the 12th, where he collected four points, proved fleeting. The 14th hole delivered the tournament's defining image: a quintuple bogey, ten strokes on the par four. The hole averaged three over par for the field and also claimed a triple from Patterson. Baker's stretch from the 13th to 15th averaged three over per hole, his worst of the round.

Yet the lead never wavered. Patterson's 42 points, fueled by a strong front nine of 23 points and a brilliant birdie at the 17th worth five points, closed to within eight. Williams' 44 points—earned despite a closing triple at the 18th—matched Mullin's record with an 84. His stretch from the third to eighth averaged 0.17 over par, and he never threatened to relinquish the Jacket. The 18th claimed triple bogeys from Williams, Jon Baker and Patterson in a fittingly chaotic finale.

Baker's 38 points, joint third for the round, included six triple bogeys and the quintuple. His 169-point total represented an eight-point victory margin. Patterson finished second with 161. Williams claimed third in the Trophy with 160 whilst securing the Green Jacket by 14 strokes at 66 over par. Mullin placed second in the Jacket at 80 over. Jon Baker collected the Wooden Spoon with 126 Trophy points and 92 over par in the Gross.

## TOURNAMENT SUMMARY {.summary}

Alex Baker's wire-to-wire Trophy triumph represented the most paradoxical victory in TEG history. His 27 worse-than-double bogeys across 72 holes would sink any ordinary campaign. Instead, his opening 46 points established a lead that never dipped below eight points, extending through each round despite repeated blow-ups. The final tally of 169 points arrived via seven final-round disasters that merely reduced rather than eliminated his advantage. He led for 70 of 72 holes in the Trophy competition.

Gregg Williams erased a disastrous opening round—featuring a quadruple at the seventh and triple bogeys at the third and fourth—with dominant performances on Saturday and Sunday. His 42-point second round won both competitions on the day. Sunday's 44 points and 84 gross equalled Mullin's course record despite a closing triple. He commanded the Jacket from the third round's second hole to the finish line, a 14-stroke margin over Mullin validating his consistency across the final 36 holes.

Jon Baker's collapse provided the tournament's cruellest narrative arc. Leading the Jacket through 36 holes, his third-round quintuple at the fourth triggered a slide that ended with the Wooden Spoon. Patterson's steady accumulation of 161 points secured second in the Trophy, whilst Mullin's third-round course record of 84 earned him second place in the Jacket at 80 over par. The Stadium Course averaged brutal across the field, particularly the 14th, which claimed disasters from multiple competitors in the final round. Baker's victory margin survived seven disasters in 18 holes. The lead that wouldn't die.
