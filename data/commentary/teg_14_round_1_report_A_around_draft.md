# Mullin Masters the Kent Wind

Royal Cinque Ports does not, as a rule, ask polite questions. A two-time Open host on TEG's second-ever pilgrimage to Kent, Deal spent the day doing what serious links do when the breeze gets up: handing out lessons, one fairway at a time. The winning Stableford score was 40. Forty. That is not a number that flatters golfers; it is a number that flatters the course. And yet out of the wind, with the cards scattering and the par-5s biting back, one man simply got on with it. David Mullin's four-hole stretch from the 9th to the 12th was less a purple patch than a small, dignified mugging — the moment he relieved the rest of the field of the Trophy, the Jacket, and any lingering illusions about what kind of day this was going to be.

The damage, in fairness, had started long before Mullin lit the fuse. At the par-5 3rd, Gregg Williams opened proceedings by signing for a quadruple-bogey 9 — a hole that established, in one swift transaction, both the difficulty of the golf course and the shape of Gregg's afternoon. Two groups, or two minds, away, Alex Baker was discovering the same truth in instalments: a double-bogey 7 at that same 3rd, a double-bogey 5 at the par-3 4th, and another double-bogey 7 at the par-5 5th. Six shots gone in three holes, and with hindsight a fairly unmistakable warning klaxon for what the back nine had in store.

The 4th, meanwhile, was quietly doing its own work. While Williams was nursing his 9 and Alex was busy bogeying everything in sight, Jon Baker dropped a shot at the par-3 — a small, unflashy slip that nevertheless sent him to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon race before most people had finished their first sleeve of balls. He would not climb back out of it all day.

For a brief moment around the turn, the leaderboard wobbled into ambiguity. A par at the 6th drew Alex Baker level for the Green Jacket — a flicker of resistance, the field's last credible push against the inevitable. And then Mullin happened.

The 9th: par, and level with Alex for the Jacket. The 10th: birdie at the par-4. The 11th: par, level with Alex for the Trophy. The 12th: par again. Thirteen Stableford points across four holes on a course where the rest of the room was just trying to keep its trousers dry. By the time Mullin came off the 12th green, both competitions had quietly slipped into his pocket and the others were essentially playing for second.

And while Mullin was making his measured par at the 11th, Alex was making — there is no kind way to phrase this — a quadruple-bogey 8 at the same par-4. Jon, to his credit, was producing the most coherent passage of his day in the same breath: three straight pars at 11, 12 and 13, nine points, a brief insistence that he could in fact play this game. Mullin's surge finally hiccupped at the 13th with a double-bogey 6, but by then the round was already framed and hung.

The Trophy was formally settled at the par-5 16th. Mullin made a par-5 and walked off in outright command. Alex Baker, on the same hole, made a 10 — a quintuple-bogey, the kind of number that doesn't so much ruin a round as redefine it. From level for the Jacket at the 6th to a card properly detonated by the 16th: a slow-motion collapse, almost respectable in its thoroughness.

## Where the Races Stand

**Trophy.** Mullin leads on **40**, three clear of Alex Baker on 37, with Williams on 33 and Jon Baker on 29. Level at the 11th, outright at the 16th, and three points clear when the flag came down.

**Green Jacket.** Mullin on **15** over gross, five ahead of Jon Baker on 20, Williams on 21, Alex Baker on 26. Levelled with Alex at the 9th; pulled away as the back nine did its work.

**Wooden Spoon.** Jon Baker bottom on **29**, four adrift of Williams. Dropped to last at the 4th and never found the ladder.

## The men, in brief

- **David Mullin** — The only player who looked as though he'd read the course's CV before teeing off. A 13-point flurry from 9 to 12 turned a contest into a procession; leads both the Trophy and the Jacket overnight.
- **Alex Baker** — Bled six shots across 3–5, briefly drew level for the Jacket at the 6th, then watched the wheels come off in stages: a quad-bogey 8 at the 11th and a frankly cinematic 10 at the par-5 16th. Three back in the Trophy, but it feels further.
- **Gregg Williams** — The quad-bogey 9 at the 3rd set the tone and the tone stuck. Third in both competitions, which is a polite way of saying he was never quite in either.
- **Jon Baker** — Bottom of the Spoon by the 4th and immovable thereafter, despite a perfectly respectable three-par run at 11–13. Sneaks Jacket runner-up, which is the silver lining of a long, breezy afternoon.
