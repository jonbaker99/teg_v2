# OVERVIEW

Jon Baker won the Trophy at -18 (net vs par), 18 clear of runner-up David Mullin. Mullin took the Green Jacket at +72 gross, 12 ahead of Stuart Neumann. Henry Meller took the Wooden Spoon at +16 net vs par. All four rounds were played at Boavista Golf & Spa Resort, Lagos, between 5 and 8 October 2010.

# ROUND 1 — Baker's -9, Mullin's +17, Meller's 12: the day the record book caught fire

Baker shot -9 net (gross +19), the lowest round in TEG history to date (b04, b11). Mullin shot +17 gross (-1 net), the lowest Gross round in TEG history to date (b03); Mullin's -1 net was the 3rd-best round in TEG history to date (b10). Baker ended the day eight clear in the Trophy.

In the Jacket, Mullin moved into a share of the lead at the 4th and held or shared it from then on; he finished the round two clear.

Meller made a 12 at the par-4 17th — eight over par, the worst score to par on any hole in TEG history (b02). The blow-up dropped him to the foot of the Wooden Spoon standings at hole 17 (b58), where he ended Round 1.

# ROUND 2 — Meller's -2 and Mullin's 9: the chase that wasn't

Baker shot -2 net (gross +26), the 2nd-best round in TEG history to date (b06), and extended the Trophy lead to 13.

Meller shot -2 net / +18 gross, the 2nd-best round and 2nd-best Gross round in TEG history to date (b08, b09). The round lifted him off the bottom of the Spoon; Neumann took over at the foot of the standings after a bogey at the par-5 8th (b42).

Mullin made a 9 (quintuple bogey) at the par-4 17th (b17) but still posted +21 gross and stretched the Jacket lead to five.

# ROUND 3 — Mullin breaks his own record; Meller breaks himself

Mullin shot +14 gross (-4 net), the lowest Gross round in TEG history to date, breaking his own week-old mark (b05); the -4 net was also the 3rd-best round in TEG history to date (b12). The Jacket lead grew to nine.

Baker shot -7 net (gross +21), the 2nd-best round in TEG history to date (b07), and pushed the Trophy lead to 16. Within the round he gathered 17 Stableford points across holes 6–10: bogey at the par-3 6th, par at the par-4 7th, bogey at the par-5 8th, par at the par-4 9th, par at the par-4 10th (b19).

Neumann shot +18 gross, the 3rd-best Gross round in TEG history to date (b13). Williams shot -4 net (gross +26), the 3rd-best round in TEG history to date (b14).

Meller's day went the other way. A triple bogey at the par-4 1st dropped him to the bottom of the Spoon (b33-equivalent path); Williams briefly took over after a quadruple at the par-3 2nd; Meller then made a 10 (quintuple bogey) at the par-5 3rd and returned to last for good (b20, b35). He stayed there for the remainder of the tournament.

# ROUND 4 — Baker home by 18, Mullin by 12 — and Neumann finds a 10

Baker shot level-par 0 to win the Trophy at -18, 18 clear (b01).

Mullin shot +20 gross — including a 7 (quadruple bogey) at the par-3 11th (b23) — and won the Green Jacket at +72, 12 clear of Neumann (b16).

Neumann made a 10 (sextuple bogey) at the par-4 17th (b15).

Meller finished at +16 net vs par and collected the Wooden Spoon (b21).

# HOW THE COMPETITIONS WERE DECIDED

**The Trophy.** Baker led after every round. The only lead change came at hole 6 of Round 1, when Mullin briefly drew level (not outright). Baker's -9 (b04) opened an eight-shot gap by the end of Round 1; the margin then grew to 13 after Round 2 (-2, b06), 16 after Round 3 (-7, b07), and 18 after a level-par closing round. Final margin: 18 shots over Mullin.

**The Green Jacket.** Mullin led from hole 4 of Round 1 to the finish. He drew level at that hole, as did Meller, before Mullin moved clear; he was never headed thereafter. The Jacket lead by round ran 2 → 5 → 9 → 12. His Round 1 +17 (b03) and Round 3 +14 (b05) were each, in turn, the lowest Gross round in TEG history. Final margin: 12 shots over Neumann.

**The Wooden Spoon.** The bottom of the standings changed hands nine times. Meller dropped to last after his 12 at the 17th in Round 1 (b58). His -2 in Round 2 (b08) lifted him out and Neumann took over at the par-5 8th (b42). In Round 3 the position changed three times in the opening three holes: Meller to last at the 1st, Williams at the 2nd, and Meller back at the par-5 3rd after his 10 (b35) — the decisive drop. He stayed bottom through Round 4 and finished at +16.

# PLAYERS

- **Jon Baker** — Led the Trophy from Round 1 to the finish; -9 in R1 (TEG record at the time), -2 in R2 (then 2nd-best ever), -7 in R3 (then 2nd-best ever), level par in R4; won by 18. Within the week made a quadruple at the par-4 5th and a quintuple at the par-4 18th in R3.
- **David Mullin** — Led the Jacket from R1 hole 4 to the finish; +17 gross in R1 and +14 gross in R3 each set the TEG record for lowest Gross round. Made a 9 at the 17th in R2 and a 7 at the par-3 11th in R4. Won the Jacket at +72 by 12; runner-up in the Trophy.
- **Henry Meller** — 12 at the par-4 17th in R1; -2 net / +18 gross in R2 (then 2nd-best round and 2nd-best Gross round in TEG history); 10 at the par-5 3rd in R3; Wooden Spoon at +16.
- **Stuart Neumann** — +18 gross in R3 (then 3rd-best Gross round in TEG history); 10 at the par-4 17th in R4; runner-up for the Green Jacket, 12 behind Mullin.
- **Gregg Williams** — -4 net in R3 (then 3rd-best round in TEG history); also made quadruple bogeys at the par-3 6th and par-4 18th in R1, the par-4 10th in R2, the par-3 2nd in R3, and the par-5 3rd, par-4 7th and par-4 12th in R4.
