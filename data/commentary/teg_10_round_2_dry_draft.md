# Round 2 Draft — Palmares (Praia / Alvor)

## Overview
Gregg Williams posted the round of the day with a personal-best 48 points; Alex Baker also set a personal best with 45. Stuart Neumann's 27 points was the worst card of the day, weighed down by two 10s and a 9 inside six holes. David Mullin held the Trophy lead but saw his cushion shrink from 8 to 5, while his Jacket lead narrowed only fractionally from 14 to 13; John Patterson's 10 at the 1st kept him anchored to the Wooden Spoon despite Neumann's efforts.

## The Round
The day started badly for John Patterson, who ran up a 10 at the par-4 1st — a sextuple bogey worth no points. At the same hole, Jon Baker opened with a par, then birdied the par-5 2nd and parred the par-3 3rd to bank 10 points in three holes. Alex Baker matched him for steadiness if not flair, bogey-bogey-par across the same opening trio for 9 points.

Mullin's round took shape from the 4th. He parred the par-5 4th, the par-3 5th, the par-4 6th, the par-4 7th and the par-3 8th — five straight pars for 15 points — before a double bogey 7 at the par-5 9th cost him a shot to the field.

Alex Baker added another 10 points across the 5th to 7th (bogey, bogey, par), while Jon Baker's calm continued with six straight pars from the 5th to the 10th — 18 points on the spin — though that run had been preceded by a double bogey 7 at the par-5 4th that interrupted his opening surge.

Williams' round came alive on the inward stretch. From the 8th he went par, par on the par-5 9th, birdie at the par-4 10th, par at the par-3 11th, bogey at the par-4 12th — 18 points across five holes. He then closed par-par-bogey-bogey from the 15th for a further 12 points, bringing him home in 48 for a personal best.

The 13th to 15th was where Neumann's card collapsed. A 10 at the par-5 13th (quintuple bogey), a 9 at the par-4 14th (quintuple bogey) and a 7 at the par-4 15th (triple bogey) bled 13 shots in three holes. He completed the damage with another 10 at the par-4 18th, a sextuple bogey. Earlier he had played the 9th to 12th in 14 points (par, bogey, par, par), which only highlighted how much he gave back afterwards.

Alex Baker's own wobble came at 12 to 14 — three straight double bogeys (6 at the par-4 12th, 7 at the par-5 13th, 6 at the par-4 14th) for 6 shots dropped — but he had enough in the bank to post 45 and a personal best.

Patterson, after the opening 10, never recovered the round and finished bottom of the day's cards alongside Neumann's 27.

## Race Shifts
**Trophy:** Mullin's 42 points were outscored by Williams (48), Alex Baker (45) and Jon Baker. The lead is down from 8 to 5, with Alex Baker and Williams now tied for second on 86 and Jon Baker fourth on 82.

**Green Jacket:** Mullin's gross lead over Jon Baker narrowed only from 14 to 13. Everyone else is now 39 or more over par.

**Wooden Spoon:** Patterson remains the laggard, but the gap to Neumann has widened from 3 to 5 — Neumann's two 10s and a 9 saw him score worse than Patterson on the day without overtaking him at the bottom.
