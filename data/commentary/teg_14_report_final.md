# Deal Or No Deal: Mullin's Kentish Coronation

TEG's second pilgrimage to Kent in 2021 was, on the face of it, a procession. David Mullin led both competitions at every checkpoint, from the opening tee at Deal to the final green at Prince's, and walked off with the Trophy on 154 points and the Green Jacket at +68. Read the scoreboard at halfway and you'd have packed up and gone home: nine clear in the Trophy, twelve in the Jacket, the field reduced to playing for second.

But this is TEG, and Alex Baker was in the field. By Sunday tea the Trophy margin was two. The Jacket was never in doubt. And down at the foot of the table, Jon Baker was working on the longest unbroken stay in the cellar that anyone could remember — sixty-eight holes of it, dating back to a single bogey on Friday morning.

The thread tying it all together was the par-4 16th. Two of them, in fact, at two entirely different courses. Alex Baker found both.

## Round 1 — Deal Me In: Mullin Lays Down a Marker

Royal Cinque Ports, Deal — formidable championship links, two-time Open host, the venue's second TEG appearance — set the tone. Mullin's round was a study in patience punctuated by one decisive surge: par, a birdie 3 at the par-4 10th, par, par, for thirteen Stableford points from the 9th to the 12th. He had drawn level in the Trophy on the 11th; he took outright lead at the par-5 16th and never relinquished it for the remaining sixty-five holes of the week.

Forty points; clear at the top of both boards by sundown.

Alex Baker, meanwhile, was already auditioning for the role he would play all week. A quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 11th was the warm-up. The main event came at the par-5 16th, where he ran up a quintuple-bogey 10 — a hole number, dear reader, to remember. Gregg Williams chipped in with a 9 at the par-5 3rd, because somebody had to.

And then there was Jon Baker, who slipped to last in the Spoon race at the par-3 4th. Not via some flaming wreck — just a bogey 4. The most ordinary blemish imaginable. He would not be seen above the cellar floor again.

## Round 2 — Littlestone, Big Lead: Mullin Out to Nine

To the Romney Marshes, and Laidlaw Purves's Littlestone, refined later by Braid and MacKenzie — second TEG visit, and for one player a particularly hostile reception.

Alex Baker bled twelve shots in four holes. Triple at the par-4 2nd, double at the par-4 3rd, triple at the par-4 4th, a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 5th. By the time he located solid ground he was a long way behind, and for good measure he later added an 8 at the par-4 8th. Five holes accounting for the best part of an afternoon.

Mullin, to be clear, was not immaculate. He went triple at the par-4 4th and double at the par-5 5th, and looked momentarily mortal — until he stopped the rot with a birdie at the par-5 7th. He pulled the trick again on the way in, bogey-bogey-double at 15, 16 and 17 erased by a birdie 4 up the par-5 18th. Two timely birdies, one tidy 41-point card, and a Trophy lead now sitting at a positively luxurious nine.

## Round 3 — Prince's and the Pauper Field: Mullin Cruises

The first of two days on the Shore/Dunes routing at Prince's. The leader was happy to be here.

He was not, however, immune. Mullin racked up a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 8th — a reminder, if one were needed, that the man can shed strokes in bulk when the mood takes him. But where others would have unravelled, he simply rebooted on the back nine, where his scoring was a full ten points stronger than the front. He closed in style: pars at 15, 16 and the par-3 17th, then a birdie 3 at the par-4 18th. Thirteen points in four holes to sign off the day.

Round won. 36 points. Trophy lead: eight. Jacket lead: an untroubled twelve.

Elsewhere, Alex Baker found an 8 at the par-4 10th, because of course he did. Gregg Williams produced the round's most pleasing miniature — three pars in a row at 9, 10 and 11 for nine points — before promptly carding a triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 12th, which more or less summarised his week on a single scorecard.

And Jon Baker, briefly, looked liberated. A neat middle stretch, the basement momentarily a place he might leave. Then a triple bogey at the par-3 17th, and he was back where he had been since Friday morning.

## Round 4 — Sixteenth Time Unlucky: Alex Baker's 10 Hands Mullin the Trophy

Back to Prince's, third TEG visit, the rare luxury of consecutive days on the same loop. Alex Baker, eight back, came out as if he meant it. Three pars at the 1st, 2nd and 3rd for ten points. The lead was being chipped at. By the time he had stitched together a further three pars in the middle of the round — eleven points across holes 13 to 15 — the Trophy gap had shrunk to something genuinely uncomfortable.

And then, the par-4 16th. Different course, different routing, same hole number, same outcome: a sextuple-bogey 10. The second 10 of his week at a 16th, an act of such precise self-sabotage that it bordered on the artistic. Contest over.

Mullin, for his part, contributed a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 10th — a final, almost ceremonial admission that he could detonate too — and still posted 37 points. Two-shot Trophy win, seven-shot Jacket win, his third-best Trophy finish on record.

Jon Baker produced the most thrilling sequence of his week and possibly his TEG career: birdie at the par-5 12th, birdie at the par-4 13th, pars at 14 and 15. Fourteen points in four holes. Glorious. He then conjured a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 18th to round things off in the only way he knew how, and accepted the Spoon with the weariness of a man who'd worn it since Friday lunchtime.

## The Reckoning

**Trophy.** Mullin led by 3, 9, 8 and 2 — seizing outright control at Deal's par-5 16th in Round 1 on the back of that 9th-to-12th burst, building the cushion to nine at Littlestone, and surviving Alex Baker's Sunday charge thanks to a familiar number on a familiar hole. Margin: two.

**Green Jacket.** Briefer skirmish. Alex Baker drew level at the par-4 6th in Round 1; Mullin levelled back at the par-4 9th, and from there the gap only widened. Twelve at halfway, twelve through three, seven at the close. Even quadruples at the 8th and 10th couldn't dent it. +68, and the jacket fits.

**Wooden Spoon.** Jon Baker, par-3 4th at Deal, Friday morning. He didn't move. 125 points, and an honourable mention for the only man this week to author both the field's most exhilarating four-hole stretch and a closing 8 to undo it.

A Kentish coronation, then, but a closer-run one than the leaderboard at halfway suggested. Mullin led from the gun. Alex Baker kept him honest. And somewhere out there, on a par-4 16th at a links course yet to be announced, the joke is presumably already being written.
