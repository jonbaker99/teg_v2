## Round Report

# Williams and Patterson Share Spoils as Baker Cruises

**PGA Catalunya - Tour • 12/10/2025 • Round 2 of 4**

### Round Summary

Alex Baker came to PGA Catalunya's Tour course with a comfortable 12-point cushion, and despite dropping from 46 to 41 points in Round 2, he never looked remotely threatened. The leader's steady 14-15-12 splits across the three six-hole segments told the story of a man in complete control, extending his advantage to 11 points with the halfway point now reached.

The day's real fireworks came from Gregg Williams and John Patterson, who shared round honors with 42 points each—though Williams claimed the gross victory with a stunning 14-vs-par performance. Both players lit up the closing stretch, with Williams matching Jon Baker's explosive 18-point finale to vault from fourth to third. Patterson, who began the round in second place, held his position with a powerful back-nine surge totaling 25 points.

The round's most dramatic subplot belonged to David Mullin, whose tournament unraveled and recovered and unraveled again across 18 holes. A disastrous eight-point opening six left him scrambling, before the field's best middle split (16 points) hauled him back into contention. But an 11-point fade over the final six dropped him two positions to fifth, 20 points off the pace.

### How It Unfolded

**Disaster Strikes Early**

David Mullin's tournament threatening to slip away before he'd barely begun, managing just eight points over the opening six holes—the worst start of anyone in the field. The par-4 4th hole, which proved the round's toughest examination (averaging 2.6 over par, yielding just 1.2 points), was symptomatic of a front six that saw Mullin hemorrhage ground to the leaders.

While Mullin struggled, Baker and Williams both posted solid 14-point starts to set their respective tones. Baker's was the measured beginning of a man protecting a lead; Williams' hinted at the fireworks to come.

**The Fightback**

Mullin's middle six (holes 7-12) provided the round's most compelling narrative. His 16-point haul was the best split of the entire field, dragging him back from the brink and temporarily threatening to turn his round into something special. Baker chose this stretch to assert himself with 15 points, extending his lead while Patterson matched the total to keep pace in second.

Williams, meanwhile, posted a quiet 10 points through the middle third—consolidating rather than attacking, saving his ammunition for the run home.

**Closing Thunder**

The final six holes exploded into life. Jon Baker and Gregg Williams both unleashed 18-point barrages—tied for the best closing split of the round—transforming their respective positions. Baker's surge lifted him from fifth to fourth and consolidated his gross lead at +15, while Williams' pyrotechnics rocketed him up to third place overall, just three points behind Patterson.

Patterson himself was no slouch over the closing stretch, firing 16 points to finish with an impressive 25-point back nine. Baker, comfortable at the top, coasted home with 12 points—enough to maintain complete control without needing to take risks. Mullin, exhausted from his mid-round heroics, could muster only 11 points and watched two players leapfrog him in the standings.

### What's At Stake

**With 2 rounds remaining:**

Alex Baker has built a commanding position in the Stableford competition, but the mathematics remain intriguing. His 11-point advantage over John Patterson means the chaser needs to average 46 points per round while holding Baker to 35 or fewer—a tall order but not impossible given Patterson's 42-point performance today and Baker's drop from 46 to 41. Gregg Williams sits third, 14 points back, requiring 49 points per round if Baker maintains even modest form around 38 points—a steep climb but his closing stretch today (18 points over six holes) shows the firepower exists.

Jon Baker has seized control of the Green Jacket race despite sitting fourth in the Stableford standings. At +15 gross, he leads David Mullin by seven strokes and brother Alex by 19. With two rounds remaining, Mullin needs to average three-to-four strokes better per round while hoping Jon falters—plausible given the volatility we've seen. Gregg Williams' gross performance today (+14) keeps him mathematically alive at 26 strokes back, though he'd need near-perfection combined with a Jon Baker collapse.

The battle for third place in the Trophy race looks wide open, with just four points separating Williams (73) from Mullin (67). More critically, the gaps suggest we could see significant movement at the top if Baker's form dips even marginally across the final two rounds.
