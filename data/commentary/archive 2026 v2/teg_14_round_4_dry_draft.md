# Round 4 — Prince's (Shore / Dunes), 7 November 2021

## Overview
David Mullin completed the double at Sandwich, taking the TEG 14 Trophy by 2 from Alex Baker and the Green Jacket by 7 from Jon Baker. The round of the day belonged to Alex Baker until it didn't: a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 16th turned a live Trophy challenge into a runner-up finish. Jon Baker's quadruple-bogey 8 at the last sealed the Wooden Spoon, despite a four-hole burst on the back nine that had briefly threatened to make a race of it.

## The Round
The story of the day is the 16th green at Prince's, where Alex Baker — three behind, charging hard, the Trophy genuinely live — wrote down a 10 on a par 4. Everything else either led to that hole or reacted to it.

Baker had begun the round eight behind Mullin and announced himself immediately, posting pars at the 1st, 2nd and 3rd for 10 Stableford points across the first three holes. A double bogey 6 at the 4th broke the streak but did nothing to dim the threat. He kept coming. Through 13, 14 and 15 — par, par, par on two par 4s and a par 5 — he banked another 11 points and the gap to Mullin had narrowed to the point where the Trophy was a live question. Then came the 16th. A 10 on a par 4. Six over for the hole, zero points, and with it the tournament.

Mullin had given Baker his opening earlier. The leader's afternoon turned on the 10th, a par 4 he played in 8 for a quadruple bogey and a blank on the card. That single hole was what made Baker's charge through the middle of the back nine matter; without it, the eight-shot overnight cushion would have absorbed everything Baker could throw at it. As it was, Mullin's lead shrank, wobbled, and held — but only because the 16th intervened on his behalf.

Jon Baker produced the round's other notable surge. From the 12th he went birdie (par 5, 4), birdie (par 4, 3), par, par through the 15th — 14 Stableford points across four holes, two under gross. For a stretch it looked as though the Spoon, 13 adrift overnight, might yet be a contest. The 16th had something to say to him as well: a double-bogey 6 ended the run. The 18th finished the job, an 8 on the par 4 for a quadruple bogey to close the round. His gross card was strong enough overall to lift him past Gregg Williams into second on the Green Jacket, but the Stableford total left him bottom of the Trophy.

Gregg Williams was the quiet figure of the day. No headline beat attaches to his round, and the consequence was a slide from second to third on both the Trophy and the Green Jacket, with the Wooden Spoon now only 12 points behind him rather than the comfortable margin it had been.

## Race Results
Mullin takes the TEG 14 Trophy by 2 from Alex Baker — an eight-shot overnight lead reduced almost to nothing by Baker's front-nine and mid-back-nine bursts, then preserved by the 16th. He also wins the Green Jacket by 7, with Jon Baker's gross scoring (the 8 at the last notwithstanding) enough to displace Gregg Williams in second. Jon Baker takes the Wooden Spoon by 12 from Williams; the double at 16 and the quad at 18 confirmed what the overnight 13-point gap had implied.
