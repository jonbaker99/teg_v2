# Mullin's Kent Coast Procession

**TEG 14 — Royal Cinque Ports · Littlestone · Prince's Shore/Dunes**

---

There is a particular silence that follows a 10. Not a groan, not a curse — those come later, in the bar. The hush on the 16th tee box at Prince's, fourth round, Sunday afternoon, was the sound of a competition ending in real time. Alex Baker had walked off the par-4 with a six-over sextuple bogey, nought points, nothing in the column where two or three were urgently required. Across the fairway, David Mullin did not need to do the maths. Nobody did.

That was the moment. To understand why it mattered, we need to wind it back a long way — to a Wednesday on the Deal links, when the lead Mullin would carry untouched to within sniffing distance of the clubhouse was still a small, perfectly ordinary thing.

---

## Round 1 — Deal Me In: Mullin Lays Down a Marker
**Royal Cinque Ports**

TEG's second pilgrimage to Deal, an Open venue with an Open venue's habit of separating the prepared from the romantic. Mullin, on this evidence, had prepared.

He drew level on the Jacket at the 9th and seized the Trophy lead outright at the 16th — an advantage, it should be noted now and remembered later, that he would never relinquish for the rest of the week. The engine room was holes 9 to 12: par, birdie (at the 10th), par, par. Thirteen points. Forty for the round. By the time the cards were signed he was three clear on Stableford and five up on Gross, and the tournament had quietly decided what kind of tournament it was going to be.

Around him, the field was busy reminding itself that links golf is hard. Gregg Williams put a 9 on the card at the par-5 3rd. Jon Baker bogeyed the par-3 4th and tumbled to the foot of the Wooden Spoon table, where — although none of us knew it yet — he had taken out a long lease.

And Alex Baker, the eventual nearly-man, opened his account in the manner he would also close it. A quadruple-bogey 8 at the 11th. A 10 at the par-5 16th. The first ten of the week, at the first 16th of the week. File that away.

---

## Round 2 — Marsh Music: Mullin +41, Lead +9
**Littlestone**

Across to the Romney Marshes, to Purves's championship layout with its fingerprints from Braid and MacKenzie and that particular sense of remoteness Littlestone does so well. TEG's second go at it.

Mullin posted 41. His best round of the week. The Trophy margin bloomed from three to **nine**, the Jacket cushion from five to **twelve**, and the rest of the field was officially playing for placings.

Or, more accurately, Alex Baker was playing for damage limitation and failing. He bled twelve shots across holes 2 to 5 — triple, double, triple, and then a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 5th — before adding a second quad of the day, an 8 at the 8th. Two quads in a round is a particular kind of afternoon. Two quads in a round when the man ahead of you is shooting 41 is the kind of afternoon you'd rather not discuss.

Nine clear. The week, on the face of it, was done.

---

## Round 3 — Cruise Control on the Shore
**Prince's Shore/Dunes**

Down to Sandwich for the first of a Prince's double-header — the second TEG round on this routing. Mullin signed for 36 and the leads barely twitched: Trophy down a point to eight, Jacket unchanged at twelve.

It was not, in truth, a frictionless 36. A quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 8th was the sort of number that, in a tighter race, might have started a conversation. Here it was simply absorbed. Mullin was demonstrably stronger coming home, and holes 15-18 returned thirteen points — par, par, par, and a birdie at the last to put the round to bed.

Alex Baker, meanwhile, contributed his fourth quad of the week, an 8 at the par-4 10th. Jon Baker stayed exactly where he had been since Wednesday afternoon — last — and added an 8 of his own at the 13th for good measure. Gregg Williams strung together nine points across 9-11 before the par-5 12th bit him for a triple.

Eight clear, with eighteen Prince's holes to play. A formality, surely.

---

## Round 4 — The 16th, Again: Alex's Number Comes Up
**Prince's Shore/Dunes**

It very nearly wasn't.

Alex Baker, who had spent three rounds writing 8s and 9s and 10s on his own cards, finally got out of his own way. Holes 1, 2, 3 at Prince's: par, par, par. Ten points. The kind of opening that makes a man at the top of the leaderboard glance sideways at his playing partners.

Mullin then did exactly what Mullin had occasionally done all week, which was to lob a large number into the middle of a perfectly good round — a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 10th. The cushion, which had been nine after Littlestone, was suddenly something less reassuring. And then Alex went again: par, par, par across 13-14-15 for eleven more points. Forget eight clear; we were now in the realm where a finishing flourish from the chaser and a flinch from the leader might genuinely flip it.

And so to the 16th.

It is, of course, a *different* 16th from the one at Royal Cinque Ports. Different course, different par — Prince's a par-4, Deal's a par-5 — different week, different weather, different everything. The only thing the two holes had in common was a number on a card and a man called Alex Baker, who took 10 at both of them. Sextuple bogey. Nought points. The charge did not so much stall as drive itself off a pier.

Mullin played the rest in par and signed for 37. Trophy by two.

Behind them, Jon Baker enjoyed his best afternoon of the week — a 39, built around a fourteen-point salvo through 12-15 (birdie, birdie, par, par) that suggested he'd been keeping it in reserve for a moment when it would change absolutely nothing. He capped it with a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 18th, because of course he did.

---

## How It Was Won (and Lost)

**The Trophy.** Mullin took the lead at Royal Cinque Ports' 16th and held it for the next 64 holes. The round-by-round margins tell the story of a procession that nearly tripped on the last lap: 3, 9, 8, 2. The 41 at Littlestone built the cushion; the 37 at Prince's defended it; Alex Baker's 10 at the 16th in R4 ended the argument. Final: **Mullin 154, Baker 152**.

**The Green Jacket.** Drawn level on Gross at the 9th of the very first round, never headed thereafter. The gross leads ran 5, 12, 12, 7 — the late shrinkage owed to Mullin's own quads at Prince's (the 8th in R3, the 10th in R4), but the buffer was never seriously imperilled. Final: **Mullin +68, seven clear of Jon Baker**.

**The Wooden Spoon.** Jon Baker bogeyed the par-3 4th at Deal on Wednesday morning and sat at the bottom of the standings from that moment until he holed out on Sunday evening — a piece of consistency at least as impressive as Mullin's at the other end. Rounds of 29, 29, 28 and a closing 39 made 125. The 39 was a flourish; the 8 at the 18th was the punctuation. **Jon Baker, Spoon, 125.**

---

A wire-to-wire double, then, for Mullin: Trophy and Jacket, both seized on a single Wednesday in Deal and never given back. Alex Baker will reflect, perhaps over a long winter, that he was two points off a Trophy in a week in which he made twin 10s and four quadruple bogeys. Jon Baker will mull over the par-3 4th at Royal Cinque Ports. Gregg Williams may prefer not to think about the 3rd.

Same coast, same county, same winner from hole 16 of round 1 to hole 16 of round 4. A procession — but only just.
