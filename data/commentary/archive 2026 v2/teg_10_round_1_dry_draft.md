# Round 1 Draft — Boavista

## Overview

David Mullin opened TEG 10 with 49 Stableford points, the second-best round in TEG history to date. John Patterson's 29 was the worst card of the day. Mullin took outright leads in both the Trophy (by 8 over Alex Baker) and the Green Jacket (by 14 over Jon Baker), while Patterson dropped to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon, three adrift of Stuart Neumann.

## The Round

The day's organising fact was Mullin's 49 points off a gross score of +4. He drew level for the Trophy lead at the 6th with a par at the par-3, and drew level for the Green Jacket lead at the same hole. A par-par-par run from the 6th to the 8th (par-3, par-4, par-5) brought 9 points. He drew level for the Trophy lead again at the 10th, then assembled the round's defining stretch from 12 to 14: par at the par-4 12th, birdie at the par-5 13th, birdie at the par-3 14th — 11 points in three holes. He added another 10-point stretch from 16 to 18: birdie at the par-5 16th, par at the 17th, par at the 18th.

Alex Baker finished second on 41. A quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 2nd was an early dent, and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 8th was another. In between he ran off three bogeys at the 4th, 5th and 6th for 9 Stableford points, and at the 11th his par at the par-3 drew him level with Mullin at the top of the Trophy table — a parity that did not survive Mullin's birdie burst on 13 and 14.

Jon Baker posted 37 and finished fourth on Stableford but second in the Jacket race at +18. His par at the par-4 9th took him to outright top of the Trophy table and also drew him level with Mullin for the Gross lead. He went no higher as Mullin pulled away.

Gregg Williams compiled 38 points to sit third in the Trophy, then ran up a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 18th.

Stuart Neumann's card carried a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 3rd before unravelling at the close: quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 15th, double-bogey 7 at the par-5 16th, quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 17th, and a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 18th — 16 shots dropped over the final four holes.

John Patterson dropped to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon race with a bogey at the 4th and stayed there. A quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 6th and a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 8th anchored the front nine; the closing stretch went double-bogey 6, double-bogey 7, triple-bogey 7, quadruple-bogey 8 from 15 to 18 — 11 shots dropped. He finished on 29 points.

## Race Shifts

The Trophy lead changed hands several times on the front nine before Mullin took control. Mullin and Alex Baker drew level at the 6th; Jon Baker took outright lead at the 9th; Mullin drew level again at the 10th; Alex Baker drew level at the 11th. Mullin's birdies at 13 and 14 broke the deadlock and his closing pars left him 8 clear of Alex Baker.

In the Green Jacket, Mullin drew level at the 6th and Jon Baker matched him at the 9th. Mullin's level-par finish at +4 ended the round 14 clear of Jon Baker.

Patterson dropped to last in the Wooden Spoon at the 4th and remained there, finishing 3 behind Neumann.
