# Baker's Coast — Dry Storyline Draft

## Overview
Jon Baker won the Trophy on 158 Stableford points, six clear of Gregg Williams. He also won the Green Jacket at +50 gross, by 18 from Gregg Williams. David Mullin took the Wooden Spoon on 135 points.

## Round 1 — Deal Or No Deal: Baker Pockets Nine at Royal Cinque Ports

TEG's first round in Kent, on a Royal Cinque Ports links that has twice hosted the Open. Jon Baker shot 39 Stableford points (+13 gross) to lead the Trophy by 6 and the Jacket by 9 after one round.

John Patterson set the tone for his week with three quadruple bogeys at Deal: an 8 at the par-4 1st, an 8 at the par-4 10th, and a 9 at the par-5 16th. He added a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 7th. He finished the day at the bottom of the Wooden Spoon table; the Spoon position had already changed hands six times in the round (Patterson, Williams, Mullin, Williams again, Alex Baker, then Patterson again).

## Round 2 — Baker Wobbles, Then Birdies the 18th

At Prince's on the Dunes / Himalayas routing. Jon Baker scored 36 points (+16 gross). Gregg Williams closed the Trophy gap to a single point; the Jacket lead slipped to 7.

Gregg drew level with Jon for the Trophy lead during the round (R2 H15). At the par-4 18th, Jon made birdie (3) to retake the outright lead heading into Littlestone.

John Patterson opened with a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 1st and added another quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 17th. David Mullin made an 8 (quadruple bogey) at the par-4 9th.

## Round 3 — Baker's +8 Buries the Field

At Littlestone on the Romney Marshes. The pivotal day.

On the par-4 1st, Gregg Williams made par to draw level with Jon Baker for the Trophy lead (level, not outright). Jon then shot +8 gross — the second-best Gross round in TEG history to date — for 44 Stableford points. The Trophy lead went back out to 5; the Jacket lead jumped from 7 to 14. Across holes 8 to 11 Jon went par-birdie-par-par (par-4 8th, par-3 9th in 2, par-4 10th, par-4 11th) for 13 points.

John Patterson posted the round of his life on the same day: a personal-best gross +12 for 49 Stableford points, the third-best round in TEG history to date. It gained him almost nothing on the leader.

## Round 4 — Mullin's Ninth Circle at Prince's

At Prince's on the Shore / Dunes routing. Jon Baker closed with 39 points (+13 gross). The Trophy margin finished at 6, the Jacket margin at 18.

The Spoon was settled on the par-4 10th, where David Mullin made a 9 (quintuple bogey). That dropped him from 4th to 5th on the Wooden Spoon table, and he stayed there.

John Patterson made eagle (3) at the par-5 2nd, then later ran up a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 15th. Alex Baker posted a personal-best gross +17.

## How the competitions were decided

**The Trophy (Stableford).** Jon Baker led after every round: by 6 after R1 (39 pts), by 1 after R2 (36 pts), by 5 after R3 (44 pts), by 6 after R4 (39 pts). Three lead changes, all involving Gregg Williams: Gregg drew level at R2 H15; Jon retook the outright lead with a birdie at R2 H18; Gregg drew level again on the par-4 1st at Littlestone in R3. The decisive moment was Jon's 44-point +8 round at Littlestone, which restretched the lead from level to 5. Final margin: 6 points.

**The Green Jacket (Gross).** Jon Baker led from start to finish: +9 after R1, +7 after R2, +14 after R3, +18 after R4. Zero lead changes across the four days. The decisive round was the +8 at Littlestone, which extended the lead from 7 to 14. Final margin: 18 shots over Gregg Williams.

**The Wooden Spoon.** John Patterson was bottom after R1 and R2. Alex Baker was bottom after R3. David Mullin sat 3rd, 3rd, 4th through three rounds. The decisive drop came at the par-4 10th at Prince's Shore / Dunes in R4, where Mullin made 9 and fell from 4th to 5th. He finished on 135 points.

## Players

- **Jon Baker** — Wire-to-wire leader in both Trophy and Jacket. Rounds of 39, 36, 44, 39 for 158 points; gross 13, 16, 8, 13 for +50. The +8 at Littlestone is the second-best Gross round in TEG history to date.
- **Gregg Williams** — Runner-up in both Trophy and Jacket. Closed to within one point after R2 and drew level twice (R2 H15, R3 H1), but did not take the outright lead at any point.
- **John Patterson** — Four quadruple bogeys (R1 1st, R1 10th, R1 16th, R2 1st, R2 17th, R4 15th — six in total) and a quintuple bogey (R1 7th). Also an eagle at the par-5 2nd in R4, and a personal-best gross +12 / 49 Stableford points at Littlestone, the third-best round in TEG history to date.
- **Alex Baker** — Briefly bottom of the Spoon table at the end of R3. Personal-best gross +17 in R4 at Prince's Shore / Dunes.
- **David Mullin** — 3rd, 3rd, 4th on the Trophy after three rounds. A quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 10th in R4 dropped him to the bottom, and he stayed there. Finished on 135 points and took the Spoon.
