A scorecard, then. Boavista, Sunday afternoon, Atlantic light coming in sideways off the cliffs. Alex Baker's name at the top, and underneath it the number 51. Fifty-one Stableford points. The best round TEG has ever recorded, signed for in plain blue ink as if it were a tax return.

To understand how a man arrives at 51 you have to rewind about twenty-four hours, to that same coastline, to the clubhouse, and to David Mullin walking off the 18th green at Boavista on Saturday having just turned in the second-best round in TEG history. At the time, this was the story. At the time, Mullin's 49 was supposed to be the high-water mark of the week — possibly of the decade. It had stood for, in the end, a little under seventy-two hours.

This was TEG's fourth visit to the Algarve and its first to Palmares, where Robert Trent Jones Jr's two new combinations — Praia/Alvor and Alvor/Lagos — were rolled out for inspection between Boavista bookends. The Trophy went to Baker by eleven. The Green Jacket went to Mullin by twelve. The Wooden Spoon went to John Patterson by a distance that requires its own section.

## Round 1 — Mullin Lands the Second Punch in TEG History {.round1 .round}

Boavista, the 11th TEG round at the venue, and Mullin essentially decided the Green Jacket before lunch on Saturday. Forty-nine Stableford points at +4 gross, which at the time of writing — that is, at the time of being set down — was the second-best round in TEG history. The damage was concentrated: eleven points across holes 12 to 14, the par-5 13th yielding a birdie, the par-3 14th producing another. By the close he held the Trophy lead by eight and the Jacket lead by fourteen. The only lead change in the Jacket all week was a brief, ceremonial wobble — Mullin pulled level at the 6th and Jon Baker nosed outright in front at the 9th — before order was restored and never disturbed again.

Second, improbably, was Alex Baker on 41 points. Improbably because his card included a quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 2nd and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 8th, two holes that between them would normally suggest a man wandering back to the car park rather than sitting eight off the lead.

The bottom of the Trophy, meanwhile, was decided with the speed of a guillotine. John Patterson dropped to last at the 4th hole and would not surface above that line at any point in the remaining 68 holes of the tournament. He marked his scorecard with a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 8th and a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 18th and signed for 29.

Stuart Neumann's round, by contrast, was perfectly fine until it wasn't. From the 15th to the 18th he shed sixteen shots to par, the closing flourish a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 18th. Remember the 18th, and the 10. They are about to come round again.

## Round 2 — Palmares Debuts; Two Personal Bests, Two Tens {.round2 .round}

New ground. Praia/Alvor, the Palmares combination making its TEG debut, and the scoring took on a slightly hysterical quality almost immediately. Alex Baker carded a personal-best 45. Gregg Williams carded a personal-best 48. Mullin's eight-point cushion was, by close of play, a five-point cushion, and the Trophy had quietly stopped being a procession.

Williams's round was built on an 18-point burst across the 8th to the 12th — par, par, birdie at the par-4 10th, par, bogey — the sort of stretch that, in retrospect, was the moment the chase became real. Jon Baker chipped in with six consecutive pars from the 5th to the 10th. Mullin himself, refusing to be hurried, posted five straight pars from the 4th and stayed within touching distance of his Boavista benchmark.

John Patterson opened his round with a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 1st. He recovered, in the technical sense of going on to make further strokes, and signed for 35.

Stuart Neumann, asked to describe his round in a single phrase, would presumably nominate "holes 13 to 15". A quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 13th, a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 14th, a triple-bogey 7 at the par-4 15th — thirteen shots to par in three holes. He then closed with another sextuple-bogey 10 at the 18th. A different venue, a different finishing hole — the one at Palmares this time — but the same number on the scorecard. The habit was now a motif.

## Round 3 — Baker Takes Charge at the 6th {.round3 .round}

Palmares again, this time the Alvor/Lagos combination, and the round in which the Trophy actually changed hands. Baker posted his third personal best in a row, 47 points, and at the par-4 6th a routine par lifted him into outright control of the leaderboard for the first time in the tournament. Mullin nicked it back at the 7th — briefly, almost out of politeness — but the direction of travel was now set.

At the par-5 13th the duel turned into a small civil war. Baker ran up a quintuple-bogey 10. Williams went outright into the lead at the very same hole. Williams then bogeyed the 14th, 15th and 16th, stalling himself before stopping the bleed with a birdie at the par-5 17th. Baker, in the meantime, had reclaimed at least a share of the lead at the 15th. Mullin, refusing to attend his own funeral, signed off with eleven points across the last three holes proper — a birdie at the par-5 15th, par, birdie at the par-5 17th — and so the day ended with Baker eleven clear in the Trophy and Mullin still ten clear in the Jacket.

And then there was Patterson, who between the 11th and the 15th holes of Palmares' Lagos nine shed nineteen shots to par. Triple at the par-4 11th. Quintuple at the par-3 12th. Triple at the par-5 13th. Quadruple at the par-3 14th. Quadruple at the par-5 15th. He had earlier carded a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 8th, which had until then been considered the round's headline event. Twenty-seven points. The Spoon, by now, was less a contest than a piece of bookkeeping.

## Round 4 — Fifty-One: Baker Rewrites the Record {.round4 .round}

Back to Boavista, the 12th TEG round at the venue, Atlantic on the right, history quietly waiting in the wings.

Williams, refusing to let go, drew level for the Trophy lead at the par-4 5th with a birdie. It was the last moment of the week in which the destination of the Trophy could be sensibly described as open.

What followed, across holes 5 to 10, was 21 Stableford points from Alex Baker. The list reads bogey, par, par, bogey, par, par — which on a Boavista back-of-the-card looks unremarkable, the work of an afternoon, until you remember it was happening inside the best round TEG has ever recorded. He signed for 51 points at +17 gross. Three personal bests in a row — 45, 47, 51 — each one rolling the previous one under the carpet, the last of them the new TEG benchmark.

Mullin, going about his own business in his own way, closed at +13 gross at Boavista to seal the Green Jacket at +51 total, twelve clear of Jon Baker. Wire to wire, lead held after every round (by 14, 13, 10 and 12), the only blips back in the opening nine of the first morning.

John Patterson signed for 21, his worst TEG round to date, the centrepiece a sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 8th — the fourth time in four rounds that Boavista's or Palmares' 8th had produced a number on his scorecard that started with a 1. He finished the week on 112. The Spoon, formalised.

## The men, in brief

- **Alex Baker** — 41, 45, 47, 51. Eight back after a scrappy opener that included a 7 and a 9, then three consecutive personal bests, the last of them the round of TEG's life. Trophy by eleven, and the only signature on the record book that matters this week.
- **David Mullin** — The 49 at Boavista was, for one long evening, the best round any of them had ever seen. The Jacket led after every round and was never sensibly in doubt. He walked off with the green coat while watching someone else walk off with everything else.
- **Gregg Williams** — A personal-best 48 at Palmares, an outright Trophy lead at R3's 13th, and level pegging again at R4's 5th. Beaten in the end by a number nobody in TEG had ever posted before. A proud runner-up at 173.
- **Jon Baker** — Held the Trophy lead, alone, for approximately one hole at R1's 9th. Settled instead into the role of Jacket runner-up: six straight pars from the 5th to the 10th at Palmares in R2 was the foundation, and twelve back of Mullin was the destination.
- **John Patterson** — Bottom of the Trophy from the 4th hole of the tournament to the last putt. Tens at Boavista's 8th, Palmares' 1st and Palmares' 8th; an 11 at Boavista's 8th to sign off; a nineteen-shot bleed across five holes at Lagos; and a closing 21, the worst round of his TEG career. Spoon collected without ceremony.
- **Stuart Neumann** — Specialist subject: the closing holes. Sixteen shots dropped across R1's 15th to 18th, ending with a 10 at the finishing hole. Thirteen shots dropped across R2's 13th to 15th, followed by another 10 at the 18th. Two different 18ths, two different venues, the same number written down twice.
