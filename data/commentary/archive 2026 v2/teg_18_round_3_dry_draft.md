# Catalonia Reshuffle: Williams Seizes the Jacket as Jon Baker Slides to the Spoon

## Overview

Gregg Williams posted the round of the day on the Stadium Course, building a comfortable Green Jacket lead from a serene opening stretch. Jon Baker had the worst of it, a 9 at the 4th and four straight doubles to finish dragging him from Jacket leader to bottom of the Spoon race. Alex Baker survived a five-hole bleed in the middle to nudge his Trophy lead from 11 to 12, and the Jacket leadership changed hands at the 2nd.

## The Round

**Gregg Williams** set the tone early. After the 1st he played par-par-par-par from the 2nd through the 5th — 12 Stableford points in four holes — and the par at the 2nd was enough to take the Green Jacket lead outright from Jon Baker. The run ended with a double bogey 6 at the par-4 6th, but the platform was already laid, and by the close he sat eight clear of Mullin in the Gross race.

**Jon Baker** went the other way. He began the day two clear in the Jacket; a triple-bogey 7 at the par-4 2nd handed the lead to Williams and simultaneously shoved him to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon race. A quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 4th followed. The closing four holes were uniformly grim: double bogey 7 at the par-5 15th, double bogey 5 at the par-3 16th, double bogey 6 at the 17th and double bogey 6 at the 18th — eight shots dropped over the final stretch.

**Alex Baker**'s round split into three. A quiet front gave way to a brutal middle: triple-bogey 7 at the 6th, double-bogey 7 at the par-5 7th, double-bogey 5 at the par-3 8th, double-bogey 6 at the 9th and triple-bogey 7 at the par-4 10th — 12 shots gone in five holes. He answered with 21 Stableford points across holes 11 to 16 (par, bogey, par, par, bogey, bogey), which steadied everything just in time.

**David Mullin** was the most consistent man on the course. He opened with three pars for 9 points across holes 1-3, and added another strong run from the 8th — par, bogey, birdie at the par-4 10th — for 10 points across holes 8-10. That was enough to climb out of the Spoon basement and slot in as runner-up in the Jacket.

**John Patterson** had no headline moments either way. A flat round, but enough to hold second place in the Trophy as the players around him swung wildly.

## Race Shifts

The Green Jacket changed hands at the 2nd hole and never went back: Williams's two-shot deficit became an eight-shot lead by sunset, with Mullin moving up to runner-up and Jon Baker tumbling to third. The Wooden Spoon also changed occupant — Mullin started the round two adrift but climbed out, while Jon Baker's collapse left him 14 points adrift of safety. In the Trophy, Alex Baker's lead over Patterson stretched by one to 12, and Williams's strong round lifted him to third.
