# Round 2 Draft — Palmares (Praia / Alvor)

## Overview
Gregg Williams posted the round of the day with a personal-best 48 points (gross +14); Stuart Neumann's 37 included two double-figure holes and was the worst points return outside John Patterson's 35. David Mullin's 42-point round held both the Trophy and the Green Jacket leads, although the Trophy gap to second narrowed from 8 to 5 and Patterson's Wooden Spoon deficit grew from 3 to 5.

## The Round

**David Mullin** anchored his round on a five-hole par streak from the 4th to the 8th — par at the par-5 4th, the par-3 5th, the par-4 6th, the par-4 7th and the par-3 8th — worth 15 Stableford points. The run ended with a double-bogey 7 at the par-5 9th. He finished on 42 points, gross +15, enough to keep both leads.

**Alex Baker** posted a personal-best 45 points, gross +23. He opened bogey-bogey-par across the 1st to 3rd for 9 points, then added 10 points across the 5th to 7th (bogey, bogey, par). The middle of the round bit back: double bogeys at the 12th (par-4), 13th (par-5) and 14th (par-4) cost six shots in three holes.

**Gregg Williams** carded the day's best round, a 48-point personal best at gross +14. The engine was an 18-point burst from the 8th to the 12th — par at the par-3 8th, par at the par-5 9th, birdie at the par-4 10th, par at the par-3 11th and bogey at the par-4 12th. He closed with 12 points across the last four (par, par, bogey at the par-5 17th, bogey at the par-4 18th).

**Jon Baker** played the round's best gross golf at +10 for 45 points. He started 10 points across holes 1-3 (par, birdie at the par-5 2nd, par), then dropped a double bogey 7 at the par-5 4th. From the 5th he reeled off six straight pars through the 10th — the par-3 5th, par-4 6th, par-4 7th, par-3 8th, par-5 9th and par-4 10th — worth 18 points.

**Stuart Neumann** scored 37 in the round. Around the turn he banked 14 points across the 9th to 12th: par at the par-5 9th, bogey at the par-4 10th, par at the par-3 11th, par at the par-4 12th. He then bled 13 shots over the next three holes — a 10 (quintuple bogey) at the par-5 13th, a 9 (quintuple bogey) at the par-4 14th and a 7 (triple bogey) at the par-4 15th, all for zero points. He signed off with a 10 (sextuple bogey) at the par-4 18th.

**John Patterson** opened with a 10 (sextuple bogey) at the par-4 1st for no points. He finished the round on 35 points, the lowest return of the day.

## Race Shifts

The Trophy lead narrowed from 8 to 5 despite Mullin's 42-point round: Alex Baker (45) and Gregg Williams (48) both out-scored him, and Williams' personal best lifted him level with Alex Baker on 86. Jon Baker's 45 brought him to 82, four behind that pair. The Green Jacket gap moved from 14 to 13; Jon Baker (+10 gross) remained the nearest challenger. In the Wooden Spoon, Patterson's 35 against Neumann's 37 stretched the gap from 3 to 5.
