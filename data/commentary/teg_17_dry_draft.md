# The Coronation at Óbidos — Dry Storyline Draft

## Overview

Jon Baker won the Trophy on 166 Stableford points, 18 clear of runner-up David Mullin, the 9th-best Trophy total of all time. He also won the Green Jacket at +67 gross, 13 clear of Mullin. Gregg Williams took the Wooden Spoon on 123 points.

## Round 1 — A Single Point Clear at Praia

Saturday, Praia D'El Rey (Cabell Robinson), the 5th TEG round at this venue. John Patterson finished the day one point clear on the Trophy despite a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 1st (par 4) and another 8 at the 6th (par 4); his back nine was steadied by a recovery that stopped a bogey-or-worse run with a birdie at the 12th (par 5). Gregg Williams led the Green Jacket on a gross 93, drawing level at H17; his round included a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 5th (SI 2). David Mullin's back-nine surge produced 16 points across holes 12–16, including a birdie at the 13th and pars at 12, 14 and 16. Alex Baker set a new Praia D'El Rey course-worst with 114 gross, exceeding the prior worst of 110 across 24 prior visits, fuelled by a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 5th (SI 2), a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 4th and a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 18th. Lead changes on the Trophy were routine: Jon Baker took it outright at H3, Patterson regained it outright at H16.

## Round 2 — Two Course Records at the Cliffs, In Both Directions

Sunday, West Cliffs (Cynthia Dye), the 2nd TEG round at this venue. David Mullin shot 82 gross — a new course record, beating the prior mark of 88 across 6 prior visits — including a 14-point burst across holes 7–10 (bogey, par, birdie, birdie). At the other end, John Patterson posted 116 gross, a new course-worst exceeding the prior 115; his round included a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 3rd (SI 1), and a cold stretch of 27 shots dropped across holes 8–15 (double, triple, triple, triple, double, quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 13th, triple, sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 15th). Jon Baker took the outright Trophy lead at H1 and held it for the remaining 53 holes of the tournament; his round was anchored by a 14-point stretch across holes 2–5 (par, par at the SI 1 3rd, birdie, par). Mullin drew level on the Jacket at H18 to take it into R3. Alex Baker posted 105 gross — a personal best at the venue, 5 better than TEG 15 — featuring a 16-point stretch across holes 4–8 and a recovery birdie at the par-3 12th (SI 18, the easiest hole) after quadruple-bogey 8s at the 10th and 11th. Williams's round included a recovery birdie at the par-3 5th breaking a bogey-or-worse run. Patterson dropped to the bottom of the Spoon race at H10.

## Round 3 — Jon Baker Rewrites Seve's Course

Monday, Royal Óbidos (Seve Ballesteros), the 4th TEG round at this venue. Jon Baker shot 86 gross — a new course record, beating the prior mark of 87 across 18 prior visits. He piled up 20 Stableford points across holes 11–16: birdie at the par-5 11th, bogey at the par-4 12th (SI 2), birdie at the par-3 13th (SI 18, the easiest hole), and pars at 14, 15 and 16. On the Green Jacket, Jon Baker drew level at H2; Mullin took it outright at H6; Jon Baker drew level again at H7 and pulled clear. He led the Jacket by 8 at round's end. Mullin's R2 lead lasted six holes of R3.

At the bottom, Gregg Williams shot 26 Stableford points. He dropped 14 shots across holes 12–18 — seven consecutive double bogeys at holes 12 (par 4), 13 (par 3, SI 18), 14 (par 4), 15 (par 3), 16 (par 4), 17 (par 4) and 18 (par 5). He dropped outright to the bottom of the Spoon race at H14 and stayed there for the remaining 22 holes. Earlier in the round he had a recovery birdie at the par-5 11th after a bogey-or-worse run, and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 5th. Alex Baker bled 15 shots across holes 12–16 (double, double, sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 14th, double, triple) and added a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 18th. Patterson's round included a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 11th and a 13-point stretch across holes 12–15.

## Round 4 — Procession at Praia

Tuesday, Praia D'El Rey, the 6th TEG round at this venue. The 11-point Trophy lead and 8-shot Jacket lead were never threatened. Jon Baker ran up a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th and another quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 17th (SI 1, the hardest hole) and still won both prizes; he had earlier stopped a bogey-or-worse opening with a birdie at the par-4 4th. John Patterson signed off with a sextuple-bogey 9 at the par-3 14th and a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 15th (SI 3, one of the hardest), plus quadruple-bogey 8s at the 1st and 18th. Alex Baker dropped 25 shots across holes 5–13 (double, double, triple, triple, double, double, triple, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 12th, quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 13th) but the Spoon was already settled. Williams ran up a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 1st.

## How the Competitions Were Decided

**Trophy.** Patterson led by 1 after R1. Jon Baker seized the outright lead at R2 H1 at West Cliffs and was never headed across the next 56 holes. The cushion went from 2 after R2 to 11 after R3 (built largely on the 20-point burst across holes 11–16 at Royal Óbidos) to 18 at the close. Final: Jon Baker 166, Mullin 148.

**Green Jacket.** Five lead changes in the first 42 holes. Williams led after R1 by 3. Mullin took it on his 82 course record at West Cliffs and led after R2 by 3. In R3 at Royal Óbidos, Jon Baker drew level at H2, Mullin went outright at H6, Jon Baker drew level again at H7 and pulled clear with the course-record 86. He led by 8 after R3, by 13 at the close. Final: Jon Baker +67, Mullin +80.

**Wooden Spoon.** Alex Baker sat bottom after R1, cushioned by the sextuple-bogey 10 at the 5th and the quintuple 9 at the 18th. Patterson took the basement at R2 H10 after the 27-shot bleed across holes 8–15. Williams dropped outright to the bottom at R3 H14 during the 14-shot bleed across holes 12–18 and never climbed off. Final: Williams 123 points.

## Players

- **Jon Baker** — Three straight TEGs finishing 4th on the Trophy. Won both Trophy (166, by 18) and Green Jacket (+67, by 13) here; new course records at Royal Óbidos (86, beating his own 89 from TEG 9) and at West Cliffs (86, 15 better than TEG 15). Third Jacket; 9th-best Trophy total of all time.
- **David Mullin** — Runner-up in both Trophy and Jacket. His 82 at West Cliffs was the round of the week and set a new course record (12 shots better than TEG 15), but his Jacket lead lasted from R2 H18 to R3 H6.
- **Gregg Williams** — Arrived as reigning Green Jacket holder on back-to-back Jacket wins and a four-time Trophy winner. Led the Jacket after R1, then collapsed at Royal Óbidos with a 26-point round and a 14-shot bleed across holes 12–18, dropping to the Spoon at R3 H14. Second-ever Wooden Spoon on 123 points.
- **John Patterson** — R1 Trophy leader by 1 after eighteen holes. Lost the lead at R2 H1 and never threatened again; posted a new West Cliffs course-worst 116 with a 27-shot bleed across holes 8–15. New PB at Royal Óbidos (94, 6 better than TEG 9).
- **Alex Baker** — Carrying back-to-back Spoons. Set a new Praia D'El Rey course-worst 114 in R1, then posted a personal-best 105 at West Cliffs (5 better than TEG 15) and dropped off the bottom. Williams's R3 collapse passed the cutlery on.
