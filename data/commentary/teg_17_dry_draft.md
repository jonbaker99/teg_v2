# OVERVIEW

Jon Baker won the Trophy on 166 Stableford points, 18 clear of David Mullin — the 9th-best Trophy total in TEG history and his first Trophy. He also took the Green Jacket at +67, 13 shots clear of Mullin, the 5th-best Gross total in TEG annals. Gregg Williams took the Wooden Spoon on 123 points.

# Patterson edges out at Praia, Alex bleeds at the 5th

TEG 17 opened at Praia D'El Rey, Cabell B. Robinson's links-and-parkland mix on the Silver Coast — the 5th TEG round at the venue.

The Trophy lead changed hands twice during the round. Jon Baker took it outright at the 3rd; John Patterson reclaimed it outright at the 16th and held it overnight, posting 39 points to Baker's 38. On the Gross board, Gregg Williams drew level for the lead at the 17th and finished the day at +20, the Jacket leader after R1.

The day's standout hole was Alex Baker's 10 at the par-4 5th — a sextuple bogey (b05).

Bottom of the Trophy board shifted three times: David Mullin at the 2nd, Gregg Williams at the 15th, then Alex Baker at the 18th, who carried the Spoon out of R1.

# West Cliffs eats John Patterson alive

Round 2 moved to Cynthia Dye's West Cliffs links, only the 2nd TEG round there.

Jon Baker took the Trophy lead outright at the 1st — the decisive takeover of the week. He never gave it back. He finished the round on 44 points for a two-point lead.

John Patterson posted his worst-ever TEG Gross round at +44. The damage came in an eight-hole stretch from the 8th to the 15th in which he dropped 27 shots: double bogey at the par-4 8th; triple bogeys at the par-4 9th, par-4 10th and par-4 11th; double at the par-3 12th; a 10 at the par-5 13th (quintuple bogey, b12); triple at the par-4 14th; and an 11 at the par-5 15th (sextuple bogey, b06).

David Mullin drew level for the Gross lead at the 18th and led the Jacket overnight.

The Spoon slot moved again: Gregg Williams at the 2nd, then John Patterson at the 10th, who carried the Spoon out of R2.

# Baker buries it at Seve's place

Round 3 was at Royal Óbidos, Seve Ballesteros' final design and the 4th TEG visit.

The Gross lead flipped three times in the front nine. Jon Baker drew level at the 2nd, David Mullin moved outright in front at the par-3 6th with a par (b17), and Jon Baker drew level again at the 7th. He pulled clear from there.

The decisive Trophy stretch came on the back nine. Across holes 11 to 16, Jon Baker took 20 points (b04): birdie at the par-5 11th, bogey at the par-4 12th, birdie at the par-3 13th, then pars at the par-4 14th, par-3 15th and par-4 16th. He finished on 44 points and led the Trophy by 11.

Alex Baker ran up a 10 at the par-4 14th — a sextuple bogey (b07).

Gregg Williams dropped to last on the Trophy table at the par-4 14th with a double bogey (b39) and stayed there. Over the closing seven holes he leaked 14 shots — seven straight double bogeys at the par-4 12th, par-3 13th, par-4 14th, par-3 15th, par-4 16th, par-4 17th and par-5 18th (b50). His round score was 26.

Jon Baker led the Jacket by 8 and the Trophy by 11 overnight.

# The Baker procession returns to Praia

Round 4 returned to Praia D'El Rey, the 6th TEG round at the venue.

Jon Baker posted 40 points to close out the double, even with two big numbers on the card: a 9 at the par-5 10th (quadruple bogey, b19) and a 9 at the par-5 17th (quadruple bogey, b20).

John Patterson's farewell card included a 10 at the par-4 15th (sextuple bogey, b09).

Gregg Williams' round of 29 confirmed the Spoon at 123 points (b14).

Final margins: Trophy by 18, Jacket by 13.

# HOW THE COMPETITIONS WERE DECIDED

**Trophy.** John Patterson led after R1 by one point following his outright takeover at the 16th of Praia D'El Rey. Jon Baker took the lead outright at the 1st of West Cliffs in R2 — the decisive takeover — and was never headed. Rounds of 38, 44, 44 and 40 turned a one-point R1 deficit into leads of 2, 11 and finally 18. Final: Jon Baker 166, David Mullin runner-up.

**Green Jacket.** Gregg Williams led after R1 at +20. David Mullin drew level at the 18th of West Cliffs in R2 and led overnight. In R3 at Royal Óbidos the lead flipped three times in the front nine: Jon Baker drew level at the 2nd, Mullin took it outright at the 6th (b17), and Jon Baker drew level again at the 7th — the decisive takeover. He led by 8 after R3 and 13 after R4, finishing on +67.

**Wooden Spoon.** The bottom slot changed hands six times. Alex Baker carried it out of R1 (after Mullin at the 2nd and Williams at the 15th had each spent time there). Gregg Williams briefly held it at the 2nd of R2 before John Patterson took it at the 10th and carried it to the end of R2. In R3, Gregg Williams dropped to last at the 14th of Royal Óbidos (b39) and stayed there. A round of 26 in R3 and 29 in R4 confirmed it: 123 points, last.

# PLAYERS

- **Jon Baker** — Trailed by one after R1, took the Trophy lead at R2 H1 and never gave it back; rounds of 38, 44, 44, 40 for 166 points (first Trophy, 9th-best in TEG history) and Gross +67 for the Green Jacket (third, by 13).
- **David Mullin** — Runner-up in both competitions; briefly led the Jacket after R2 and at the 6th of R3, the only player who got near Baker.
- **John Patterson** — Led the Trophy after R1; posted a personal-worst Gross 44 at West Cliffs with a 10 at the par-5 13th and an 11 at the par-5 15th; signed off with a 10 at the par-4 15th in R4.
- **Gregg Williams** — Led the Jacket after R1 at +20; dropped to last on the Trophy at R3 H14 and stayed there after leaking 14 shots over the closing seven holes at Royal Óbidos; finished on 123 points for the Spoon.
- **Alex Baker** — Card littered with double-figure holes: a 10 at the par-4 5th at Praia in R1, a 10 at the par-4 14th at Royal Óbidos in R3, and a 25-shot bleed from holes 5 to 13 in R4; spared the Spoon by Williams' R3 collapse.
