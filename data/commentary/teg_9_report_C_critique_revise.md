# Patterson's Portuguese Procession

There are TEGs that ebb and flow, and there are TEGs settled in a single blistering afternoon. This one — the third visit to the Lisbon Coast, alternating Seve's last-ever drawing-board exercise at Royal Óbidos with Cabell Robinson's dunes-and-fairways number at Praia D'El Rey — was emphatically the latter. John PATTERSON went out on Saturday and posted **49 points**, the second-best round in TEG history. The Trophy was wrapped, ribboned and on the mantelpiece by sundown. David MULLIN, meanwhile, had already taken charge of the Green Jacket on Friday evening and treated the rest of the week as a stroll around the estate. And Stuart NEUMANN's Wooden Spoon? Booked before anyone had ordered a second beer on Friday night.

Three races, three winners by Tuesday lunch, and a great deal of theatre in between for those of us who like our coronations preceded by carnage.

---

## Round 1 — Seve's Last Design Spits Out Five Different Leaders

Royal Óbidos, all water features and Seve-fingerprinted mischief, did what it was built to do: it served chaos for breakfast. The Trophy lead changed hands at **five of the first six holes**. Jon BAKER levelled at the 2nd; MULLIN joined the party at the 3rd; Gregg WILLIAMS — yes, *that* Gregg WILLIAMS — bumbled into a share of the lead at the 4th via, of all things, a double bogey; PATTERSON took it outright at the 5th; Jon BAKER squared it again at the 6th. Nip to the loo between holes two and seven and you'd have missed the entire opening movement.

The carnage wasn't confined to the leaderboard. WILLIAMS opened with a **9 at the 1st**, one way to introduce yourself to a course, and would later add a **9 at the par-3 15th** — a man committed, all week, to inventing new ways of going six over par on a single hole. Alex BAKER produced his now-traditional cocktail of mediocrity and arson, signing for an **11 at the par-4 14th** alongside a quintuple at the 12th. And PATTERSON himself — the eventual champion, remember — coughed up a **10 at the 4th** and a quad at the 15th, the sort of card a man usually folds up and quietly burns.

He didn't. He went round in 38 somehow and sat one off the lead on countback. A powderkeg, smoking gently.

At the gross sharp end, MULLIN was already going about his business with grim efficiency. He took the outright Jacket lead at the 3rd, was briefly joined by Jon BAKER at the 6th, again at the 15th, and then — with the inevitability of a man clearing his throat — **reclaimed it outright at the 18th**. He would not let it go again. Gross +15. Done.

And at the bottom? A brief opening shuffle — WILLIAMS last after the 1st, Alex BAKER after a 9 at the 2nd — before NEUMANN settled into the role at the 11th. He briefly clambered out, then **dropped back for keeps at the par-3 15th**, where he made an 8. The killer was the closing five: **16 shots leaked over 14–18**, just 23 points for the round. The Spoon was, to all intents and purposes, his by Friday tea. The rest of us hadn't noticed because we were too busy watching the front.

**After R1:** Jon BAKER leads the Trophy by one; MULLIN the Jacket by two; NEUMANN props up everything.

---

## Round 2 — Forty-Nine Shades of Patterson

To Praia D'El Rey, for what was supposed to be a tightening contest and turned out to be a one-man recital with the coastal dunes for an orchestra pit.

PATTERSON snatched the outright Trophy lead with a bogey at the **1st** — par-4, no fireworks, just a quiet shifting of the tectonic plates. MULLIN levelled at the 2nd. So far, so Friday.

Then PATTERSON's wheels came off. The Friday powderkeg, apparently doing its work a day late, leaked eight shots in three holes around the turn-towards-trouble. At which point, of all places, he birdied the 7th. And then something genuinely odd happened. He simply stopped making mistakes. Five holes from the **9th to the 13th** yielded **17 points** — bogey, par, par, birdie at the par-5 12th, par. He was level by the 11th and past for good shortly after. The closing four — **15 through 18** — added **14 more**: par, bogey, par, bogey on holes that on most weeks chew people up and spit them seaward.

The total: **49 points**. Gross +14. The second-best round in TEG history. A ten-shot lead.

MULLIN, to his credit, kept his head down — five straight bogeys around the turn, snapped by a birdie at the 10th, gross 14 — and the Jacket lead ballooned to seven. Jon BAKER's day, by contrast, had a hot patch (9 points across 11–13) and then ran into the par-3 14th, where a **quadruple-bogey 7** detonated the round; his recovery birdie at the 17th felt like a man pulling a tablecloth over a broken vase. Alex BAKER, ever the headline split, signed for a personal-best **44** that somehow contained a quad 9 at the 10th and another quad 7 at the 14th. He is not a golfer; he is a weather system.

WILLIAMS, ongoing experiment, recorded a **10 at the par-4 9th**.

**After R2:** PATTERSON +10 on Trophy. MULLIN +7 on Jacket. NEUMANN still anchored, 30 points the round.

---

## Round 3 — Back to Royal Óbidos, Back to Earth

A second visit to Seve's water-feature gauntlet, and a reminder that ten-shot leads in this game are paper, not iron. PATTERSON's morning went sideways early — a double at the 2nd, triples at the par-3 3rd and the 4th — eight shots gone, eight shots that suddenly mattered. Worse to come at the par-4 12th (a quadruple-bogey 8) and the par-5 18th (a 9). He scraped together 34 points and watched his cushion trimmed from ten to three.

Jon BAKER, sensing an opening, came out of the blocks like a man who'd had a stern word with himself in the mirror: **16 points across the opening five** — par, birdie at the 2nd, par, bogey, par. The contest was, briefly, a contest. Then he ran into the par-5 11th and made a 9, the back nine went the way of all flesh, and Alex BAKER mirrored him stride for stride with **an 8-shot bleed across the same trio of 10–12**. Twins, those two, in disaster as in surname.

MULLIN, the supposed metronome, very nearly wasn't. A **quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 10th** is the sort of number that empties a clubhouse bar in sympathy. He shrugged, made his pars, posted gross +22 — his worst round of the week — and somehow his lead held steady at seven. The advantage of playing alongside people who are also having a bad day.

NEUMANN, for the record, found a small patch of sun (9 points across 6–8, 12 across 10–13), still finished bottom, still posted 37.

**After R3:** PATTERSON +3 on Trophy. MULLIN +7 on Jacket. NEUMANN unmoved.

---

## Round 4 — Alex Briefly Levels, Then Alex Being Alex

Back to Praia D'El Rey, with the leaderboards tight enough to be interesting and the principals all still apparently capable of doing something stupid.

MULLIN, just to keep us honest, opened with a **quadruple-bogey 8 at the 1st**, a curious way of nursing a seven-shot Jacket lead but, on reflection, very on-brand: he then made four straight pars (broken by a double at the 5th), strung together **nine points across 7, 8 and 9**, and resumed his procession. Gross +16. Jacket by ten.

PATTERSON looked briefly mortal — a **quintuple-bogey 9 at the 5th** — then began compiling a 47-point masterclass before he'd quite realised what was happening.

The drama, such as it was, came from Alex BAKER, chuntering through another personal-best 44 in the usual mix of brilliance and arson (quad at the 6th, recovery birdie at the par-3 8th). At the **par-3 14th**, of all the unlikely tee-boxes, he made a double bogey — and somehow that double bogey was enough to draw him **level with PATTERSON on Stableford**. The Trophy race had, for one shimmering moment, a pulse.

It lasted exactly one hole. The next four went **8, 7, 7 and a closing flourish** — **11 shots gone across 14–17**, a quadruple at the 15th the centrepiece. The wheels did not so much come off as detach themselves and roll into the Atlantic. He still finished runner-up for the Trophy, which tells you all you need to know about everyone else's week.

Jon BAKER added a quad 9 at the 17th to round out a frustrating finish. WILLIAMS, asked one final time to demonstrate his commitment to going six over par, obliged with an **11 at the par-5 7th**. And NEUMANN — bless him — produced the closing flourish of the week: **16 points across holes 11 to 15**, a birdie at the 13th in the mix, a small flag planted on the summit of a very long week. He still bled 8 shots over the closing three and finished bottom. But he finished, and he finished swinging.

---

## The Reckoning

- **Trophy:** **John PATTERSON, 168 points** — the highest total in TEG history — by six from Alex BAKER. Settled, in truth, between the 9th and the 13th on Saturday afternoon.
- **Green Jacket:** **David MULLIN**, by ten from Jon BAKER. Settled on the 18th green at Royal Óbidos on Friday and never seriously in doubt thereafter.
- **Wooden Spoon:** **Stuart NEUMANN**. Settled on the par-3 15th on Friday afternoon, formalised by sundown.

Three trophies, three winners, one record book rewritten and one piece of TEG history almost — almost — equalled. Seve would, one suspects, have approved of the chaos at his place; Cabell Robinson can take quiet pride that his coast hosted the second-greatest round any of us has ever played. Onwards.
