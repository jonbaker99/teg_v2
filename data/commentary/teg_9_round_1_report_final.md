# Seve's Last Laugh

Royal Óbidos is the final course Seve Ballesteros designed before he died, and on the evidence of day one he wasn't in a forgiving mood when he sketched it. The aggregate damage across six cards is genuinely impressive: a 10, three 11-no-wait-just-the-one-11, four separate 9s, a sextuple at a par-3, and enough quintuples to keep a statistician in business for a week. The par-4 4th, the par-4 12th and the par-3 15th did the bulk of the carving, but no hole on the property went entirely unmolested. In that context the leaderboard reads less like a list of the day's best golf and more like a casualty report sorted by least-injured first. Jon Baker (39) edges Mullin and Patterson (38 apiece) not because he played the round of his life but because the men chasing him kept handing back fistfuls of shots. This was attrition golf on Seve's final canvas, and the man laughed loudest from beyond.

**Jon Baker** is the survivor-in-chief, his 39 Stableford points the leading total despite a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 18th that ensured he walked off the final green with the look of a man who'd just been mugged in his own driveway. The Trophy lead had bounced in and out of his pocket all afternoon — level at the 2nd with a par, level again at the 6th, which also drew him alongside Mullin on Gross — and at +17 he sits two off the Jacket pace as well. Two leaderboards, both within touching distance, both within blowing-up range: Baker's performance was a study in not-quite-comfortable competence, and the 9 at the last means the cushion he carries into Round 2 feels rather more provisional than the single-point margin suggests.

**David Mullin** produced the day's most composed card, and the gross numbers say so plainly: +15, two clear of Baker, and the Green Jacket lead in his pocket. He took it with a par at the par-3 3rd, lost it briefly when Baker drew level at the 6th, and reclaimed it for keeps by parring the 18th. The Trophy, though, slipped through his fingers at exactly the moment it looked most his. Pars at 13, 14 and 15 — three holes of serene, this-is-actually-quite-easy golf — set up what should have been a coronation, and instead he made double-bogey 6 at the par-4 16th. One slip, one stroke, and the outright Trophy lead with it. He sits tied for second on 38, a point back, and will reflect that the difference between leading and chasing this evening was a single hole on Seve's final stretch.

**John Patterson** is the round's great curiosity. He made 10 at the par-4 4th — a sextuple bogey, the kind of number that traditionally ends not just rounds but weekends — and added a quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 15th for good measure. And yet he is tied for second. Tied. For. Second. The arithmetic only works if the rest of the scorecard was, by Royal Óbidos standards, very serious golf indeed, and so it was: he held the outright Trophy lead briefly at the 5th with a par, and the 38 points on the bottom line bears no obvious scar of the calamity in the middle. He sits +28 on Gross, well back of Mullin and Baker but very much in the Jacket conversation should either of them slip.

**Alex Baker** had the kind of afternoon that, in years to come, he will describe in fewer and fewer words. A quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 2nd — which briefly deposited him at the bottom of the Spoon race — was merely the appetiser. A quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 12th was the main course. And the dessert, served at the par-4 14th, was the round's outright worst single score: a septuple-bogey 11. Three nines and an eleven, on three different holes, on the opening morning. The 32 Stableford points he salvaged from the wreckage are, frankly, a triumph of bloody-mindedness; the +43 Gross total props up the Jacket standings and tells the unvarnished truth.

**Gregg Williams** began and ended his round with disaster and threw another one in the middle for symmetry. A quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 1st was a hell of a way to introduce himself to Seve's creation. He recovered well enough to draw level for the Trophy lead at the 4th — with, of all things, a double-bogey 6, which tells you everything you need to know about how the field was scoring on that hole — before the par-3 15th took a sextuple-bogey 9 off him (a six-over on a par-3 being one of those scores that requires a moment's quiet contemplation) and the par-5 18th levied a final quadruple-bogey 9 on the way home. 31 points, fifth, and a sense that the round contained roughly three good golfers' worth of holes interleaved with three very poor ones.

**Stuart Neumann** went out in respectable order and came back in something close to freefall. The front nine and the back nine are separated by eleven Stableford points — eleven — and the inflection point can be located with forensic precision. A quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 4th was the first crack, on a hole that did damage to several. Then, across holes 10 to 12, he leaked ten shots in a triple-triple-quadruple sequence that handed him the Spoon position at the 11th. The par-3 15th — that hole again — took a quintuple-bogey 8 off him and the Spoon was his, decisively, for the night. Across the closing five holes he dropped sixteen shots: triple, quintuple, triple, double, triple. 3-5-3-2-3, in over-par terms, totalling exactly that. 23 points, +42 Gross, bottom of all three boards, eight clear of the rest at the wrong end. Seve, one suspects, would have raised a glass.

The Trophy lead bounced through four sets of hands on the front nine — Patterson at the 5th, Jon Baker level at the 2nd and 6th, Mullin level at the 3rd, Williams level at the 4th — before Baker's 39 held by a single point. The Green Jacket toggled between Mullin and Baker and ended with Mullin two ahead. And the Spoon, having briefly belonged to Alex Baker after his 9 at the 2nd, found a new owner at Neumann's 11th and was nailed down at Neumann's 15th. With three rounds remaining, the top three are within a point and a half on the Trophy, the Jacket lead is a flimsy two, and the Spoon gap — eight points — is the only number on the board that looks remotely settled.
