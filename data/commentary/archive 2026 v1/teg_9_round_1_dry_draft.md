# Round 1 Draft — Royal Óbidos

## OVERVIEW

Jon Baker's 39 Stableford points led the Trophy by a single point from David Mullin and John Patterson, both on 38. Stuart Neumann's 23 points was the day's worst, leaving him bottom of all three boards. Mullin took the Green Jacket lead on +15, two clear of Baker on +17.

## THE ROUND

Royal Óbidos — Seve Ballesteros's final design, on its second TEG visit — produced blow-ups across the field. The par-4 4th, the par-4 12th and the par-3 15th did most of the damage.

**Jon Baker** posted the round's leading Stableford total of 39, despite a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 18th. He drew level for the Trophy lead at the 2nd with a par, and again at the 6th, where he also drew level for the Green Jacket. He finished +17 gross, two adrift of Mullin.

**David Mullin** posted 38 points and led the Green Jacket on +15. He took the Gross lead with a par at the par-3 3rd, drew level again at the 15th and went back in front by parring the 18th. His one stumble came at the par-4 16th: pars at 13, 14 and 15 were followed by a double-bogey 6, which broke the run and ultimately cost him the outright Trophy lead.

**John Patterson** finished tied for second on 38 points despite the round's most extreme single hole among the leaders — a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 4th — and a quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 15th. He briefly held the outright Trophy lead at the 5th with a par. He sits +28 gross.

**Alex Baker** signed for 32 points and propped up the Gross standings on +43. His card included a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 2nd (which dropped him to the bottom of the Spoon race at that point), a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 12th, and the round's worst single score — a septuple-bogey 11 at the par-4 14th.

**Gregg Williams** finished on 31 points. He opened with a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 1st, briefly drew level for the Trophy lead at the 4th with a double-bogey 6, then took a sextuple-bogey 9 at the par-3 15th and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 18th. He sits +35 gross.

**Stuart Neumann** finished bottom on 23 points and +42 gross, with an 11-point front-nine/back-nine split in favour of the front. A quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 4th was the first major mark. He then leaked 10 shots across holes 10-12 (triple, triple, quadruple), taking the Spoon position at the 11th. He took it again, decisively, at the 15th with a quintuple-bogey 8. Across the closing five holes (14-18) he dropped 16 shots: triple, quintuple, triple, double, triple.

## RACE SHIFTS

The Trophy lead passed through four hands on the front nine — Patterson at the 5th, Jon Baker drawing level at the 2nd and 6th, Mullin drawing level at the 3rd, and Williams drawing level at the 4th — before Baker's 39 held by one from Mullin and Patterson on 38. The Green Jacket lead moved between Mullin and Baker: Mullin out front at the 3rd, Baker level at the 6th, Mullin back in front by the 18th, the gap two at the close. The Wooden Spoon position changed three times: Alex Baker at the 2nd, then Neumann at the 11th and again at the 15th, ending the round eight points adrift of the field.
