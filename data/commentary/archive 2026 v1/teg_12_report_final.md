# Reign in Spain: Patterson's Catalan Coronation

Saturday lunchtime at PGA Catalunya, the 4th green of the Stadium course. Gregg Williams is signing for a nine. John Patterson, who has just made a serviceable bogey, is signing for the outright lead of a tournament he will not surrender for the remaining 32 holes. Neither man says very much. There is nothing, really, to say. The Trophy has just changed hands, the Green Jacket has just changed hands at the same green a group earlier, and the only person at Camiral who does not yet know the week is decided is the man still tallying his card.

It is worth pausing here, on this small Catalan plateau, before flashing back to how we got to it — because the journey involves Patterson taking a 12 at a par 4 on Tuesday morning and somehow standing on this 4th green forty-eight hours later, one shot from the front, about to claim a tournament by ten.

## Round 1 — Williams Sets the Tone, Patterson Sets Fire to the 4th

The Tour course at Camiral is the undercard. Two days of it, then the Stadium for the weekend; everyone knew the running order, nobody quite believed how cleanly it would resolve.

Gregg Williams announced himself with a personal-best gross round of +12, taking a five-shot lead in the Jacket and a one-point cushion in the Trophy. It was the round of a man in form, in command, and in possession of a draw at his shoulder that included Patterson and Neumann generously providing the comic relief.

Patterson's contribution came at the 4th, a par 4, where he made twelve. Eight over on one hole. A TEG par-worst. There is no useful way to describe a 12 on a par 4 except to say it happened, was written down, and was signed for. He responded with bogey-par-par-par-par from the 7th — 19 Stableford points across five holes, the kind of stretch that, in another world, would have been the day's headline — before a double bogey at the par-4 12th capped things off. He finished one point behind Williams. Trailing by a single shot after carding a duodecuple-anything is a particular form of grace.

Stuart Neumann, never knowingly understated, posted a 10 at the par-4 13th and then a 12 at the par-5 15th — another TEG par-worst, achieved within the same nine holes as his previous one. Alex Baker, meanwhile, contributed a 7 at the par-3 2nd, an 8 at the 13th and a 10 at the par-5 10th, and slid quietly to the bottom of the Trophy board, where he would remain for some time. Jon Baker, doing nothing showy, was already joint-top of the Gross board. He had not yet said a word.

## Round 2 — Status Quo on the Tour: Williams by One, Again

A second lap of the Tour course did almost nothing. Williams led the Trophy by one and the Jacket by one, exactly as he had the night before, which sounds like stability and was in fact two men quietly trading errors in roughly equal measure.

Patterson made a 10 at the par-5 15th and an 8 at the par-4 3rd, and was still one back. Williams replied with an 8 at the par-4 4th — the first warning shot from a hole that would, on the Stadium two days later, do considerably worse to him — and recovered with a birdie at the par-5 10th after dropping strokes at the 8th and 9th. Both men, and Mullin too, posted twelve-point bursts through the middle of the round; the leaderboard, like a stuck clock, refused to move.

Neumann opened with an 8 at the par-4 1st and then went bogey-bogey-par-birdie for 13 points across the 3rd to the 6th, which is the Neumann week in one sentence. Alex Baker stayed bottom. Nobody yet sensed that this was the calm part.

## Round 3 — Stadium Rock: Patterson Takes Over, Williams Falls Apart

The Stadium course on Saturday is where the tournament happened. All three competitions either turned or were settled, and the front nine alone did most of the heavy lifting.

The 3rd, a par 5, took a 10 off Patterson. Williams parred it and, for the first time in the week, held the outright Trophy lead. It lasted one hole. At the par-4 4th — and this is the green we opened on — Williams made nine, Patterson bogeyed for three points, and the Trophy lead came back the other way for keeps. At the same hole, Jon Baker turned a bogey into two Gross points, which was enough to seize outright control of the Green Jacket from a man who was, by that stage, no longer really in the conversation. Two trophies, one green, one group of holes.

Williams briefly drew level with Patterson on Stableford at the 9th — a tie, not a takeover, and not the same thing — and then walked onto the 13th and made eleven. A septuple bogey on a par 4. Whatever was left of the Trophy contest left with it. Patterson, perhaps sensing the door was now off its hinges, went par-bogey-bogey-birdie-bogey-bogey from the 12th: 19 points across six holes, the birdie at the par-5 15th interrupting what had been threatening to become a collapse. He led the Trophy by eight at the close.

Patterson had also made a 9 at the par-5 7th in the middle of all this, which on any other card would have stopped the narrative dead. On Saturday at the Stadium it barely registered. Neumann managed a 9 of his own at the par-5 15th. Jon Baker steadied a bogey-double-double wobble from the 9th with a birdie at the par-5 12th and went to bed leading the Jacket by five.

## Round 4 — Coronation on the Stadium, Capitulation at the 3rd

Sunday on the Stadium course was a procession at the top and an unscheduled execution at the bottom.

Patterson shot 40 Stableford points, which is the score of a man who has no interest whatsoever in surprises. His lead grew from eight to ten. Across the 3rd to the 6th he went par-bogey-par-bogey for 13 points, which on the front nine of the Stadium is roughly equivalent to a victory lap. He finished on 172 — the fourth-highest Stableford total in TEG history, and his first Trophy.

Jon Baker shot +17 to seal the Jacket by eight at +63, the third-best Green Jacket result on record. Williams's +18 closing round took him to +76 gross for the tournament — also, by way of consolation prizes, a personal best. Runner-up in both, with the second-best gross total of his life: a result that, in any sober assessment, is excellent, and on this evidence felt like being mugged.

And then there was Mullin. He arrived at Sunday's first tee in fifth on the Trophy, comfortably mid-pack, on track to be Green Jacket runner-up, and entirely uninvolved in any Wooden Spoon argument. He played the par-5 3rd in nine. He played the par-4 4th in eight. By the time he walked off the 4th green he had dropped to last on the cumulative Trophy and would stay there for the remaining fourteen holes. A four-hole stretch from the 11th — par, birdie, par, birdie — was worth 14 points and is the kind of run most weeks would be the story. This week it was a man trying to bail out a boat that had already sunk. He finished on 139, holding the Spoon.

Alex Baker, who had been bottom of every individual round for three days and was the universally agreed Spoon recipient by Saturday tea, finished above him. There is no consolation in this, but there is a small administrative dignity.

Stuart Neumann eagled the par-5 15th — the tournament's only eagle — having gone double-bogey through the 13th and 14th. It was the closing line of a week that had previously consisted of a 12, a 10, an 8 and a 9, all on different par-worst exhibits, and it was, in its way, perfect.

## The men, in brief

- **John Patterson** — Made a 12 at a par 4 on Tuesday morning and won the Trophy by ten on Sunday afternoon, which ought to be impossible and is, in fact, the fourth-best Stableford total ever posted in TEG. His first Trophy. The Stadium course was where he took it and the Stadium course was where he kept it.

- **Gregg Williams** — Led for two rounds on the back of the gross round of his life, posted the gross total of his life, and finished runner-up in both main events. The Stadium 4th took nine off him and the Stadium 13th took eleven, and that was the week.

- **Jon Baker** — Joint-top of the Gross board from hole one of the tournament and outright top from the 4th hole of Round 3 onwards. A patient, almost bureaucratic Green Jacket, won by eight at +63 — the third-best Jacket result on record. He said little. He did not need to.

- **David Mullin** — On course for a Green Jacket runner-up finish, undone by 9-8 at the Stadium 3rd and 4th on Sunday morning. Took the Wooden Spoon with fifteen holes still to play, despite a 14-point stretch through the middle of the back nine that was, on any reasonable view, the work of a much better player having a much worse week.

- **Stuart Neumann** — A walking exhibit of the par-worst column: 12 at the Tour 15th, 10 at the Tour 13th, 8 at the Tour 1st, 9 at the Stadium 15th. Redeemed himself in the final hour with the tournament's only eagle, at the same 15th that had taken nine off him the day before. A career in microcosm.

- **Alex Baker** — Bottom of every individual round through Saturday night, bottom of cumulative Trophy at the close of each, and the agreed-upon Spoon by midweek. Finished above Mullin by virtue of Mullin's Sunday morning. Will take it.
