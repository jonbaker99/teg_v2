# Round 4 — Oitavos Dunes — Dry Draft

## Overview
Gregg Williams converted a five-point overnight Trophy lead into a twelve-point winning margin, while David Mullin overhauled Jon Baker to take the Green Jacket by six on gross. Henry Meller authored the round's worst golf, leaking 22 shots over the opening six holes. Stuart Neumann's Wooden Spoon was confirmed, though the gap to Meller closed from 22 points to 11.

## The Round
Oitavos Dunes, an Arthur Hills links on the Lisbon Coast, was a new course for TEG and punished the field from the opening holes.

Meller's round came apart immediately: a double-bogey 6 at the 1st, a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 2nd, a triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 3rd, a sextuple-bogey 10 at the par-4 4th, a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 5th and a double-bogey 6 at the 6th — 22 shots dropped over the opening six. He added further damage later with doubles at the 11th, 12th and 13th (six dropped across that stretch), and a closing run of double-triple-quad at 16, 17 and 18 for nine more shots gone. He finished fifth in the Trophy.

Mullin's day shaped both races. A bogey 5 at the par-4 2nd nudged him into the outright Green Jacket lead, a position he held the rest of the round. He then ran bogey-bogey-bogey at 8, 9 and 10 before a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 11th. He stopped the bleeding with a birdie 4 at the par-5 13th. Two more bogeys at the 14th and 15th followed, before a second recovery birdie at the par-5 16th.

Williams's round was less smooth than the margin suggests. He took a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 5th, then doubled the 6th, doubled the par-5 7th and tripled the par-5 8th — 11 shots leaked across holes 5-8. He steadied on the back nine and produced his decisive sequence at 12-15: par, bogey, par, par on a stretch of par-3, par-5, par-3, par-3, worth 12 Stableford points and effectively closing out the Trophy.

Jon Baker began the day leading the Jacket by two and second in the Trophy by five. He ended the round overtaken in both — second on gross, six behind Mullin, and third on Stableford.

Alex Baker climbed to Trophy runner-up despite his own damage: a quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 15th and a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 18th. The movement came largely from Jon Baker's slide rather than his own scoring.

Neumann's day included quadruple-bogey 8s at the par-4 5th and the par-4 17th. The Spoon was already long since beyond doubt, and remained so.

## Race Shifts
Williams's overnight five-point Trophy lead over Jon Baker became a twelve-point lead over Alex Baker, with Jon Baker dropping to third and Mullin to fourth. In the Green Jacket, Mullin's bogey at the 2nd took him into an outright gross lead he never surrendered; the two-point overnight deficit to Jon Baker became a six-shot winning margin. The Wooden Spoon was claimed by Neumann; the gap to Meller closed from 22 points to 11, but never threatened to flip.
