# Round 3 Draft — Palmares (Alvor / Lagos)

## Overview

Alex Baker posted the round of the day with 47 Stableford points and drew level with Gregg Williams at the top of the Trophy on 133. David Mullin, who began the round five clear in the Trophy and 13 clear in the Jacket, signed for a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 18th and dropped to third in the Trophy (122) while seeing his Jacket cushion cut to 10. John Patterson's 10 at the 8th, an 8 at the par-3 12th and a five-hole stretch of 19 dropped shots stretched his Wooden Spoon deficit from 5 to 14.

## The Round

Palmares, a Robert Trent Jones Jr. parkland on TEG's first visit to the course, traded the Trophy lead repeatedly through the day.

Alex Baker opened with 10 points across holes 2–4 (bogey-3 at the par-3 2nd, bogey-5 at the par-4 3rd, par-5 at the par-5 4th), and his par at the 4th drew him level at the top of the Trophy. Gregg Williams' par-5 at the same hole also drew him level. Baker then went outright in front with a par at the par-4 6th. David Mullin steadied with a par at the par-3 7th to draw level again. Baker kept the points flowing with three straight bogeys at 9, 10 and 11 — nine more points — and Williams added 9 of his own across 11–13 (bogey-par-bogey).

The 13th, a par-5, then did most of the damage. Williams' bogey-6 took him to the outright Trophy lead. Mullin made triple-bogey 8 there. Alex Baker carded a 10 — quintuple bogey, zero points. Patterson made triple-bogey 8. The hole alone re-set the leaderboard.

Baker drew level again with a bogey-6 at the par-5 15th. Mullin steadied with the round's standout three-hole stretch — birdie-par-birdie at 15, 16 and 17 (4-3-4) for 11 points — and looked set to reclaim ground. Williams stopped a five-hole bogey run with his own birdie at the par-5 17th. Jon Baker quietly contributed three pars across 15–17 for 9 points.

The 18th, a par-4, then took the Trophy lead off Mullin entirely. His 8 — quadruple bogey, zero points — left him on 122 and dropped him to third, with Alex Baker and Williams tied at 133.

Jon Baker's round was the unflashy one: steady through the back nine, the par-par-par at 15–17 trimming Mullin's Jacket lead from 13 to 10 and keeping him fourth in the Trophy on 117.

Stuart Neumann's card split cleanly in two. From the 6th to the 11th he dropped 14 shots — triple-bogey 7 at the par-4 6th, triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 7th, then double bogeys at 8, 9, 10 and 11. He recovered with 12 points across 13–16 (bogey-par-bogey-par) and stayed fifth.

John Patterson started with 9 points across 1–3 (bogey-par-par). Then came the 10 at the par-5 8th. The five-hole stretch 11–15 — triple-bogey 7, quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 12th, triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 13th, quadruple-bogey 7 at the par-3 14th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 15th — bled 19 shots (3+5+3+4+4).

## Race Shifts

**Trophy:** Mullin's overnight five-point lead was wiped out and inverted; he sits third, 11 adrift of the tied leaders Alex Baker and Williams on 133. **Green Jacket:** Mullin still leads, but his cushion over Jon Baker has narrowed from 13 to 10, the 18th accounting for all of the movement and more. **Wooden Spoon:** Patterson's gap to Neumann has widened from 5 to 14.
