*TEG 10 — The Algarve, fourth visit*

## The 5th green at Boavista, Sunday

Gregg Williams has just rolled in a birdie at the par-4 5th. After fifty-nine holes of chasing, he has drawn level with Alex Baker for the Trophy. Three rounds, two personal bests, a 10 at the 13th absorbed without flinching — and here, with thirteen holes to play on home soil, the tournament is square.

What happens next is the highest Stableford round in TEG history.

Bogey at the 5th for three. Par at the par-3 6th for four. Par at the 7th for four. Bogey at the par-5 8th for three. Pars at the 9th and 10th for three and four. Twenty-one points across six holes (b04). Williams, who had spent four days hauling himself level, was gone by the turn.

Baker signed for **51 points at +17** (b02) — a new TEG benchmark — and lifted the Trophy on **184 points (b01)**, eleven clear of Williams. How a man eight adrift after eighteen holes finished eleven to the good is the story of TEG 10. So we rewind.

## Round 1 — Mullin Strikes First, and Hard {.round1 .round}

Boavista, the eleventh TEG round at Howard Swan's parkland with its Atlantic views, and David Mullin treated it like a driving range. **49 Stableford points at +4 gross (b03)** — the second-best round in TEG history at the time of writing, and a 14-shot Green Jacket lead before anyone had touched a beer.

Alex Baker's tidy 41 left him second, the patient pursuer eight points back and not minding it. Jon Baker pushed Mullin briefly on Gross — level at the 9th — before Mullin restored order at the 10th and was never caught again, in this round or any other.

Lower down, the Spoon had already chosen its man. John Patterson dropped to last at the 4th and added a **10 at the par-5 8th (b17)** for company. Stuart Neumann, meanwhile, found his vocation: a **10 at the par-4 18th (b11)**, the first deposit in what would become a sizeable account at that hole.

## Round 2 — Personal Bests All Round (Bar One) {.round2 .round}

Palmares made its TEG debut: Robert Trent Jones Jr.'s 27-hole spread, the Praia/Alvor combination — links one minute, parkland the next — and two of the chasing pack rose to it.

Gregg Williams went round in **48 points at +14, a personal best (b07)**. Alex Baker matched the mood with **45 at +23, also a personal best (b05)**. Between them they trimmed Mullin's Trophy lead from eight to five. Mullin himself simply got on with the Green Jacket, his cushion easing only fractionally to thirteen.

Patterson opened with a **10 at the par-4 1st (b08)** — five over before he'd warmed up — and the Spoon was sealed in cement. Neumann, finding the Palmares routing no kinder than Boavista's, posted a **10 at the par-5 13th (b23)** as part of a **three-hole bleed of 13 shots across 13, 14 and 15 — 10, 9, 7 against pars of 5, 4, 4 (b67)** — then, with the inevitability of a punchline, signed off with **another 10 at the 18th (b12)**. Different course, different hole number, same man.

## Round 3 — The Handover {.round3 .round}

Palmares again, this time the Alvor/Lagos all-parkland routing — another TEG debut combination. And the round in which apprenticeship ended.

Alex Baker's **47 points at +22 (b06)** — a second consecutive personal best — flipped a five-point deficit into an eleven-point lead. He took the Trophy outright at the 6th and looked set for a serene afternoon, until the **par-5 13th went down for a 10 (b15)**. Williams, briefly, was top of the pile.

It lasted barely an hour. Baker reclaimed a share by the 15th and led by eleven at the close, helped by the fact that the man he'd overtaken — Mullin — was no longer playing the same game. Patterson, on 27 points for the round, added a **10 at the par-5 8th (b18)** to his collection. That hole, it would emerge, owed him nothing.

## Round 4 — 51, and the Spoon Engraved {.round4 .round}

Back at Boavista. We've already told you what Baker did between the 5th and the 10th. What remains is the bookkeeping.

David Mullin signed for **+13 gross**, completing his Green Jacket runaway at **+51 (b10)** — twelve clear of Jon Baker, who had spent four rounds locked into silver from the moment Mullin retook the Gross lead at the Boavista 10th on day one. Wire to wire, no genuine threat, one untroubled coronation.

And John Patterson, whose round-by-round of 29-35-27-21 reads like a man descending a staircase, found the **par-5 8th once more — this time for 11 (b09)**, his fourth double-figure hole of the week and the heaviest of them. **112 Stableford points. The Wooden Spoon (b21).**

## The men, in brief

- **Alex Baker** — 41, 45, 47, 51. Second, second, first, first. Two personal bests in successive rounds, a 10 at the 13th shrugged off inside two holes, and the best Stableford round TEG has ever seen to close it out. The apprentice is the champion.
- **David Mullin** — 49 at Boavista set a tone he never had to revisit. Led the Trophy for two rounds, lost it cleanly to a superior player, and consoled himself by lapping the field for the Green Jacket by twelve. Wire to wire on Gross from the 10th of round one.
- **Gregg Williams** — A quiet opener, then a personal-best 48 at Palmares, a brief Trophy lead at the 13th in round three, and level pegging again at the 5th on Sunday. Then six holes happened to him. Runner-up, and the man who pushed Alex closest.
- **Jon Baker** — Briefly outright on Gross at the 9th of round one, and otherwise the model of consistency in second place on the Green Jacket. Silver, by twelve.
- **Stuart Neumann** — Patron saint of double figures: 10 at the Boavista 18th in R1, 10 at the Palmares 13th in R2, the 10-9-7 stretch through 13-15 at Palmares, and a closing 10 at the Palmares 18th to round off Saturday. Colour merchant in chief.
- **John Patterson** — Last from the 4th hole of the week to the final putt. 10 at the Boavista 8th, 10 at the Palmares 1st, 10 at the Palmares 8th, 11 at the Boavista 8th. The Spoon was decided on day one; the engraving simply took four rounds.
