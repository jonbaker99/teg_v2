# Palmares Pyrotechnics: Personal Bests, Sextuple Bogeys, and a Lead That Won't Budge {.round-report-title}

<p class="dateline">TEG 10 | Round 2 | 01/10/2017 | Palmares - Praia / Alvor</p>

<p class="round-scores"><span class="round-scores-header">Round Stableford:</span> GW 48 | AB 45 | JB 45 | DM 42 | SN 37 | JP 35</p>
<p class="round-scores"><span class="round-scores-header">Round Gross:</span> JB +10 | DM +11 | GW +14 | AB +23 | JP +25 | SN +33</p>

Palmares made its TEG debut and the scoreboard immediately developed a split personality. Williams and A. Baker filed career-best Stableford cards. Patterson opened with a 10. Neumann signed off with one. And through all of it — the fireworks at one end, the fires at the other — Mullin's lead barely flinched. A Robert Trent Jones Jr. layout stitching the parkland Alvor nine onto the seaside links of Praia was always going to spread the field; few expected it to expand it quite this wide.

**David Mullin** went round in 42 points and kept both leads, which is all the brief required. The spine was five consecutive pars from the 4th — par-5, par-3, par-4, par-4, par-3 — fifteen points banked without fuss. The only real wobble was a double-bogey 7 at the par-5 9th to break the run. He didn't win the day. He didn't have to.

**Gregg Williams** had the round of the day and a personal best to match: **48 points**, the finest Stableford return of his TEG career. The middle of the card did the work, an 18-point burst across holes 8 to 12 anchored by a birdie 3 at the par-4 10th and three pars either side. He closed in similar fashion, twelve points over the final four, and trimmed the Trophy gap from 8 to 5. A marker laid down for rounds three and four.

**Alex Baker** matched the personal-best theme with **45 points** of his own — a career high, built on a quick start (nine points through the first three) and ten more across the 5th, 6th and 7th, where a par at the par-4 7th was worth four. The Jacket bid, however, walked into a wall at holes 12, 13 and 14: double, double, double, **+6 gross in three holes**. A 6 at the par-4 12th, a 7 at the par-5 13th, a 6 at the par-4 14th. The Stableford ledger survived; the gross challenge did not.

**Jon Baker** quietly returned the same 45, and is now the closest man to Mullin in the Jacket. He opened with ten points in three holes — par, birdie at the par-5 2nd, par — then donated a double-bogey 7 at the par-5 4th before reverting to type with **six consecutive pars from the 5th through the 10th** for 18 points. Metronomic when required.

**Stuart Neumann** played one of the more remarkable cards of the TEG era, and not in the way he'd have hoped. Through holes 9 to 12 he was serene: 14 points, a four-point par at the par-5 9th, a bogey, a par, another four-point par at the par-4 12th. Then the wheels detached. A quintuple-bogey **10 at the par-5 13th**, a quintuple-bogey **9 at the par-4 14th**, and a triple-bogey 7 at the 15th — **13 shots over par in three holes**. For symmetry, he signed off with a sextuple-bogey **10 at the par-4 18th**.

**John Patterson** opened with a sextuple-bogey **10 at the 1st** and spent the rest of the afternoon trying, and failing, to outrun it. He remains anchored to the foot of the table. Remarkably, despite Neumann's 10-9-10 contribution to the cause, the Spoon gap *grew*.

The Trophy lead halved-ish — eight down to five — as Williams and A. Baker hauled themselves onto 86, three behind Mullin on 89; J. Baker on 82 keeps the pursuit four-handed. The Jacket gap moved a single shot, 14 to 13, with J. Baker still the only realistic pursuer and the rest of the field stretched out behind by A. Baker's mid-round bleed and Neumann's back-nine pyrotechnics. The Spoon, against all visible evidence, widened from three to five.
