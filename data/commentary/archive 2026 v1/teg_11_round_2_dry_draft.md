# Round 2 Draft — PGA Catalunya Tour

## Overview
Jon Baker posted the best round of the day with 41 Stableford points, extending his Trophy lead and pushing his Green Jacket advantage to 3. John Patterson had the worst of it, leaking eight shots across holes 3-5 and then a sextuple-bogey 11 at the par-5 15th. The shape of all three races shifted: Alex Baker climbed to outright second in the Trophy despite his own blow-up, Mullin slipped further back in the Trophy but stayed nearest in the Jacket, and Patterson's Wooden Spoon cushion grew from 3 to 5.

## The Round
The damage at PGA Catalunya's par-5 15th defined the day. Patterson signed for an 11 there (sextuple bogey, +6) and Gregg Williams for a 10 (quintuple bogey, +5) — both scored zero Stableford points on the hole.

Patterson's round was in trouble long before that. Across holes 3-5 he went double bogey, triple bogey, triple bogey — a 6 at the par-4 3rd, a 7 at the par-4 4th and an 8 at the par-5 5th, eight shots dropped in three holes. The 11 at the 15th then ended any hope of a recovery.

Alex Baker's card carried its own blow-up: a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 12th, +5 in one hole. He nonetheless finished the day in outright second in the Trophy, helped by larger collapses elsewhere.

Gregg Williams' round was wrecked by the 10 at the 15th. He played the closing stretch bogey-quintuple-double-birdie: a 4 at the par-3 14th, the 10 at the 15th, a 5 at the par-3 16th and then a birdie 3 at the par-4 17th to stop the bleeding.

David Mullin produced the round's best mid-round burst, 10 Stableford points across holes 8-10: par at the par-3 8th, par at the par-4 9th, birdie 4 at the par-5 10th. He couldn't sustain it and finished 4th in the Trophy, but remained the closest challenger in the Green Jacket.

Jon Baker's anchor stretch was holes 9-11 — par, par, par for 9 Stableford points: a 4 at the par-4 9th, a 5 at the par-5 10th, a 3 at the par-3 11th. He led both the Trophy and the Jacket from start to finish.

## Race Shifts
**Trophy:** Jon's lead over second narrowed from 3 to 2, but the chasing pack splintered. Alex Baker overtook both Gregg Williams and Mullin to become the nearest challenger; the gap from Jon to 4th (Mullin) widened from 6 to 11.

**Green Jacket:** Jon extended his gross lead over Mullin from 2 to 3. Alex and Gregg dropped sharply back after the 12th and 15th respectively. Patterson's 11 left him 41 behind on gross.

**Wooden Spoon:** Patterson's cushion at the bottom grew from 3 to 5. The race is, for now, a one-man procession.
