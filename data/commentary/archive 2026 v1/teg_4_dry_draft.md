# TEG 4 — Mullin's Forest (Draft)

## Overview
David Mullin won the Trophy at +9, by 8 from Jon Baker — the best Trophy score in TEG history. Mullin also took the Green Jacket at +81 (gross), 19 clear of Stuart Neumann. Gregg Williams collected the Wooden Spoon at +40. The tournament was TEG's first visit to East Sussex (and Surrey), with all four courses making their TEG debuts: Royal Ashdown Forest (heathland, no bunkers), Lingfield Park (parkland with water), Crowborough Beacon (MacKenzie heathland above the South Downs) and Bletchingley (modern parkland).

## Round 1 — A Forest with No Bunkers, and Meller Finds the Only Trouble
Royal Ashdown Forest, 29/08/2011. Baker led the Trophy by 3 from Mullin at day's end; Mullin led the Gross by 3 (he took the Gross lead outright at H11 of the opening round and never gave it back). Henry Meller ran up a 12 at the par-3 11th (+9). Gregg Williams played the closing nine in 22 over par: double bogey at the par-4 10th (+2), triple at the par-3 11th (+3), doubles at the par-4 12th (+2) and 13th (+2), triple at the par-4 14th (+3), doubles at the par-4 15th (+2) and par-3 16th (+2), and triples at the par-4 17th (+3) and 18th (+3).

## Round 2 — Mullin Takes the Trophy Lead at Lingfield
Lingfield Park, 30/08/2011. Mullin overhauled Baker on the Trophy and ended the round 2 clear; his Gross lead extended to 7.

## Round 3 — Mullin's +17 at Crowborough: A Round for the Record Books
Crowborough Beacon, 31/08/2011. Mullin posted +17 gross, the second-best Gross round in TEG history to that point. He took the Trophy lead outright at the par-5 10th with a par. Both leads stretched to 16.

## Round 4 — The Eagle, the Avalanche, and the Coronation
Bletchingley, 01/09/2011. Baker took the Trophy lead outright at the par-3 2nd with a birdie (2). Mullin drew level again at the 3rd. Baker then eagled the par-4 8th (a 2) to seize the Trophy lead.

From the 9th tee Baker dropped 27 shots in eight holes: double at the par-5 9th (+2), triple at the par-4 10th (+3), triple at the par-5 11th (+3), sextuple at the par-3 12th — a 9 (+6), double at the par-4 13th (+2), triple at the par-3 14th (+3), sextuple at the par-5 15th — an 11 (+6), and double at the par-4 16th (+2). Mullin retook the Trophy lead outright at the par-3 12th with a routine par.

At the foot of the table, Williams dropped to last at the par-5 6th after a quadruple bogey 9. Meller then took last at the par-5 11th with a quintuple bogey 10. Williams sank to the bottom for good at the par-4 13th with a triple bogey 7. Williams's closing six holes ran to 16 over: triple at 13 (+3), quadruple at the par-3 14th (+4), triple at 15 (+3), and doubles at 16 (+2), 17 (+2) and 18 (+2) — a personal-worst +15 round (and a personal-worst +43 gross). Meller's card also included a quintuple bogey 10 at the par-5 15th.

## How the Competitions Were Decided

**The Trophy (Net vs Par).** Baker led after Round 1 by 3. Mullin took the lead at Lingfield (R2) and led by 2; he stretched it to 16 at Crowborough after taking the lead outright at the R3 10th. At Bletchingley, Baker briefly took the lead outright at the 2nd, was drawn level by Mullin at the 3rd, then went ahead again with the eagle at the 8th. Mullin reclaimed the lead outright at the par-3 12th with a par as Baker made 9 at the same hole — the decisive moment. Final margin: 8 shots. Mullin's +9 is the best Trophy score in TEG history.

**The Green Jacket (Gross).** Wire-to-wire for Mullin. He took the lead outright at the 11th of Round 1 and was never headed. Round-end margins: 3, 7, 16, 19. The Crowborough +17 (second-best Gross round in TEG history to that point) was decisive. Final: Mullin +81, Neumann +100 — both PBs, alongside Gross PBs for Baker (+113), Meller (+107) and Williams (+152).

**The Wooden Spoon.** Williams was not at the bottom after any of Rounds 1–3 (Meller R1, Neumann R2, Meller R3). At Bletchingley he dropped to last at the par-5 6th (quad bogey 9), Meller took it back at the par-5 11th (quintuple 10), and Williams returned to the bottom for good at the par-4 13th (triple 7). He finished at +40.

## Players
- **David Mullin** — Trailed Baker by 3 after R1, then led every subsequent round on both metrics. Trophy at +9 (TEG record), Jacket at +81 by 19. Crowborough +17 was the centrepiece.
- **Jon Baker** — Led the Trophy after R1, runner-up throughout, briefly retook the lead at Bletchingley with an eagle at the 8th before dropping 27 shots in eight holes. Finished runner-up Trophy; Gross PB +113.
- **Stuart Neumann** — Runner-up for the Jacket at +100 (Gross PB). Never in Trophy contention, never near the Spoon.
- **Henry Meller** — Opened with a 12 at Ashdown's 11th, held last on R4 at the 11th, but Gross PB of +107 and Williams's late collapse spared him the Spoon.
- **Gregg Williams** — Three large bleeds (22 at Ashdown's closing nine, 18 over Crowborough's first six, 16 over Bletchingley's closing six). Took the Spoon at +40 but posted a Gross PB of +152.
