# Carnage on the Estoril Coast

At sundown on the Estoril Coast, the Quinta da Marinha scoreboard read like a casualty list. Six players, one Robert Trent Jones Sr. parkland none of them had ever seen before, and a body count to match: a 10, three 9s at consecutive holes from the same man, an 8 at a par-3, and a leaderboard that ran from Gregg Williams' 38 points all the way down to Stuart Neumann's 17 — a 21-point spread on day one. The elevated targets did their share of damage. The bunkering did the rest. TEG's second visit to the Lisbon Coast was, by any honest accounting, a hiding.

Rewind to the first tee, though, and the most striking thing was how briskly Alex Baker set about the new course. Bogey at the par-5 1st, bogey 4 at the par-3 2nd, bogey 6 at the par-5 3rd, bogey at the 4th, bogey at the par-3 5th: five holes, five dropped shots, 15 Stableford points in the bag. On a course this unfamiliar and this punishing, it was the round of the day through five — and, as the rest of the afternoon would prove, very nearly the round of the day full stop.

Behind him, Henry Meller was furnishing the round's first signature disaster. A 10 at the par-5 3rd — the kind of number that doesn't fit comfortably on the card — dumped him to the foot of the Wooden Spoon race before most of the field had reached the turn. A birdie 2 at the par-3 5th at least stopped the immediate bleeding, but the basement tenancy was already changing hands behind him: Neumann picked up a triple-bogey 7 at the 4th and quietly took the Spoon off Meller there. He would not let go of it.

The middle of the front nine then chewed up David Mullin. Three doubles in a row — a 7 at the par-5 6th, a 5 at the par-3 7th, a 6 at the par-4 8th — bled six shots out of his gross card in three holes, and Jon Baker, doing what Jon Baker tends to do, drew quietly level for the Green Jacket lead with a bogey at the 6th in the middle of it.

That brought everyone to the holes that decided the day. From the 8th tee Williams produced the round's decisive surge — bogey at the 8th, par at the 9th, birdie 4 at the par-5 10th, bogey at the 11th — 14 Stableford points in four holes. The birdie at the 10th took him outright into the Trophy lead, and at the 11th he drew level on gross too. For a few holes, Williams was leading both races.

The very same stretch was where Alex Baker's gleaming front nine came apart. A quadruple-bogey 8 at the 8th, a triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 9th, a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 10th: 11 shots dropped in three holes, the wheels not so much coming off as being unbolted, one at a time, in plain sight.

And the same stretch, lengthened, was where Neumann's afternoon disintegrated entirely. From the 7th to the 15th, in order: double-bogey 5, double-bogey 6, triple-bogey 6, quadruple-bogey 9, quadruple-bogey 9, quintuple-bogey 9, double-bogey 6, quintuple-bogey 8 at the par-3 14th, double-bogey 6. Twenty-nine shots dropped in nine holes — three 9s on the spin through 10-12 the gruesome centrepiece — and a Wooden Spoon lead that no longer needed defending. It is, comfortably, his worst TEG round to date.

The 13th then did for two more men. Jon Baker carved out a quintuple-bogey 9 of his own there; Mullin, in the same group's worth of mayhem, calmly made par and moved outright into the Green Jacket lead — the lead he would still hold at sundown. Meller, meanwhile, was bleeding six shots over 13-15 (double, double, double) to cap a 29-point round of his own.

Williams, having spent the front nine and early back nine looking like the only man who'd read the yardage book, then leaked it back. A double-bogey 6 at the 12th, a double-bogey 6 at the 13th, a double-bogey 5 at the par-3 14th and a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 15th: 10 shots in four holes, the Trophy lead trimmed but, crucially, not surrendered.

The closing stretch finished Alex Baker off properly. A double at the 15th, a triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 16th, a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 17th, a quadruple-bogey 8 at the 18th — 14 shots dropped over the last four holes, and a card split clean down the middle: 20 points out, 10 back. From the best front nine of the day to one of its more harrowing back nines, in the space of one round.

Jon Baker, to his credit, kept his head where others lost theirs, threading a birdie 3 through the 17th to stay within four of Williams in the Trophy and one of Mullin in the Jacket. Mullin himself, despite his 6-8 wobble, held the gross lead by that single shot. And Williams, even with the 12-15 bleed, signed for 38 — four clear, and the best round of the day on Stableford by a comfortable margin.

The Trophy lead was Williams' the moment his putt dropped at the 10th, and four points clear of Jon Baker is where he ended; the Jacket lead changed hands repeatedly across the back nine — Mullin drew level at the 10th, Williams drew level at the 11th — before Mullin's par at the 13th nudged him outright clear and he held on by one. At the wrong end, the Spoon passed from Meller to Neumann at the 4th and Neumann's nine-hole bleed from the 7th to the 15th opened a 12-point gulf that already looks structural.
