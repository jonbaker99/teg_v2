# Round 3 — El Prat (Azul), Barcelona

## Overview

Alex Baker took outright command of the Trophy at the 13th, ending the day two points clear of brother Jon. Jon Baker retained the Green Jacket lead by three over David Mullin, despite a seven-shot bleed at 11-13. David Mullin replaced John Patterson at the bottom of the Wooden Spoon table, trailing by five.

## The Round

El Prat's Azul routing was a new course for TEG — a Greg Norman parkland on TEG's second visit to Catalonia, with no local knowledge in the field.

Alex Baker opened with a bogey 5 at the par-4 1st. That was enough to draw level with Jon for the Trophy lead, which Jon had carried two clear into Barcelona. Two holes later, David Mullin parred the par-4 3rd to draw level with Jon at the top of the Green Jacket race.

The opening stretch then bit. Gregg Williams ran up an 8 at the par-4 5th — a quadruple bogey that took the edge off his round. At the par-3 6th, David Mullin made a 7 (quadruple bogey). Jon Baker's own bogey 4 at the same hole was enough to restore his outright Jacket lead as Mullin's card came apart.

From the 7th, Jon Baker assembled a three-hole burst worth 10 Stableford points: par at the 7th (3 pts), par at the 8th (3 pts), and a birdie 4 at the par-5 9th (4 pts). John Patterson followed close behind with his own nine-point stretch across 8-10 — bogey, bogey, bogey at a par-4, par-5, par-5, three Stableford points apiece.

Jon's lead did not survive the turn. At the par-4 11th he made a double-bogey 6 (+2); at the par-3 12th, a double-bogey 5 (+2); at the par-4 13th, a triple-bogey 7 (+3). Seven shots dropped across three holes. Alex Baker, playing the same stretch, parred the par-3 12th, the par-4 13th and the par-4 14th — nine Stableford points — and the par at the 13th carried him into the outright Trophy lead.

Patterson's recovery was undone late: a 9 at the par-4 16th, a quintuple bogey. Mullin's bogey 4 at the par-3 12th had already dropped him beneath Patterson in the Spoon standings.

## Race Shifts

The Trophy lead changed hands. Jon Baker began the round two clear; Alex drew level at the 1st, fell behind during Jon's 7-9 burst, and moved into outright command at the 13th as Jon bled through 11-13. The two-shot gap is preserved, reversed in direction.

The Green Jacket lead held with Jon Baker, the three-shot margin over Mullin unchanged from overnight despite Mullin's level-pegging cameo at the 3rd.

The Wooden Spoon changed occupant. Patterson held it overnight; Mullin's quadruple at the 6th and bogey at the 12th completed the swap, and he now trails the field by five — the same gap Patterson carried 24 hours earlier.
