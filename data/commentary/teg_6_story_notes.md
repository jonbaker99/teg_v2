# TEG 6 Story Notes

## Key Points
- Gregg WILLIAMS's 1st Trophy - wire-to-wire dominance, led all 72 holes and never trailed despite multiple lead losses during rounds
- David MULLIN's 5th Green Jacket - led 62/72 holes gross, prevailed by 4 strokes despite late-tournament Stableford collapse
- WILLIAMS won by narrowest margin: 3 points over J. BAKER after surrendering leads twice in final round
- Historic volatility: WILLIAMS posted tournament's best (48pts R1) and worst (28pts R4) rounds
- MELLER's 2nd consecutive Wooden Spoon despite winning Round 2 outright (41pts)

## How It Unfolded
- **Round 1:** GW 48pts leads Trophy by 9; DM +17 leads Jacket by 1
- **Round 2:** GW maintains 9pt Trophy lead despite 36pt round; HM's 41pts & DM's 37pts tie for Jacket lead
- **Round 3:** GW extends Trophy lead to 7pts; JB's final-hole par steals Jacket lead from DM's blow-up finish
- **Round 4:** GW survives 28pt round to win Trophy by 3; DM's best gross (22) reclaims Jacket by 4 strokes

## Story Angles
- Two different champions: WILLIAMS (Trophy), MULLIN (Jacket) - first split title outcome at TEG
- WILLIAMS's rollercoaster: 48-36-33-28 round progression, 21 triple-bogeys-or-worse yet wire-to-wire Trophy dominance

- MULLIN's redemption arc: collapsed from Jacket lead in R3, stormed back with R4's best gross to reclaim title
- H7 and H8 disaster corridor: multiple blow-ups across all four rounds on these holes
