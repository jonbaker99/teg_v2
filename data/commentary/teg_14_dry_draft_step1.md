# The King of Kent: Mullin's Coronation by the Sea

## OVERVIEW

David Mullin won the Trophy on 154 Stableford points, two clear of Alex Baker. He also took the Green Jacket at +68 gross, seven shots ahead of runner-up Jon Baker. Jon Baker collected the Wooden Spoon on 125 points, last in every round.

## Deal Me In: Mullin Seizes Cinque Ports

Round 1 was played at Royal Cinque Ports, Deal.

Mullin drew level for the gross lead with a par at the 9th, and from there pulled clear in both competitions. A four-hole burst from 9 to 12 — par, birdie at the par-4 10th, par, par — brought him 13 points and the Stableford lead, drawing level at the par-4 11th and going outright in front with a par at the par-5 16th. He finished on 40 points for the round, three clear on Stableford and five clear on gross.

Alex Baker had the day's two pratfalls: a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 11th and a quintuple-bogey 10 at the par-5 16th. Gregg Williams ran up a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 3rd. Jon Baker dropped to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon table with a bogey at the par-3 4th and stayed there.

## Littlestone, Big Lead

Round 2 was at Littlestone on the Romney Marshes.

Mullin posted 41 points and stretched his Stableford lead from three to nine, with the gross gap out to twelve. Twice he checked his own slide with a birdie: after a triple-bogey 7 at the par-4 4th, a double-bogey 7 at the par-5 5th and a bogey at the par-3 6th, he birdied the par-5 7th; and after bogeys at the 15th and 16th and a double at the par-3 17th, he birdied the par-5 18th to close.

Alex Baker conceded 12 shots in four holes from 2 to 5 — triple bogey, double, triple, and a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 5th — and added another quadruple, an 8 at the par-4 8th, later in the round.

## Prince's of the Realm

Round 3 was the first day at Prince's on the Shore/Dunes routing.

Mullin signed for a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 8th but answered with a four-hole closing flourish from 15 to 18 — par, par, par at the par-3 17th, birdie at the par-4 18th — worth 13 points. His 36-point round kept the Stableford lead at eight and the gross lead at twelve.

Alex Baker leaked six shots in the opening three holes (double, double, double from 1 to 3) and added a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 10th. Gregg Williams strung three pars together from 9 to 11 before a triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 12th. Jon Baker was steady through 14, 15 and 16 before a triple-bogey 6 at the par-3 17th, and ran up a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 13th.

## The 16th Bites Back

Round 4 was the second day at Prince's.

Mullin began with the Trophy lead at eight and the gross lead at twelve. He carded a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 10th, cracking the door open. Alex Baker — three opening pars worth 10 points from holes 1-3 — then ran a steady stretch that ended with a double-bogey 6 at the par-4 4th, and later strung 11 points across holes 13-15 (par, par, par) to threaten the lead. He shut the door himself at the par-4 16th, where he ran up a sextuple-bogey 10, his second 10 at a 16th in the tournament.

Jon Baker produced the day's late bright spot, taking 14 points across holes 12-15 with birdies at the par-5 12th and par-4 13th and pars at 14 and 15, but a double-bogey 6 at the 16th and a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 18th left him on 39 for the round.

Mullin's 37-point round was enough. He won the Trophy by two and the Green Jacket by seven.

## HOW THE COMPETITIONS WERE DECIDED

**Trophy (Stableford).** Mullin led from R1 onwards. He drew level at the par-4 11th and went outright in front with a par at the par-5 16th at Royal Cinque Ports — the decisive takeover. He led by 3 after R1, 9 after R2, 8 after R3 and 2 after R4. The decisive survival was steadying after the R4 quad at the 10th, with Alex Baker's 10 at the 16th closing off the chase. Final margin: 154 to 152.

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Alex Baker briefly drew level at the par-4 6th in R1 before Mullin drew level again at the par-4 9th and pulled clear. Gross leads of 5, 12, 12 and 7 followed. Mullin finished on +68, seven clear of Jon Baker.

**Wooden Spoon.** Jon Baker dropped to the bottom with a bogey at the par-3 4th in R1 and stayed there for the remaining 68 holes — last in all four rounds with 29, 29, 28 and 39. A four-hole 14-point burst in R4 (12-15) was not enough to undo the earlier rounds, and a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 18th confirmed the Spoon. Final score: 125.

## PLAYERS

- **David Mullin** — Wire-to-wire dual champion: led both competitions from R1, stretched the Stableford gap to nine after Littlestone, survived quadruple bogeys at the 8th in R3 and the 10th in R4 to take the Trophy by 2 and the Jacket by 7.
- **Alex Baker** — Runner-up in both. Strung together pars and a final-day charge that nearly caught Mullin, but undone by repeated big numbers, including a 10 at the 16th in both R1 and R4.
- **Jon Baker** — Anchored to last from R1 hole 4 onwards; a late R4 birdie burst from 12 to 15 and a 39-point round hinted at what might have been before an 8 at the par-4 18th confirmed the Spoon.
- **Gregg Williams** — Quiet mid-pack week, punctuated by a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 3rd in R1 and a triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 12th in R3 after a steady stretch.
