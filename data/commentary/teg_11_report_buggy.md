# The Baker Boys Take Catalonia

For three TEGs, Jon Baker had been the man at the church door clutching the bouquet. Three Green Jackets, three runner-up finishes, three quiet trains home. In Catalonia, across four rounds bookended by the PGA Catalunya Stadium course and detoured through a Tour layout and a debut at Greg Norman's El Prat Azul, that pattern finally broke — and broke in the most uncomfortable way available, by way of his own brother.

This was not a tournament won by daylight. The Trophy lead changed hands within the family on Sunday morning and switched back again on Sunday afternoon. The Green Jacket was levelled twice by David Mullin — a man arriving with eight of them and back-to-back form — and surrendered by him twice in the same week. By the end Jon Baker had both pots, sitting on 143 Stableford points and +70 gross, while Mullin, the man to beat, was holding the Wooden Spoon on 125. Catalonia, in short, took the established order and rearranged the furniture.

## Round 1 — Jon Baker Sets The Tone At The Stadium

The Stadium course — Gallardo and Coles, Spain's premier examination paper — opened proceedings with the kind of clean ledger that flatters whoever sets it. Jon Baker set it: 35 points, a three-point Trophy lead, two clear on the Gross. Not a procession, but a marker, laid down on a course that does not usually reward such declarations.

Around him, the Spoon race began to organise itself with grim efficiency. Gregg Williams went round in 8 at the par-3 5th — a quintuple bogey on a hole where, to put it generously, par was on the menu. John Patterson then went one better, or one worse, with a quintuple-bogey 9 at the 4th, and confirmed his position with a triple-bogey 7 at the 14th that dropped him to the bottom of the table. Where, as it turned out, he would sit for some time.

## Round 2 — An Eleven At The Fifteenth

The Tour course took the baton on Day 2, and that layout's par-5 15th took most of the headlines. In a single round, on a single hole, Patterson made 11 and Williams made 10. A sextuple bogey and a quintuple bogey, one after the other, from the same tee box. Williams responded to his 10 in the way only he can: a double at the 16th, then a birdie at the par-4 17th, which is one of the harder holes on the course. The man contains multitudes, most of them double-figured.

Above this, almost separately, Jon Baker went round in 41. The card included three straight pars at the 9th, 10th and 11th — nine Stableford points without fuss — and the Trophy lead, while trimmed to two, stayed where it was. The Gross lead held at three. The shape of the week, you would have said at this point, looked settled.

## Round 3 — The Brother Coup At El Prat

It was not settled.

El Prat Azul, a Norman design outside Barcelona and a new course for TEG, is where the story turned. Alex Baker, defending Trophy champion and quiet presence to this point, opened with a bogey 5 at the 1st that drew him level with his brother on Stableford. By the 13th he was outright leader, having strung together pars at the 12th, 13th and 14th — nine points across three holes, including a par at the par-3 12th, the easiest hole on the course. Catalonia had its family handover.

At the top of the Gross board, Mullin began his own approach. A par at the par-4 3rd drew him level with Jon. Three holes later, at the par-3 6th, Jon made a bogey 4 to reclaim the outright lead, and Mullin — at that same location — made a quadruple-bogey 7. This is the decisive moment of the Green Jacket. Stated like that it looks small, a single hole on a Spanish afternoon. It was, in fact, the hinge.

For Mullin the day was not done. At the par-3 12th — that same SI 18, the easiest hole on the course — he made a bogey 4 and dropped to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon table. The eight-time Jacket holder, undone not by a blow-up but by a routine over-par on the hole least likely to produce one. Patterson, who had occupied last place from the 14th hole of Round 1, was overtaken without resistance. He would not regain it.

At round's end Alex led the Trophy by two; Jon led the Jacket by three; Mullin held the Spoon. Three competitions, three storylines, all in motion at once.

## Round 4 — Jon Baker, At The Fourth Time Of Asking

Back at the Stadium, the same examination paper as Round 1, different candidates. Mullin opened by drawing level on Gross for a second time, a par 5 at the par-5 3rd. Jon Baker, twice levelled in three rounds by a man with eight Green Jackets, declined to break.

The Trophy required slightly more drama. Jon drew level with Alex at the par-4 6th with a double-bogey 6 — a curiously workmanlike levelling, achieved by Jon being only mildly bad while Alex was rather more so. From there his brother's round simply came apart in three discrete instalments. Holes 4–6 cost him seven shots (double, double, triple). Holes 12–14 cost him another eight (triple at the par-5 12th, triple at the 13th, double at the 14th). Holes 16–18 cost him eight more (double, a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 17th, double at the 18th). A round of 27 points — his worst in TEG — assembled from three separate cold fronts moving through one card. Jon was never headed across the closing 13 holes.

Mullin, meanwhile, made his own contribution to the Jacket settlement: seven shots dropped over holes 15–17, a triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 15th — the easiest hole on the course — followed by doubles at the 16th and 17th. The man could not get out of his own way on the easy ones. Jon Baker's 35, which included a quadruple-bogey 8 of his own at the 13th, was nonetheless enough. Comfortably so. Gregg Williams, for completeness and consistency, dropped 11 shots across holes 8–11, his contribution including a quintuple-bogey 9 at the par-4 10th — a third quintuple in three different rounds, on three different par numbers, and still not last.

Two pots, one weekend, one Baker. The bridesmaid finally got married, and at the reception was his brother.

## How the competitions were decided

**The Trophy.** Jon led from the front: 35, then 41, a two-point cushion into Sunday morning. Alex's level-pegging at El Prat's 1st became an outright lead at the 13th via that three-par burst at 12–14. The reversal came at the Stadium's 6th on the final day, where Jon's double-bogey was, in the circumstances, the more solid score on offer. Across the next 13 holes Alex bled through three separate stretches; Jon did not. Final margin six points, 143 to 137. But for those three Sunday collapses — and you can pick any one of them — this was a Trophy that would have stayed in the family the other way.

**The Green Jacket.** Jon led on Gross at every round-end, by 2, 3, 3 and 3. The narrowness flatters the contest only slightly: Mullin drew level twice, once at El Prat's 3rd and once at the Stadium's 3rd, and on both occasions Jon reclaimed outright. The decisive takeover came at El Prat's par-3 6th, where Jon made bogey and Mullin made 7. Final margin three shots, +70 to +73 — Jon Baker's first Green Jacket, after three consecutive runner-up finishes. The bridesmaid pattern, painstakingly assembled over three previous tournaments, was dismantled in a single hole on a course nobody had played before.

**The Wooden Spoon.** Patterson held the bottom from the 14th hole of Round 1, through his sextuple at the Tour 15th, all the way to El Prat. The handover came at El Prat's 12th — a bogey on SI 18 by David Mullin, an act of housekeeping so minor that to call it a collapse would overdress it. Mullin held the position for the remainder of the week. Final total 125 points. The eight-time Jacket champion, runner-up for a ninth on Gross, last on Stableford. Catalonia is a venue that, evidently, takes things from people.

## The men, in brief

- **Jon Baker.** The bridesmaid no more. 35, 41, 32, 35 — never flashy, never absent. Twice levelled by Mullin on Gross and once overhauled by his brother on Stableford, and answered each time. First Green Jacket, by three; Trophy, by six. A tournament won by the simple expedient of refusing to have a bad round.

- **Alex Baker.** Defending Trophy champion who, on Sunday morning, was leading outright and looked entirely capable of keeping it that way. The 27 on Sunday afternoon — a personal worst — was not one collapse but three, neatly distributed across holes 4–6, 12–14 and 16–18. Runner-up by six to his own brother, which is a particular kind of weekend.

- **David Mullin.** Eight Green Jackets, back-to-back form, levelled the Gross lead twice and could not hold it either time. Runner-up on Gross at +73; Spoon on Stableford at 125. The decisive moment, in a career littered with them, was a bogey at the easiest hole on a course he had never seen before.

- **John Patterson.** Reigning Spoon holder, bottom from the 14th hole of Round 1, contributor of the week's signature horror in an 11 at the Tour 15th. Overtaken at the bottom only by an eight-time Jacket champion, which is — in its way — a result.

- **Gregg Williams.** Three quintuples on three different par numbers in three different rounds: an 8 at the Stadium 5th, a 10 at the Tour 15th, a 9 at the Stadium 10th. Also a birdie at the Tour 17th, one of the hardest holes on the course, sandwiched between catastrophes. Did not finish last. Genuinely difficult to explain.

- **Stuart Neumann.** Spoon in two of the previous three, and on a week of free-flowing crooked numbers contrived to be in none of them. Survived by remaining largely off-camera.

- **Henry Meller.** Three-time Spooner, fourth time absent from the discussion in either direction. A tournament one suspects he will remember chiefly for not being in it.
