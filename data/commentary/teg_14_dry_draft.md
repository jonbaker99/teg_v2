# Mullin's Kent Coronation — Dry Draft

## Overview

David Mullin won the Trophy on 154 Stableford points, two clear of Alex Baker. He also took the Green Jacket at +68 gross, seven shots ahead of runner-up Jon Baker. Jon Baker collected the Wooden Spoon on 125 points.

## Round 1 — Mullin Plants the Flag at Deal

Royal Cinque Ports, 4 November 2021. Mullin opened with 40 Stableford points (15 over gross) to lead the Trophy by 3 after R1 and the Jacket by 5.

The Trophy lead changed hands twice, both times to Mullin: he drew level at the par-4 11th with a par, then took outright control at the par-5 16th, also with a par. The Jacket lead had Alex Baker draw level at the par-4 6th with a par, before Mullin drew level again at the par-4 9th. From there Mullin's scoring did the rest: a 13-point burst across holes 9–12 (par, birdie at the par-4 10th, par, par) put daylight between him and the field. He couldn't quite sustain it — that run ended with a double bogey at the par-4 13th — but the damage was done.

Alex Baker burned six shots across holes 3–5 (double bogey at the par-5 3rd, double at the par-3 4th, double at the par-5 5th), added a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 11th, and finished the round with a 10 at the par-5 16th. Gregg Williams ran up a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 3rd. Jon Baker bogeyed the par-3 4th and dropped to the bottom of the Wooden Spoon table, where he would remain for the rest of the tournament.

## Round 2 — Littlestone Becomes Mullin Country

Littlestone, on the Romney Marshes. Mullin posted 41 Stableford points (14 over gross). The Trophy lead stretched to 9; the Jacket lead ballooned to 12.

Mullin's round had two visible wobbles, both arrested by birdies. A triple bogey at the par-4 4th, double at the par-5 5th and bogey at the par-3 6th ended with a birdie at the par-5 7th. Then a bogey-bogey-double run at 15, 16 and the par-3 17th was closed out by a birdie at the par-5 18th.

Alex Baker bled 12 shots across holes 2–5 (triple at the par-4 2nd, double at the par-4 3rd, triple at the par-4 4th, quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 5th), and added another quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 8th.

## Round 3 — Prince's, Princely Lead

Prince's Shore/Dunes, the 2nd TEG round at the venue. Mullin scored 36 points (20 over gross). The Trophy lead settled at 8.

Mullin survived a quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 8th. He was far stronger on the back nine — a 10-point split favouring the inward half — and closed with a 13-point flourish on holes 15–18 (par at the par-5 15th, par at the par-4 16th, par at the par-3 17th, birdie at the par-4 18th).

Alex Baker bled 6 shots over holes 1–3 (doubles at the par-4 1st, par-5 2nd and par-3 3rd) and then ran up a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 10th. Jon Baker scored 28 and posted a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 13th. Gregg Williams had a tidy 9-point run at holes 9–11 (three pars) before a triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 12th ended it.

## Round 4 — A Ten at the Sixteenth

Prince's Shore/Dunes again, the 3rd TEG round at the venue. Mullin scored 37 points (19 over gross) and finished the Trophy 2 clear and the Jacket 7 clear.

This was the only round with genuine tension. Mullin ran up a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 10th. Alex Baker had opened with 10 points across holes 1–3 (par at the par-4 1st, par at the par-5 2nd, par at the par-3 3rd), with that steady start ending in a double bogey at the par-4 4th. He then took 11 points off holes 13–15 (par, par, par at the par-5 15th) to cut the Trophy lead to something genuinely tight.

At the par-4 16th, Alex Baker made 10 — a sextuple bogey — ending his steady run and the contest with it. It was his second 10 of the week at a 16th hole. Jon Baker compiled 14 points across holes 12–15 (birdie at the par-5 12th, birdie at the par-4 13th, par at the par-4 14th, par at the par-5 15th); his own steady run ended with a double bogey at the par-4 16th, and he finished with a quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 18th. He still posted 39 for the round, his best of the week.

## How the Competitions Were Decided

**Trophy (Stableford).** Mullin led at every checkpoint — by 3 after R1, 9 after R2, 8 after R3, 2 at the finish. He drew level at R1 H11 and took outright control at R1 H16; that was the decisive takeover and the lead never changed hands again. Rounds of 40, 41, 36 and 37 built the cushion; his own quadruple at the par-4 10th in R4, combined with Alex Baker's hot stretches at the start of R4 and at 13–15, cut it to a couple of points, but Baker's 10 at the par-4 16th in R4 settled it. Final margin: 2.

**Green Jacket (Gross).** Mullin led on gross from R1 onwards — by 5, 12, 12 and 7. Alex Baker briefly drew level at R1 H6; Mullin drew level again at R1 H9 and was never caught. Gross rounds of 15, 14, 20 and 19 produced a +68 total. Jon Baker was runner-up at +75.

**Wooden Spoon.** Jon Baker dropped to the bottom at R1 H4 (a bogey at the par-3) and stayed there for the remaining 68 holes. Stableford rounds of 29, 29 and 28 confirmed it; a 39 in R4, including the 14-point burst at 12–15 and the quadruple at the par-4 18th, did not change the order. He finished on 125 points.

## Players

- **David Mullin.** Wire-to-wire winner of both the Trophy (154 pts, by 2) and the Green Jacket (+68, by 7). Led both competitions from R1 H16 (Trophy) and R1 H9 (Jacket, level) onward. Weathered quadruple bogeys at the par-5 8th (R3) and the par-4 10th (R4).
- **Alex Baker.** Runner-up in the Trophy. Two 10s in the tournament, both at 16th holes (R1 par-5, R4 par-4). Quadruples at the par-4 11th (R1), the par-5 5th and par-4 8th (R2), and the par-4 10th (R3). Cold stretches at the start of R1, R2 and R3; hot stretches at the start of R4 and at 13–15 in R4.
- **Jon Baker.** Wooden Spoon on 125 points, but runner-up for the Green Jacket at +75. Bottom of the Spoon race from R1 H4 to the finish. Quadruple-bogey 8 at the par-4 13th (R3) and at the par-4 18th (R4). Best round his R4 39, including a 14-point burst at holes 12–15.
- **Gregg Williams.** Mid-pack throughout. Quadruple-bogey 9 at the par-5 3rd (R1); a 9-point run at holes 9–11 in R3 was immediately followed by a triple-bogey 8 at the par-5 12th.
